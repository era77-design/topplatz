To delete a Facebook page, navigate to your page's Settings, then locate "Access and Control" under the "New Pages Experience" or "General Page Settings." From there, you can choose to either temporarily deactivate or permanently delete your page. Be aware that permanent deletion typically has a 14-day grace period before final removal.

## What You Need

- Access to the Facebook account that has admin rights for the page
- A computer or mobile device with internet access

## Steps

### Step 1: Switch to Your Page Profile

If you're using the "New Pages Experience," click your profile picture in the top right, then click "See all profiles" and select the page you want to delete. If you're on the classic page experience, just ensure you're logged in as an admin of the page you wish to delete.

### Step 2: Access Page Settings

Once you are managing your page, locate and click on "Settings" in the left-hand menu. For the "New Pages Experience," you might first need to click on "Professional dashboard" to reveal the navigation options, then proceed to "Page Access" or directly to "Settings."

### Step 3: Find "Access and Control"

Within the Settings menu, look for "Privacy" and then "Facebook Page Information" if you're on a classic page. For the "New Pages Experience," you'll typically find "Access and Control" directly. This is the section where options for page deactivation or deletion are located.

### Step 4: Select Deactivation and Deletion

Click on "Deactivation and Deletion." You will usually be presented with two distinct options: "Deactivate Page," which is a temporary measure, or "Delete Page," which is a permanent action. Choose "Delete Page" to proceed with the permanent removal process.

### Step 5: Confirm Your Choice

Facebook will prompt you to confirm your decision to delete the page permanently and may require you to enter your password for security verification. Carefully review the information provided about what will happen to your page's content and messages upon deletion.

### Step 6: Review and Delete Page

Before finalizing, Facebook often offers options to download your page's information or transfer content to another location. Take advantage of these if you need to preserve any data. Once you've reviewed these choices, click "Continue" and then "Delete Page" to initiate the deletion process.

### Step 7: Understand the Grace Period

After you initiate the deletion, your page will be scheduled for permanent removal, typically within 14 days. During this grace period, your page will not be visible to the public, but you retain the option to cancel the deletion by logging back into the page within those 14 days.


## Tips & Tricks

- Before deleting, download any important data, photos, or videos from your page to ensure you don't lose valuable content permanently.
- Inform your page followers in advance if you plan to delete your page, especially if you're migrating to a new platform or social media presence.
- Ensure you have full admin access to the Facebook page; only an admin can initiate and complete the page deletion process.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Once a Facebook page is permanently deleted after the 14-day grace period, all its content, posts, photos, videos, and followers will be irretrievably lost and cannot be recovered.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I delete a Facebook page if I'm using the new Pages experience?**

First, switch to your page profile. Then, go to your Professional dashboard, click "Page Access," and find "Access and Control." From there, you can select "Deactivation and Deletion" to proceed with deleting your page.

**How to delete facebook page permanently?**

To permanently delete your Facebook page, navigate to your page's settings, then "Access and Control," and select "Deactivation and Deletion." Choose the "Delete Page" option, confirm your decision, and understand that it will be permanently removed after a 14-day grace period.

**What is the difference between deactivating and deleting a Facebook page?**

Deactivating your page makes it invisible to others, but you can reactivate it at any time, restoring all its content. Deleting your page is a permanent action that removes all your content and followers after a 14-day grace period, and it cannot be recovered afterward.

**Can I recover a Facebook page after I've deleted it?**

Yes, you can recover a deleted Facebook page, but only within the 14-day grace period after you initiated the deletion. To do so, log back into the page within that timeframe and cancel the deletion request. After 14 days, the deletion is permanent and irreversible.

**What happens to my page's content and messages when I delete it?**

When you delete your Facebook page, all its content, including posts, photos, videos, and messages, will be permanently removed from Facebook's servers after the 14-day grace period. It's highly recommended to download any important data beforehand.

**What if I don't have admin access to the Facebook page I want to delete?**

If you don't have admin access, you cannot delete the page yourself. You would need to contact an existing admin of the page and request them to delete it, or to grant you admin access to perform the action.

**Can I delete a Facebook page from my mobile phone?**

Yes, the process for deleting a Facebook page is largely similar on a mobile device through the Facebook app or mobile browser. Navigate to your page settings, find "Access and Control," and follow the prompts for deactivation or permanent deletion.
