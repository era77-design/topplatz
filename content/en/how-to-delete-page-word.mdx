To delete a page in Word, you can select all content on the page and press Delete or Backspace. For stubborn blank pages, enable paragraph marks to reveal hidden formatting like page breaks or extra paragraph returns, then delete them. You can also use the "Go To" feature to quickly navigate to and select an entire page for deletion.

## What You Need

- Computer with Microsoft Word installed

## Steps

### Step 1: Show Hidden Formatting (Paragraph Marks)

Before attempting to delete a stubborn blank page, it's often helpful to reveal all hidden formatting. Go to the 'Home' tab in Word and click the Paragraph icon (¶), which looks like a backwards P. This will display paragraph marks, spaces, tab characters, and page breaks, making it easier to see what's causing an extra page. You might find several paragraph marks or a page break pushing content to the next page.

> 💡 **Tip:** Toggle paragraph marks off again by clicking the icon once you're done editing.

### Step 2: Delete a Blank Page

Once hidden formatting is visible, locate the blank page you wish to remove. Often, it's caused by excess paragraph marks (¶) or a manual page break. Select these hidden characters on the blank page using your mouse, then press the 'Delete' or 'Backspace' key on your keyboard. If there's a 'Page Break' visible, select it and delete it. This should collapse the extra space and remove the blank page.

> 💡 **Tip:** If the blank page is at the end, simply place your cursor at the very end of your document and press Backspace repeatedly until it's gone.

### Step 3: Delete a Page with Content

To delete a page that contains text, images, or other content, you need to select everything on that specific page. Position your cursor at the beginning of the content on the page you want to delete. Hold down the 'Shift' key and click at the very end of the content on that same page. Once all content on the page is highlighted, press the 'Delete' or 'Backspace' key.

> 💡 **Tip:** For faster selection, use the 'Go To' feature mentioned in the next step to select the entire page.

### Step 4: Use "Go To" to Select and Delete a Specific Page

Word's 'Go To' feature allows you to quickly select an entire page for deletion. Press 'Ctrl + G' (or 'Cmd + G' on Mac) to open the 'Find and Replace' dialog box with the 'Go To' tab active. In the 'Go to what:' list, select 'Page'. In the 'Enter page number:' field, type '\page'. Click 'Go To', then 'Close'. This action selects all content on your current page. With the page content selected, simply press 'Delete' or 'Backspace'.

> 💡 **Tip:** You can also type a specific page number into the 'Enter page number:' field, click 'Go To', and then manually select the content on that page for deletion.

### Step 5: Address Section Breaks

Sometimes, an extra page is caused by a section break, especially if it's a 'Next Page' or 'Odd/Even Page' section break. With paragraph marks visible (Step 1), locate any 'Section Break (Next Page)', 'Section Break (Even Page)', or 'Section Break (Odd Page)' that might be creating an unwanted page. Select the section break itself and press 'Delete' or 'Backspace'. Be aware that deleting a section break can affect formatting of the surrounding sections, as it removes the formatting division.

> 💡 **Tip:** If you need to maintain different formatting, consider changing the section break type (e.g., to 'Continuous') instead of deleting it entirely.

### Step 6: Delete Multiple Pages (Advanced)

To delete a range of pages, you can use the 'Go To' feature in conjunction with manual selection. First, navigate to the beginning of the first page you want to delete using 'Ctrl + G' and entering its page number. Then, scroll to the end of the last page you want to delete. While holding down the 'Shift' key, click at the very end of the content on that last page. This will select all content across the chosen page range. Finally, press 'Delete' or 'Backspace' to remove the selected pages.

> 💡 **Tip:** Always save a backup of your document before deleting multiple pages, especially in large or complex documents.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always save your document before making significant deletions, especially if you're unsure of the outcome.
- Use 'Ctrl + Z' (Undo) immediately if you accidentally delete content you didn't intend to remove.
- Remember that simply deleting visible text might not remove the page if there are hidden characters or section breaks still present.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Deleting section breaks can alter the formatting (headers, footers, page numbering, columns) of the surrounding sections. Review your document carefully after deleting section breaks to ensure no unintended changes occurred.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why can't I delete a blank page at the end of my document?**

This is often due to hidden paragraph marks (¶) or a manual page break. Enable paragraph marks from the Home tab to see these hidden elements and delete them to remove the extra blank page.

**How do I delete only the content on a page, but keep the page itself?**

To delete only the content, select all text, images, and objects on that specific page and press Delete. The physical page will remain, but it will be blank, ready for new content.

**What if deleting a section break messes up my formatting?**

If deleting a section break causes unwanted formatting changes, immediately use Undo (Ctrl+Z) to revert. You might need to re-insert a continuous section break or manually adjust formatting in the affected areas.

**Can I delete a specific page number, like page 5?**

Yes, use 'Ctrl + G' to open the 'Go To' dialog, select 'Page', enter '5', click 'Go To', then 'Close'. This moves your cursor to page 5. You then need to manually select all content on page 5 and delete it.

**How do I delete a page that has a large table or image?**

The process is the same as deleting any page with content. Select the table or image (and any surrounding text/paragraph marks) on that page and press Delete. If the table spans multiple pages, you'll need to select all parts of it that you wish to remove.

**Is there a quick way to delete the last page of a document?**

Often, the quickest way to delete the last page is to place your cursor at the very end of the document and repeatedly press the 'Backspace' key until the page disappears. This typically removes any excess paragraph marks or hidden characters causing the extra page.
