To clean suede shoes, first brush off loose dirt with a suede brush, then use a suede eraser for scuffs and minor stains. For tougher marks, apply a specialized suede cleaner or a diluted white vinegar solution sparingly, dabbing with a clean cloth. Always allow the shoes to air dry completely.

## What You Need

- Suede brush
- Suede eraser
- Clean cloths (microfiber or cotton)
- Suede cleaner spray (optional)
- Mild white vinegar (optional)
- Distilled water (optional)
- Shoe trees or crumpled unprinted paper
- Suede protector spray (optional)

## Steps

### Step 1: Gently Brush Off Loose Dirt

Begin by ensuring your suede shoes are completely dry. Use a specialized suede brush, brushing gently in one direction to remove any loose dirt, dust, or mud particles. This initial step prevents grinding dirt deeper into the suede fibers during subsequent cleaning. For very caked-on mud, allow it to dry completely before attempting to brush it off.

> 💡 **Tip:** Always brush with the nap of the suede, not against it, to avoid damaging the delicate fibers.

### Step 2: Erase Scuffs and Light Marks

For scuffs, shiny spots, or minor dry stains, use a suede eraser. Gently rub the eraser back and forth over the affected area, applying light pressure. The eraser works by lifting the stain and roughing up the flattened nap. Afterward, use your suede brush to brush away any eraser residue and restore the nap.

> 💡 **Tip:** A clean pencil eraser can also work in a pinch for very light scuffs, but dedicated suede erasers are more effective and less likely to leave residue.

### Step 3: Even Out Water Stains (Optional)

Water can often leave dark, noticeable rings on suede. To treat these, lightly dampen a clean cloth with distilled water and gently rub the entire affected area, feathering out the edges of the water stain. The goal is to evenly dampen the whole section, allowing it to dry uniformly, which helps prevent new water rings from forming. Avoid oversaturating the suede.

> 💡 **Tip:** If using tap water, ensure it's not hard water, as mineral deposits can leave new marks. Distilled water is always safer.

### Step 4: Spot Clean Deeper Stains

For stubborn stains like oil, grease, or ink, you may need a specialized suede cleaner. Follow the product instructions carefully, typically applying a small amount to a clean cloth and dabbing the stain gently. Alternatively, a homemade solution of white vinegar diluted with an equal part of water can be dabbed onto the stain with a clean cloth. Blot the area, don't rub aggressively, to lift the stain.

> 💡 **Tip:** Always test any cleaning solution on an inconspicuous area first to ensure it doesn't cause discoloration or damage.

### Step 5: Air Dry Naturally

After cleaning, allow your suede shoes to air dry completely, away from direct heat or sunlight, which can damage the suede or cause it to shrink. To help maintain their shape during drying, insert shoe trees or stuff them with crumpled, unprinted paper. Avoid using newspapers as the ink can transfer to the suede.

> 💡 **Tip:** Patience is key during the drying process; rushing it can lead to permanent damage or misshapen shoes.

### Step 6: Restore Nap and Apply Protector

Once the shoes are thoroughly dry, use your suede brush again to gently brush the nap back into its original direction, restoring its soft, velvety texture. For long-term protection against future stains and water damage, apply a high-quality suede protector spray. Hold the can at the recommended distance and spray evenly, allowing it to dry completely before wearing.

> 💡 **Tip:** Regular application of a suede protector spray is the best defense against most stains and makes future cleaning much easier.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always clean suede promptly after it gets dirty; fresh stains are easier to remove.
- Brush your suede shoes regularly, even if they aren't visibly dirty, to maintain the nap and remove dust.
- Avoid wearing suede shoes in heavy rain or snow, as excessive moisture is their worst enemy.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Never use harsh chemicals, detergents, or wire brushes on suede, as they can cause irreversible damage.
- ⚠️ Do not soak suede shoes in water or put them in a washing machine.
- ⚠️ Avoid direct heat sources like radiators or hair dryers for drying, as this can crack or shrink the leather.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I use water to clean suede shoes?**

While generally not recommended to saturate suede, you can use a slightly damp cloth to treat water stains by evening out the moisture. For other stains, a very diluted white vinegar solution can be dabbed sparingly onto the affected area.

**How do I get oil or grease stains out of suede?**

For oil or grease, immediately blot the excess with a clean cloth. Then, sprinkle a generous amount of cornstarch or talcum powder over the stain and let it sit for several hours or overnight to absorb the oil. Brush off the powder with a suede brush afterward.

**What should I do if my suede shoes get wet in the rain?**

If your suede shoes get wet, blot off any excess water with a clean towel. Stuff them with crumpled, unprinted paper or use shoe trees to maintain their shape, and let them air dry completely away from direct heat. Once dry, brush the nap with a suede brush.

**Can I use a regular clothes brush or toothbrush on suede?**

It's best to use a specialized suede brush. These brushes have bristles designed to lift the nap without damaging the delicate fibers. A regular clothes brush might be too soft, and a toothbrush could be too harsh or uneven.

**How often should I clean my suede shoes?**

You should clean your suede shoes as needed when they show visible dirt or stains. However, regular light brushing after each wear helps maintain the nap and removes dust, extending their lifespan and fresh appearance.

**Can I use regular shoe polish or conditioner on suede?**

No, never use regular shoe polish or leather conditioner on suede. Suede is a napped leather and requires specific suede cleaners, erasers, and protectors. Using traditional polish will flatten the nap and permanently alter its texture and appearance.

**What's the best way to prevent stains on suede shoes?**

The best preventative measure is to apply a high-quality suede protector spray to your shoes immediately after purchase and reapply it regularly according to the product's instructions. This creates a barrier that repels water and stains, making cleaning easier.
