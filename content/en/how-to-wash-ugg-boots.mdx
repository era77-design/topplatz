To wash UGG boots, gently hand-clean them using a soft brush, cold water, and a specialized cleaner or mild soap. Avoid machine washing and harsh chemicals to protect the delicate sheepskin and suede materials.

## What You Need

- UGG cleaner & conditioner kit (recommended) OR mild soap (e.g., baby shampoo)
- Soft bristle brush (suede brush or soft toothbrush)
- Clean sponge or cloth
- Cold water
- Clean towels
- Shoe tree or crumpled unprinted paper
- Suede protector spray (optional)

## Steps

### Step 1: Prepare Your Boots

Before washing, use a suede brush or a soft-bristled brush to gently remove any loose dirt, dust, or debris from the surface of your UGG boots. This step prevents dirt from being pushed deeper into the material during the cleaning process and ensures a more effective wash.

> 💡 **Tip:** For stubborn dried mud, allow it to fully dry and then gently scrape or brush it off before dampening the boots.

### Step 2: Dampen the Surface

Lightly moisten the entire outer surface of your UGG boots with cold water. You can use a clean sponge or cloth for this. Ensure the boots are evenly damp, but avoid saturating them completely, as over-wetting can damage the sheepskin.

> 💡 **Tip:** Dampening the entire boot evenly helps prevent water spots or lines from forming during the drying process.

### Step 3: Apply Cleaner

Apply a small amount of UGG cleaner (or a mild soap like baby shampoo diluted in cold water) to your sponge or soft brush. Gently scrub the entire surface of the boot in small, circular motions. Focus on stained areas without applying excessive pressure, which could damage the delicate material.

> 💡 **Tip:** Always test the cleaning solution on an inconspicuous area first to ensure it doesn't cause discoloration.

### Step 4: Rinse Gently

Using a clean, damp cloth or sponge rinsed in cold water, gently wipe away the cleaning solution from the boots. Be thorough but avoid saturating the material. The goal is to remove all soap residue without over-wetting the sheepskin.

> 💡 **Tip:** Change your rinse water or rinse your cloth frequently to ensure you're removing soap, not just spreading it around.

### Step 5: Stuff and Air Dry

Gently stuff the boots with clean, unprinted paper (like paper towels or newspaper) or use shoe trees to help them maintain their shape as they dry. Place them in a well-ventilated area away from direct sunlight or heat to air dry completely. This process can take 24-48 hours or longer.

> 💡 **Tip:** Replace the paper stuffing periodically if it becomes too damp to speed up drying and prevent mildew.

### Step 6: Restore Suede Nap

Once your boots are completely dry, use a soft suede brush to gently brush the exterior in one direction. This action will restore the natural nap of the suede, making them look refreshed and uniform after the washing process.

> 💡 **Tip:** Brush lightly and evenly across the entire surface to achieve a consistent texture.

### Step 7: Apply Protector (Optional)

For added protection against future stains and water damage, apply a quality suede and sheepskin protector spray. Ensure the boots are fully dry before application, and follow the product instructions carefully for the best results. This creates a barrier against elements.

> 💡 **Tip:** Apply protector outdoors or in a well-ventilated area, holding the can at the recommended distance.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always use cold water to prevent shrinking or damage to the delicate sheepskin material.
- Never use a washing machine or tumble dryer, as this will cause irreversible damage to your UGG boots.
- Test any cleaning solution on an inconspicuous area first to ensure no discoloration or adverse reactions.
- Allow boots to air dry naturally, away from direct heat sources like heaters or sunlight.
- Treat fresh stains immediately for the best chance of complete removal before they set in.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Do not machine wash or tumble dry UGG boots, as this will cause irreversible damage, shrinkage, and stiffening of the material.
- ⚠️ Avoid using harsh detergents, bleach, or chemical cleaners not specifically designed for suede and sheepskin, as they can strip natural oils and cause damage.
- ⚠️ Never dry UGG boots in direct sunlight, near a heater, or with a hairdryer, as this can crack, stiffen, and permanently damage the delicate sheepskin.
- ⚠️ Over-wetting the boots can damage the sheepskin, leading to stiffening, a distorted shape, or prolonged drying times which can encourage mildew.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I put my UGG boots in the washing machine?**

No, you should never put UGG boots in a washing machine. The agitation and heat can cause the sheepskin to shrink, stiffen, and permanently damage the boots' delicate structure and material.

**How often should I clean my UGG boots?**

The frequency depends on how often you wear them and their exposure to dirt. Generally, a thorough cleaning every few months or as needed for visible stains is sufficient. Regular brushing helps maintain them between washes.

**What can I use if I don't have UGG cleaner?**

If you don't have a specialized UGG cleaner, a very mild soap like baby shampoo diluted in cold water can be used as an alternative. Always test it on a small, hidden area first to ensure it doesn't cause discoloration.

**How long do UGG boots take to dry?**

UGG boots can take anywhere from 24 to 48 hours, or even longer, to completely air dry. Drying time depends on humidity, temperature, and how wet the boots became during the cleaning process.

**Can I clean the inside of my UGG boots?**

Yes, you can gently clean the inside lining. Use a damp cloth with a small amount of diluted mild soap or baking soda solution to wipe the interior. Allow them to air dry thoroughly with crumpled paper inside to absorb moisture and maintain shape.

**What if my UGG boots get water stains?**

Water stains can often be removed by lightly dampening the entire affected area with cold water using a clean cloth, then allowing it to air dry evenly. This helps blend the stain and prevents a new water line from forming.

**Is it okay to use a hairdryer to speed up drying?**

No, you should never use a hairdryer or any direct heat source to dry UGG boots. High heat can cause the sheepskin to crack, shrink, and become stiff, leading to irreversible damage to the material and fit.
