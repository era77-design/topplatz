Planting potatoes is a rewarding process that yields delicious results, whether you have a large garden or just a small container. The key steps involve selecting healthy seed potatoes, preparing them for planting, and providing the right soil and growing conditions.

## What You Need

- Certified seed potatoes
- Sharp knife (optional, for cutting seed potatoes)
- Shovel or trowel
- Well-draining garden soil or potting mix
- Compost or well-rotted manure
- Balanced organic fertilizer (optional)
- Watering can or hose
- Garden space or large containers (pots, buckets, grow bags)

## Steps

### Step 1: Choose and Prepare Seed Potatoes

Select certified disease-free seed potatoes from a reputable supplier. If your seed potatoes are larger than a chicken egg, you can cut them into pieces, ensuring each piece has at least one or two 'eyes' (small indentations where sprouts emerge). Allow the cut pieces to cure for 1-2 days in a warm, humid place to form a protective skin, which helps prevent rot.

### Step 2: Select Your Planting Location

Potatoes thrive in full sun, meaning at least 6-8 hours of direct sunlight daily. Choose a spot in your garden with well-draining, loose soil. If planting in containers, ensure they are large (at least 10-15 gallons per plant) and have adequate drainage holes.

### Step 3: Prepare the Soil

Potatoes prefer slightly acidic soil (pH 5.0-6.0) that is rich in organic matter. Amend your garden soil with 2-4 inches of compost or well-rotted manure to improve fertility and drainage. For containers, use a high-quality, well-draining potting mix, ideally one formulated for vegetables.

### Step 4: Plant the Seed Potatoes

In a garden bed, dig trenches 6-8 inches deep and about 3 feet apart. Place seed potato pieces cut-side down (or eye-side up if not cut) about 10-12 inches apart in the trenches. If using containers, add 4-6 inches of potting mix to the bottom, then place 1-2 seed potato pieces on top, depending on container size.

### Step 5: Cover and Water

Gently cover the planted seed potato pieces with 3-4 inches of soil. Avoid compacting the soil too much. Water thoroughly after planting to settle the soil around the seed potatoes. Keep the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged.

### Step 6: Begin Hilling as Plants Grow

Once potato plants emerge and reach about 6-8 inches tall, it's time to start 'hilling.' Gently mound soil or compost around the stems, leaving the top few inches of foliage exposed. This protects developing tubers from sunlight (which can turn them green and toxic) and encourages more potatoes to form. Repeat hilling every 2-3 weeks as the plants grow taller.

### Step 7: Provide Ongoing Care

Keep the soil consistently moist, especially during flowering and when tubers are actively forming. Avoid overhead watering late in the day to minimize fungal diseases. Fertilize with a balanced organic fertilizer every 2-4 weeks, following product instructions. Watch for pests and diseases, addressing them promptly.


## Tips & Tricks

- Consider 'chitting' (pre-sprouting) seed potatoes indoors for a few weeks before planting to encourage earlier growth.
- Rotate your potato crops each year to a different part of the garden to help prevent soil-borne diseases.
- Harvest potatoes when the foliage begins to yellow and die back, typically 70-120 days after planting, depending on the variety.
- Planting companion plants like marigolds or nasturtiums can help deter potato pests.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Do not plant potatoes from the grocery store, as they may be treated with sprout inhibitors and can carry diseases that could contaminate your garden.
- ⚠️ Green parts of potatoes, including skin and sprouts, contain solanine and are toxic. Always cut away any green areas before cooking and eating.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I plant potatoes?**

To plant potatoes, you'll first select certified seed potatoes and prepare them by cutting if they're large. Then, choose a sunny spot with well-draining soil, plant the pieces 6-8 inches deep, and cover them with soil. As the plants grow, you'll continuously mound soil around the stems in a process called hilling.

**How to plant potatoes from eyes?**

To plant potatoes from eyes, simply cut a seed potato into pieces, ensuring each piece has at least one or two visible 'eyes' (sprouts or buds). Let these pieces cure for a day or two to form a protective layer before planting them 6-8 inches deep in prepared soil.

**How to plant potatoes in a pot?**

Planting potatoes in a pot, bucket, or grow bag requires a large container with good drainage. Add 4-6 inches of potting mix, place your seed potato pieces, and cover with a few inches of soil. As the plant grows, continue to add more soil or compost around the stems until the container is nearly full.

**How to plant red potatoes?**

Planting red potatoes follows the same general guidelines as planting any other potato variety. Select certified red seed potatoes, prepare them by cutting if large, and plant them in a sunny location with fertile, well-draining soil, remembering to hill them as they grow to protect the tubers.

**What is the best time of year to plant potatoes?**

The best time to plant potatoes is in early spring, typically 2-4 weeks before the last expected frost date in your area, once the soil temperature consistently reaches at least 45°F (7°C). In warmer climates, a fall planting might also be possible.

**How deep should I plant seed potatoes?**

Initially, plant seed potato pieces about 6-8 inches deep in trenches or at the bottom of your chosen container. As the potato plant grows, you will progressively add more soil around the stems, known as hilling, to encourage more tubers to form and protect them from sunlight.

**How long does it take for potatoes to grow?**

The time it takes for potatoes to grow varies by variety, but generally, early varieties mature in 70-90 days, mid-season varieties in 90-110 days, and late-season varieties in 110-130 days. You'll typically harvest when the plant's foliage starts to yellow and die back.
