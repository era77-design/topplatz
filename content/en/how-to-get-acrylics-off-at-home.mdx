You can safely remove acrylic nails at home primarily by soaking them in 100% pure acetone. This method gently dissolves the acrylic, allowing it to be pushed off without damaging your natural nails underneath. Always prioritize patience and gentle handling to prevent harm.

## What You Need

- 100% pure acetone
- Cotton balls or pads
- Aluminum foil (cut into squares)
- Nail file (coarse grit)
- Nail buffer
- Cuticle pusher (wood or metal)
- Petroleum jelly or thick cream
- Hand lotion or cuticle oil

## Steps

### Step 1: File Down the Top Coat

Start by using a coarse nail file to gently file down the top layer of your acrylic nails. You don't need to remove the entire acrylic, just break the seal of the top coat and any polish or gel. This helps the acetone penetrate more effectively into the acrylic material, speeding up the removal process. Be careful not to file into your natural nail.

> 💡 **Tip:** Focus on the shiny top layer; once it looks dull, you've filed enough.

### Step 2: Protect Your Skin

Acetone can be very drying to your skin. Before soaking, apply a generous amount of petroleum jelly or a thick cream around the cuticles and the skin surrounding each nail. This creates a barrier, protecting your skin from the harsh drying effects of the acetone while allowing it to work on the acrylic. Reapply if needed during the process.

> 💡 **Tip:** Don't get petroleum jelly on the acrylic itself, as it can hinder the acetone's effectiveness.

### Step 3: Soak Nails in Acetone

Saturate a cotton ball or pad with 100% pure acetone. Place the soaked cotton directly on top of your acrylic nail, ensuring it covers the entire surface. Wrap each finger tightly with a small square of aluminum foil to secure the cotton ball in place and prevent the acetone from evaporating. Repeat for all ten fingers.

> 💡 **Tip:** You can also use a small glass bowl filled with acetone and soak your fingers directly, but foil method is less drying for surrounding skin.

### Step 4: Wait and Let it Dissolve

Allow your nails to soak for 15-20 minutes. The acetone needs time to break down and dissolve the acrylic. During this waiting period, you might feel a slight tingling sensation, which is normal. Avoid peeking too often, as this allows air in and slows down the dissolving process. Patience is key for safe removal.

> 💡 **Tip:** Gently massage the foil-wrapped fingers periodically to help the acetone work.

### Step 5: Gently Remove Acrylic

After the soaking time, remove one foil wrap and cotton ball. The acrylic should now be soft, gummy, and easily flake away. Use a cuticle pusher to gently scrape off the softened acrylic from your natural nail. Push from the cuticle towards the free edge. If the acrylic doesn't come off easily, do not force it.

> 💡 **Tip:** Work one nail at a time, keeping the others wrapped to continue soaking.

### Step 6: Re-soak if Necessary

If you encounter stubborn spots or if the acrylic isn't soft enough to remove without force, re-saturate a fresh cotton ball with acetone, reapply it to the nail, and re-wrap with foil. Allow it to soak for another 5-10 minutes. Repeat the gentle scraping process until all the acrylic is removed. Never pull or pry off hard acrylic, as this will damage your natural nail.

> 💡 **Tip:** It's better to re-soak multiple times than to damage your nails by forcing.

### Step 7: Clean and Hydrate Nails

Once all the acrylic is removed, gently buff your natural nails with a fine-grit buffer to smooth out any rough spots or residue. Wash your hands thoroughly to remove any remaining acetone. Immediately apply a generous amount of hand lotion or cuticle oil to rehydrate your nails and surrounding skin, as acetone is very drying. Consider giving your nails a break from enhancements.

> 💡 **Tip:** Apply cuticle oil daily for several days to help your nails recover their moisture and strength.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always use 100% pure acetone, not regular nail polish remover, which is too weak.
- Work in a well-ventilated area to avoid inhaling acetone fumes.
- Never pull, peel, or pry off acrylic nails; this causes severe damage to your natural nail bed.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Acetone is highly flammable; keep it away from open flames, heat, and sparks.
- ⚠️ Ensure good ventilation to avoid inhaling strong fumes, which can cause dizziness or nausea.
- ⚠️ Do not force or rip off acrylics, as this can tear off layers of your natural nail, leading to thinning, weakness, and pain.
- ⚠️ Avoid prolonged skin contact with acetone; protect surrounding skin with petroleum jelly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I use regular nail polish remover to take off acrylics?**

No, regular nail polish remover typically contains only a small percentage of acetone or uses non-acetone solvents. You need 100% pure acetone to effectively dissolve acrylic nails.

**How long does the entire process take?**

The entire process, including filing, soaking, and removal, usually takes about 20-30 minutes per hand. Patience is important for safe and effective removal.

**What if the acrylics aren't coming off easily after soaking?**

If the acrylic isn't soft enough to remove easily, do not force it. Re-saturate the cotton ball with acetone, re-wrap your finger in foil, and soak for another 5-10 minutes until it's soft enough to gently push off.

**Will removing acrylics at home damage my natural nails?**

If done correctly and without forcing, removing acrylics at home should not significantly damage your natural nails. The key is patience and allowing the acetone to fully dissolve the acrylic rather than prying or pulling.

**How can I care for my nails after removing acrylics?**

After removing acrylics, your natural nails will likely be dry and a bit fragile. Moisturize them frequently with hand lotion and cuticle oil, and consider using a nail strengthener. Avoid harsh chemicals and give them a break before applying new enhancements.

**Can I soak all my fingers in a bowl of acetone instead of using foil?**

Yes, you can soak all your fingers in a glass bowl of acetone. However, the foil method is often preferred because it reduces acetone evaporation and minimizes skin exposure, which can be very drying.

**Is it okay to just peel or chip off acrylics if they're lifting?**

Absolutely not. Peeling or chipping off acrylics, even if they're lifting, will inevitably pull off layers of your natural nail, causing significant thinning, weakness, and potential pain. Always use the acetone soak method for safe removal.
