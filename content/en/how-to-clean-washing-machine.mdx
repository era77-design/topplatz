To clean your washing machine, run an empty hot water cycle with white vinegar, then follow with a baking soda cycle. This helps remove soap scum, mildew, and odors, ensuring your clothes come out truly clean.

## What You Need

- White vinegar
- Baking soda
- Microfiber cloth
- Old toothbrush (optional)
- Spray bottle (optional, for exterior cleaner)

## Steps

### Step 1: Empty and Prepare the Washer

Ensure your washing machine is completely empty. Remove any clothes or items from the drum. This prevents anything from being bleached or damaged during the cleaning cycles and allows the cleaning agents to work effectively on the machine's interior.

### Step 2: First Cleaning Cycle (Vinegar)

Pour two cups of white vinegar directly into the detergent dispenser or the main wash drum. Select the hottest water setting and the longest cycle available on your machine. Run this cycle completely without any laundry inside. The vinegar helps to break down mineral deposits, soap scum, and kill bacteria and mold.

### Step 3: Second Cleaning Cycle (Baking Soda)

Once the vinegar cycle is complete, sprinkle half a cup of baking soda directly into the drum. Run another hot water cycle on the longest setting. Baking soda acts as a natural deodorizer and a gentle abrasive, helping to scrub away any remaining grime and neutralize odors left by the vinegar.

### Step 4: Clean the Detergent Dispenser

While the second cycle runs, if your detergent dispenser drawer is removable, take it out. Scrub it thoroughly with an old toothbrush and hot, soapy water to remove accumulated residue, mold, and mildew. Rinse it well and allow it to dry before replacing it in the machine. This step is crucial for preventing future buildup.

### Step 5: Wipe Down the Drum and Gasket

After both cycles are finished, open the washer door. Use a clean microfiber cloth dampened with a vinegar-water solution (50/50) to wipe down the inside of the drum. For front-loaders, pay special attention to the rubber gasket around the door, pulling back its folds to clean any hidden mold or mildew. This area is a common breeding ground for odors.

### Step 6: Clean the Exterior

Spray a general all-purpose cleaner or a vinegar-water solution onto a clean cloth (not directly onto the machine). Wipe down the exterior surfaces of your washing machine, including the control panel, knobs, and lid/door. This removes dust, fingerprints, and spills, leaving your appliance sparkling clean.

### Step 7: Leave Door Ajar

To prevent mildew and musty odors from forming between washes, always leave the washing machine door and the detergent drawer slightly ajar. This allows for proper air circulation and ensures the drum and dispenser can fully dry out, inhibiting the growth of mold and mildew.


## Tips & Tricks

- For front-loaders, pay extra attention to the rubber gasket seal, as it's a common spot for mold and mildew. Wipe it dry after each use.
- Always use high-efficiency (HE) detergent if your machine is HE-compatible, as it produces fewer suds and prevents residue buildup.
- To maintain freshness, perform a quick clean every 1-2 months and a deep clean every 3-6 months depending on usage.
- After each wash, wipe down the inside of the door and the gasket, and leave the door slightly ajar to allow the drum to air dry.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Never mix vinegar and bleach, as this can create dangerous chlorine gas.
- ⚠️ Always refer to your washing machine's owner's manual for specific cleaning instructions or recommendations from the manufacturer.
- ⚠️ For exterior cleaning, avoid spraying water directly onto the control panel or electrical components.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why does my washing machine smell musty?**

A musty smell usually indicates the presence of mold, mildew, or detergent residue buildup. These can thrive in the damp, dark environment of the machine, especially in the rubber gasket or detergent dispenser.

**How often should I clean my washing machine?**

It's recommended to deep clean your washing machine every 1 to 3 months, depending on how frequently you use it and the hardness of your water. Regular maintenance helps prevent residue buildup and odors.

**Can I use bleach to clean my washing machine?**

While bleach can disinfect, it's generally recommended to use vinegar and baking soda first for a thorough clean without harsh chemicals. If using bleach, run a separate cycle with only bleach and ensure no vinegar is present.

**What's the best way to clean the rubber seal on a front-load washer?**

The rubber gasket (or seal) on front-loaders is prone to mold. Wipe it regularly with a vinegar-dampened cloth, pulling back the folds to clean thoroughly. You can also use an old toothbrush for stubborn spots.

**Do I need to use special washing machine cleaner products?**

While commercial cleaners are effective, white vinegar and baking soda are excellent, natural, and cost-effective alternatives for most washing machine cleaning needs. They effectively tackle odors and residue.

**What causes mold in my washing machine?**

Mold thrives in dark, damp environments with organic material (like detergent residue and lint). Leaving the door closed after a wash, using too much detergent, or not regularly cleaning the machine contributes to mold growth.

**Can I put vinegar and baking soda in at the same time?**

No, it's best to run separate cycles for vinegar and baking soda. When combined, they neutralize each other, reducing their individual cleaning effectiveness. Use vinegar first, then baking soda.
