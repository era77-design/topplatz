To get rid of cat pee smell, act quickly by blotting up fresh urine and then treating the area with an enzymatic cleaner designed to break down uric acid crystals. Follow up with a thorough rinse and drying to prevent lingering odors and stains. Persistence is key for older, set-in smells.

## What You Need

- Paper towels or old rags
- Enzymatic pet odor cleaner
- White vinegar
- Baking soda
- Spray bottle
- Rubber gloves
- Black light (optional)
- Wet vac or carpet cleaner (optional)

## Steps

### Step 1: Locate the Source of the Odor

If the stain isn't visible, use your nose to pinpoint the exact location of the cat urine. For dried, invisible stains, a UV black light in a darkened room can make urine glow, revealing the affected areas clearly. Check both horizontal and vertical surfaces, as cats sometimes spray.

> 💡 **Tip:** Don't forget to check under furniture, along baseboards, and even on curtains or walls.

### Step 2: Blot Up Fresh Urine Immediately

For a fresh accident, speed is crucial. Place several layers of paper towels or clean, old rags over the wet area and press down firmly to absorb as much urine as possible. Avoid rubbing, as this can spread the urine and push it deeper into fibers. Replace towels until no more moisture transfers.

> 💡 **Tip:** Stand on the paper towels for a few minutes to apply maximum pressure and absorption.

### Step 3: Pre-Treat with a Vinegar Solution

Mix equal parts white vinegar and water in a spray bottle. Generously spray the affected area, allowing the solution to soak in for about 10-15 minutes. Vinegar is a natural odor neutralizer and helps break down some of the urine components. After dwelling, blot the area again with clean towels to remove the vinegar solution.

> 💡 **Tip:** Ensure the area is well-ventilated while using vinegar, as its scent can be strong initially.

### Step 4: Apply an Enzymatic Pet Odor Cleaner

This is the most critical step for eliminating cat pee smell permanently. Enzymatic cleaners contain bacteria that produce enzymes to break down uric acid crystals, which are the main cause of the persistent odor. Saturate the area according to the product's instructions, ensuring it reaches as deep as the urine penetrated. For a comprehensive guide on selecting and using the best products, refer to [How to Get Rid of Cat Pee Smell: A Complete Guide](/en/how-to-get-rid-of-cat-pee-smell-guide).

> 💡 **Tip:** Do not use other chemical cleaners before the enzymatic one, as they can interfere with its effectiveness.

### Step 5: Allow to Air Dry Completely

After applying the enzymatic cleaner, allow the area to air dry completely. This process can take several hours or even a day, depending on the humidity and the extent of saturation. Do not apply heat, as this can set the stain and odor permanently. Keep pets and children away from the treated area during this time.

> 💡 **Tip:** Placing a fan near the treated area or opening windows can significantly speed up the drying process.

### Step 6: Re-Evaluate and Repeat if Necessary

Once the area is thoroughly dry, check for any lingering odor. If the smell persists, it indicates that some urine or uric acid crystals remain. Repeat the application of the enzymatic cleaner, allowing it to dwell and dry again. ## Tips & Tricks

- Always test any cleaning solution on an inconspicuous area first to ensure it doesn't damage or discolor the material.
- Address the underlying reason for your cat's inappropriate urination by consulting a vet to rule out medical issues, and reviewing litter box hygiene.
- Sprinkle baking soda over the dried area after treatment to absorb any residual odors, then vacuum after a few hours.
- Consider using an air purifier or odor-absorbing products in the room until the smell is completely gone.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Never use ammonia-based cleaners, as their scent mimics urine and can attract your cat to re-mark the same spot.
- ⚠️ Avoid using a steam cleaner on cat urine stains, as the heat can permanently set the odor and stain into fabrics.
- ⚠️ Always wear rubber gloves when cleaning cat urine to protect your hands from bacteria and harsh cleaning agents.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why does cat pee smell so bad and linger?**

Cat urine contains uric acid crystals that don't dissolve easily in water and react with bacteria to produce a strong, pungent ammonia-like odor. These crystals can reactivate with humidity, causing the smell to return even after cleaning.

**Can I use bleach to clean cat urine?**

No, bleach is not recommended for cleaning cat urine. It can create toxic fumes when mixed with ammonia in urine, and it may not effectively remove the odor, potentially even setting the stain permanently.

**How do I remove cat pee smell from a mattress?**

For mattresses, blot up as much urine as possible, then drench the area with an enzymatic cleaner. Let it air dry completely, potentially with a fan. Sprinkle baking soda over the dry spot, let it sit for several hours or overnight, then vacuum.

**What about old, dried cat pee smells?**

Old, dried smells require thorough saturation with an enzymatic cleaner, as the uric acid crystals are deeply embedded. Use a black light to locate all affected areas, and be prepared to repeat the treatment multiple times for full effectiveness.

**Why is my cat peeing outside the litter box?**

Inappropriate urination can be due to medical issues (like UTIs), stress, or litter box problems (e.g., cleanliness, type of litter, location, number of boxes). Always consult a veterinarian first to rule out health concerns.

**Does baking soda really work for cat pee smell?**

Baking soda is an excellent odor absorbent and can help neutralize lingering smells after the main cleaning. However, it doesn't break down uric acid crystals, so it should be used as a supplementary step after applying an enzymatic cleaner.

**How long does it take for the smell to go away completely?**

The time it takes depends on the severity and age of the stain, and the thoroughness of cleaning. Fresh stains can be eliminated quickly, but old, deep stains may require several treatments over a few days or weeks to completely remove the odor.
