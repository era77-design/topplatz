A leaky bathtub faucet is a common household nuisance, wasting water and causing annoying drips. Most leaks are due to worn-out internal components like washers, O-rings, or cartridges, which are relatively easy to replace. This guide will walk you through the steps to diagnose and repair your leaky faucet.

## What You Need

- Screwdrivers (Phillips and flathead)
- Adjustable wrench or pliers
- Utility knife or putty knife
- New washers, O-rings, or cartridge (matching your faucet type)
- Plumber's grease (silicone-based)
- Rag or towel
- Bucket (optional)
- Wire brush (for cleaning)

## Steps

### Step 1: Turn Off the Water Supply

Before starting any plumbing repair, locate and turn off the main water supply to your home, or at least the shut-off valves for the bathtub if available. This crucial step prevents flooding and allows you to work safely. Open the faucet briefly to drain any residual water from the pipes after shutting off the supply.

### Step 2: Remove the Faucet Handle and Trim

Carefully remove the decorative cap or screw cover on the faucet handle, usually found in the center. Use a screwdriver to unscrew the handle screw and pull the handle off. Next, use a utility knife or flathead screwdriver to gently pry off any escutcheon plates or decorative trim covering the faucet body, exposing the internal components.

### Step 3: Identify the Type of Faucet and Leak Source

Bathtub faucets typically use compression, cartridge, ball, or ceramic disc valves. Once the handle and trim are removed, you'll see the valve stem or cartridge. For compression faucets, look for a packing nut and stem. For others, it will be a cartridge or ball assembly. The leak usually originates from a worn washer, O-ring, or the entire cartridge.

### Step 4: Disassemble and Inspect Components

Depending on your faucet type, use an adjustable wrench or pliers to carefully unscrew the packing nut (compression faucet) or remove the retaining clip/nut holding the cartridge in place. Gently pull out the stem, cartridge, or ball assembly. Inspect all washers, O-rings, and seals for signs of wear, cracks, or deterioration. Note their exact position for reassembly.

### Step 5: Replace Worn Parts

Take the worn components (washer, O-ring, or entire cartridge) to a hardware store to ensure you purchase exact matches. Clean any mineral deposits or debris from the faucet body using a wire brush or cloth. Apply a thin layer of plumber's grease to new O-rings and moving parts before installation to ensure smooth operation and a better seal.

### Step 6: Reassemble the Faucet

Carefully reinsert the new parts in the reverse order of disassembly. Ensure everything is seated correctly and tightened securely, but do not overtighten, as this can damage components. Replace the trim, handle, and decorative cap. Make sure the handle operates smoothly and is aligned properly.

### Step 7: Restore Water Supply and Test

Once everything is reassembled, slowly turn the main water supply back on. Check for any immediate leaks around the faucet base or spout. Turn the faucet on and off several times to ensure proper function and to check for drips. If the leak persists, you may need to re-examine the components or consider professional help.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always keep a small container or bucket under the faucet area during disassembly to catch any drips or dropped parts.
- Take photos with your phone at each step of disassembly; this makes reassembly much easier, especially for complex cartridges.
- If mineral deposits are severe, consider using white vinegar to soak and clean metal parts before reassembly.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Always turn off the water supply before attempting any plumbing repairs to prevent water damage.
- ⚠️ Do not overtighten nuts or screws, as this can strip threads or crack plastic components, leading to more extensive damage.
- ⚠️ If you encounter difficulty or uncertainty, it's best to consult a professional plumber to avoid further complications.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I fix a leaky bathtub faucet?**

To fix a leaky bathtub faucet, you typically need to turn off the water, disassemble the faucet handle and trim, identify the worn internal component (like a washer, O-ring, or cartridge), replace it with a new matching part, and then reassemble the faucet. Testing after reassembly is crucial to ensure the leak is resolved.

**What are the common causes of a leaky bathtub faucet?**

The most common causes of a leaky bathtub faucet include worn-out rubber washers in compression faucets, deteriorated O-rings in stem assemblies, or a faulty cartridge in newer single-handle faucets. Mineral deposits can also build up, preventing a complete seal and leading to drips.

**How do I fix a bathtub faucet that drips constantly?**

A constantly dripping bathtub faucet usually indicates a problem with the internal sealing components. You'll need to identify your faucet type (compression, cartridge, etc.) and replace the specific worn part, such as a washer, O-ring, or the entire cartridge, after turning off the water supply.

**What tools do I need to fix a leaky bathtub faucet?**

Essential tools for fixing a leaky bathtub faucet typically include screwdrivers (Phillips and flathead), an adjustable wrench or pliers, a utility knife, and replacement parts specific to your faucet. Plumber's grease and a rag are also very helpful.

**Can I fix a leaky bathtub faucet myself, or do I need a plumber?**

Many minor bathtub faucet leaks can be fixed by a DIY enthusiast with basic tools and some patience. However, if the leak persists after attempting repairs, if you're unsure about the process, or if the faucet requires more complex internal work, it's advisable to call a professional plumber.

**When should I consider replacing my bathtub faucet instead of fixing it?**

You should consider replacing your bathtub faucet if it's very old, if replacement parts are unavailable, if the faucet body itself is corroded or damaged, or if repeated repairs fail to stop the leak. Sometimes, a full replacement is more cost-effective and provides a longer-lasting solution. For guidance on replacement, see our guide on [How to Change a Bathtub Faucet: DIY Replacement Guide](/en/how-to-change-a-bathtub-faucet).

**How long does it typically take to fix a leaky bathtub faucet?**

For a simple repair where you have the correct replacement parts on hand, fixing a leaky bathtub faucet can often take between 15 to 45 minutes. The time can vary depending on the faucet's complexity and your experience level.
