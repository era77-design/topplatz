Fixing a leaky bathtub faucet typically involves replacing worn-out internal components like washers, O-rings, or cartridges. This DIY repair can stop drips, save water, and prevent further damage to your plumbing system.

## What You Need

- Screwdriver set (Phillips and flathead)
- Adjustable wrench
- Pliers
- Utility knife or small flathead screwdriver
- Replacement O-rings, washers, or cartridge (specific to your faucet model)
- Plumber's grease
- Rag or towel
- Small brush (for cleaning)
- White vinegar (for cleaning mineral deposits)

## Steps

### Step 1: Turn Off the Water Supply

Locate the main water shut-off valve for your home, typically in the basement, utility closet, or near the water meter. Turn it off completely to prevent flooding during the repair. Open the leaky faucet to drain any remaining water from the pipes and relieve pressure.

### Step 2: Drain Remaining Water and Protect the Drain

After turning off the main water supply, ensure the leaky faucet is open to release any residual water and pressure. Place a rag over the drain opening or insert a stopper to prevent small screws, washers, or other parts from falling down the drain during the repair process.

### Step 3: Remove Faucet Handles and Trim

Use a screwdriver to carefully remove the decorative caps on the faucet handles, if present. Then, unscrew the handle screws and pull off the handles. Next, remove any escutcheon plates or trim that cover the faucet body, exposing the internal valve stems or cartridge.

### Step 4: Identify and Replace Worn Components

Depending on your faucet type (compression, disc, ball, or cartridge), you'll need to remove the internal mechanism. For compression faucets, replace worn rubber washers and O-rings. For cartridge faucets, carefully pull out the old cartridge with pliers and insert a new one, ensuring correct orientation. It's crucial to have the correct replacement parts for your specific faucet model.

### Step 5: Clean and Lubricate Components

Before reassembly, use a small brush and white vinegar to clean away any mineral deposits or gunk from the faucet body and removed parts. Apply a thin layer of plumber's grease to new O-rings and any moving parts. This ensures a good seal and smooth operation, helping to prevent future leaks.

### Step 6: Reassemble the Faucet

Carefully put all the components back in reverse order of disassembly. Ensure all screws are snug but do not overtighten them, as this can damage the parts or the faucet body. Reattach the handles, trim, and decorative caps securely.

### Step 7: Test for Leaks

Slowly turn the main water supply back on. Check the faucet thoroughly for any drips or leaks. Turn the faucet on and off several times to ensure proper function and confirm that the leak has been successfully stopped.


## Tips & Tricks

- Before starting, take a picture of your faucet setup for reference during reassembly.
- Place a towel in the tub to protect the finish from dropped tools or parts.
- If you can't identify your faucet type or find replacement parts, take the old part to a hardware store for assistance.
- Consider replacing both hot and cold components if one is leaking, as they are likely the same age and condition.
- If the repair seems too complex or doesn't resolve the issue, you might consider a full replacement. Learn [How to Change a Bathtub Faucet - DIY Replacement Guide](/en/how-to-change-a-bathtub-faucet).

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Always turn off the main water supply before attempting any plumbing repair to prevent flooding.
- ⚠️ Be careful not to scratch chrome or other delicate finishes on your faucet during disassembly and reassembly.
- ⚠️ If you are unsure at any point or uncomfortable with the repair, it is best to consult a professional plumber.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I fix a leaky bathtub faucet?**

To fix a leaky bathtub faucet, you'll need to turn off the water supply, disassemble the faucet, identify and replace worn internal components like washers, O-rings, or the cartridge, and then reassemble it carefully.

**What causes a bathtub faucet to leak?**

Most bathtub faucet leaks are caused by worn-out rubber washers, O-rings, or a faulty cartridge inside the faucet mechanism. Over time, these components can degrade or accumulate mineral deposits, preventing a proper seal.

**Can I fix a leaky bathtub faucet myself?**

Yes, fixing a leaky bathtub faucet is a common DIY plumbing task that many homeowners can accomplish with basic tools and some patience. Identifying your faucet type is the most crucial step.

**How do you fix a bathtub faucet that drips?**

To fix a bathtub faucet that drips, you'll generally need to turn off the water, remove the handle and trim, and then replace the internal worn-out parts such as a rubber washer, O-ring, or the entire cartridge that is no longer sealing correctly.

**What tools do I need to fix a leaky faucet?**

You'll typically need screwdrivers (Phillips and flathead), an adjustable wrench, pliers, a utility knife, and the correct replacement parts (O-rings, washers, or a new cartridge) specific to your faucet model.

**How long does it take to fix a leaky bathtub faucet?**

A typical leaky bathtub faucet repair can take anywhere from 30 minutes to an hour, depending on your experience level, the specific type of faucet you have, and the ease of finding replacement parts.
