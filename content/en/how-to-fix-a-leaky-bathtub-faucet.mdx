A leaky bathtub faucet can be a constant annoyance and waste a surprising amount of water. Fortunately, most leaks are caused by worn-out components that you can replace yourself with basic tools. This guide will walk you through the process of fixing your dripping faucet.

## What You Need

- Screwdriver (Phillips and flathead)
- Adjustable wrench
- Pliers
- New washers, O-rings, or a replacement cartridge kit (specific to your faucet model)
- Plumber's grease
- Utility knife or small flathead screwdriver (for prying)
- Rags or towels

## Steps

### Step 1: Turn Off the Water Supply

Before starting any plumbing work, it's crucial to shut off the water supply to your bathtub. Look for the main shut-off valve for your house or, if available, specific valves for the bathroom or bathtub. Turn both hot and cold water handles on the faucet to drain any remaining water from the pipes after the supply is off.

### Step 2: Remove the Faucet Handles

Most bathtub faucets have decorative caps on the handles covering the screws. Carefully pry these off with a utility knife or a small flathead screwdriver. Once the caps are removed, use a screwdriver to loosen and remove the screws holding the handles in place. Gently pull the handles straight off the stem.

### Step 3: Access the Faucet Stem or Cartridge

Beneath the handles, you'll find a escutcheon plate or a packing nut that holds the faucet stem or cartridge in place. Remove any screws holding these in place, then use an adjustable wrench to carefully unscrew the packing nut or the cartridge retaining nut. Once loose, you can usually pull the stem or cartridge straight out.

### Step 4: Inspect and Replace Worn Parts

Examine the removed stem or cartridge for worn-out rubber washers, O-rings, or seals. These are the most common culprits for leaks. Use a small screwdriver or utility knife to carefully remove the old, deteriorated parts. Replace them with new ones, ensuring they match the size and type of the originals. If your faucet uses a cartridge, you might need to replace the entire cartridge unit; take the old one to a hardware store to find an exact match. If your faucet is severely damaged or you're looking for a complete overhaul, consider reviewing [How to Change a Bathtub Faucet: A DIY Replacement Guide](/en/how-to-change-a-bathtub-faucet).

### Step 5: Lubricate and Reassemble

Apply a thin layer of plumber's grease to the new washers, O-rings, or the new cartridge to ensure smooth operation and a good seal. Carefully insert the stem or cartridge back into the faucet body, ensuring it's properly aligned. Hand-tighten the packing nut or retaining nut, then use your adjustable wrench for a final snug turn. Be careful not to overtighten, as this can damage the new parts or the faucet body.

### Step 6: Reattach Handles and Test

Place the faucet handles back onto the stems and secure them with their screws. Snap the decorative caps back into place. Once everything is reassembled, slowly turn the main water supply back on. Check for any immediate leaks around the faucet base. Turn the hot and cold water handles on and off a few times to ensure the leak has stopped and the faucet is functioning correctly.


## Tips & Tricks

- Before disassembling, take a photo of your faucet's components to help with reassembly.
- Place a rag or towel over your drain to prevent small parts from falling down it.
- If you can't find replacement parts, take the old ones or the entire stem/cartridge to a hardware store for identification.
- Consider replacing all washers and O-rings, even if only one looks worn, for a more lasting repair.
- Keep your workspace clean and organized to avoid losing small parts.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Always turn off the main water supply before attempting any faucet repair to prevent flooding.
- ⚠️ Do not overtighten screws or nuts, as this can strip threads or crack faucet components.
- ⚠️ If you're unsure about any step or encounter difficulties, it's best to consult a professional plumber.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How to fix a leaky bathtub faucet?**

To fix a leaky bathtub faucet, you typically need to turn off the water, disassemble the faucet handles and stem/cartridge, replace worn-out washers, O-rings, or the entire cartridge, then reassemble and test.

**What causes a bathtub faucet to leak?**

Most bathtub faucet leaks are caused by worn-out internal components such as rubber washers, O-rings, or a faulty cartridge. Over time, these parts degrade from water pressure and mineral deposits, losing their ability to create a watertight seal.

**How to fix bathtub faucet drips?**

Fixing bathtub faucet drips involves identifying the type of faucet (compression, cartridge, ball, or ceramic disc) and replacing the specific internal parts responsible for the leak, usually the washers, O-rings, or the entire cartridge. Always turn off the water first.

**Can I fix a leaky faucet myself?**

Yes, fixing a leaky bathtub faucet is a common DIY plumbing task. With the right tools and a clear understanding of your faucet type, most people can successfully complete the repair. This guide provides step-by-step instructions.

**How long does it take to fix a leaky faucet?**

Most leaky bathtub faucet repairs can be completed within 30 minutes to an hour, assuming you have the correct replacement parts on hand. The time may vary depending on your experience and the complexity of your faucet model.

**When should I call a plumber for a leaky faucet?**

You should call a plumber if you're unable to identify the source of the leak, if the faucet is old and corroded, if you've tried replacing parts without success, or if you're uncomfortable with the repair process. A professional can diagnose complex issues or recommend a full replacement.
