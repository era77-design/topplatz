Fixing a leaky bathtub faucet typically involves identifying the source of the leak, often worn-out washers, O-rings, or a faulty cartridge, and replacing the faulty components. This common DIY repair can save water, prevent further damage, and usually requires basic tools and a bit of patience.

## What You Need

- Screwdriver set (Phillips and flathead)
- Adjustable wrench or basin wrench
- Plumber's grease
- Replacement washers, O-rings, or cartridge (specific to your faucet model)
- Utility knife or small flathead screwdriver (for prying caps)
- Rags or towels
- Wire brush (optional, for cleaning)

## Steps

### Step 1: Turn Off the Water Supply

Before starting any plumbing work, it's crucial to turn off the main water supply to your house or, if available, the shut-off valves for the bathtub specifically. This prevents flooding once you begin disassembling the faucet. After turning off the water, open the leaky faucet to drain any remaining water from the pipes.

> 💡 **Tip:** Place a rag over the drain to prevent any small parts from falling into it during disassembly.

### Step 2: Disassemble the Faucet Handle

Carefully remove the decorative cap or screw cover from the center of the faucet handle. Use a screwdriver to unscrew the handle, then gently pull it off. Some handles may just pop off, while others have a set screw on the underside that needs loosening. Be gentle to avoid scratching the finish.

> 💡 **Tip:** Take a photo with your phone at each stage of disassembly to help with reassembly later.

### Step 3: Remove and Inspect the Cartridge or Stem

Once the handle is off, you'll see the faucet stem or cartridge. Depending on your faucet type, you might need an adjustable wrench to loosen the packing nut or a cartridge puller to remove the cartridge. Carefully extract the stem or cartridge and inspect it for any visible wear, cracks, or hardened rubber washers and O-rings. These are often the culprits behind leaks.

> 💡 **Tip:** Have a bucket or towel ready to catch any residual water that may come out when you remove the stem or cartridge.

### Step 4: Replace Worn Components

If you have a compression faucet, replace the old rubber washer at the end of the stem and any worn O-rings. For cartridge faucets, you'll need to replace the entire cartridge with a new one that matches your faucet model exactly. Apply a thin layer of plumber's grease to new O-rings before installation to ensure a good seal and smooth operation. If the faucet body itself is corroded or severely damaged beyond simple part replacement, you might consider replacing the entire unit. [How to Change a Bathtub Faucet: A DIY Replacement Guide](/en/how-to-change-a-bathtub-faucet) offers a comprehensive walkthrough for this.

> 💡 **Tip:** Bring the old parts to a hardware store to ensure you purchase the correct replacements. There are many variations!

### Step 5: Reassemble the Faucet

Carefully reinsert the new cartridge or stem with its fresh washers and O-rings into the faucet body. Tighten the packing nut or securing screws by hand first, then use your wrench, being careful not to overtighten. Replace the faucet handle, securing it with its screw, and then replace any decorative caps or covers. Ensure all components are snugly in place.

> 💡 **Tip:** Double-check that all screws are tight but not stripped, as overtightening can damage components.

### Step 6: Restore Water and Test for Leaks

Slowly turn the main water supply back on. Return to the bathtub and slowly open the faucet to allow water to flow. Check carefully for any drips or leaks around the handle, spout, and base of the faucet. If you notice any leaks, turn off the water again and recheck your connections, making sure everything is properly seated and tightened.

> 💡 **Tip:** If the leak persists after reassembly, you may have missed a worn part, or the issue could be more complex, possibly requiring professional help.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always identify your faucet's brand and model before buying replacement parts. This information is often found on the faucet itself or in your home's original plumbing documents.
- Keep a small container handy to hold screws and other small parts during disassembly to prevent them from getting lost.
- If you encounter stubborn corrosion or mineral buildup, use a wire brush and white vinegar to clean the affected areas before installing new parts.
- Consider replacing both hot and cold cartridges/washers even if only one side is leaking, as the other is likely to wear out soon.
- Don't use excessive force when tightening components; hand-tighten first, then give a quarter-turn with a wrench.
- For older faucets, it might be difficult to find exact replacement parts. In such cases, consider a full faucet replacement.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Always turn off the water supply completely before attempting any repairs to avoid flooding.
- ⚠️ Be careful not to strip screws or overtighten nuts, as this can cause more damage and make future repairs harder.
- ⚠️ If you encounter significant resistance or are unsure about any step, it's best to consult a professional plumber to prevent further damage.
- ⚠️ Ensure replacement parts are compatible with your specific faucet model; incorrect parts can lead to continued leaks or damage.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do you fix a leaky bathtub faucet that drips constantly?**

To fix a constantly dripping leaky bathtub faucet, you typically need to turn off the water, disassemble the handle, remove the stem or cartridge, and replace worn-out washers, O-rings, or the entire cartridge. Reassemble the faucet, turn the water back on, and test for leaks.

**What causes a bathtub faucet to leak?**

The most common causes of a leaky bathtub faucet are worn-out rubber washers, deteriorated O-rings, a faulty or corroded cartridge, or a loose packing nut. Mineral buildup and corrosion over time can also contribute to leaks.

**Can I fix a leaky bathtub faucet myself?**

Yes, fixing a leaky bathtub faucet is a common DIY project that most homeowners can tackle. With basic tools and the correct replacement parts, you can usually resolve the issue without needing to call a professional plumber.

**How do I know what replacement parts to buy to fix a bathtub faucet?**

To ensure you buy the correct replacement parts to fix a bathtub faucet, it's best to first disassemble the faucet and remove the faulty components. Take these old parts (washers, O-rings, or the entire cartridge) to a hardware store to match them exactly, or note your faucet's brand and model number.

**How much does it cost to fix a leaky bathtub faucet?**

The cost to fix a leaky bathtub faucet yourself is usually minimal, typically ranging from $5 to $30 for replacement washers, O-rings, or a new cartridge. If you hire a professional, labor costs can add significantly to this, depending on your location and the complexity of the repair.

**When should I call a professional for a leaky bathtub faucet?**

You should call a professional for a leaky bathtub faucet if you've attempted the repair yourself and the leak persists, if you can't identify the source of the leak, or if you encounter complex issues like corroded pipes or a completely seized faucet component. Professionals have specialized tools and expertise for more challenging situations.
