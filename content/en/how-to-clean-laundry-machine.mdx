To clean your laundry machine, start by removing any clothes and thoroughly cleaning the detergent dispenser and door seal. Then, run a hot water cycle with a specialized cleaner, white vinegar, or baking soda to sanitize the drum and eliminate buildup.

## What You Need

- White vinegar
- Baking soda (optional)
- Microfiber cloths
- Old toothbrush or small brush
- Spray bottle
- Laundry machine cleaner (optional)
- Warm water

## Steps

### Step 1: Empty the Machine and Prepare

Before you begin cleaning, ensure your washing machine is completely empty of any laundry. Take a moment to check pockets for forgotten items and remove them. This initial step ensures you have clear access to all parts of the machine that need cleaning.

> 💡 **Tip:** It's a good habit to check for forgotten items before every wash cycle to prevent damage to your machine.

### Step 2: Clean the Dispenser Drawer

Carefully pull out the detergent dispenser drawer. Many drawers have a release tab, allowing them to be fully removed. Soak any removable parts in warm, soapy water for a few minutes, then scrub away accumulated detergent, fabric softener, and mold residue with an old toothbrush or small brush. Wipe down the inside of the dispenser compartment with a damp cloth before reinserting the clean drawer.

> 💡 **Tip:** If your dispenser drawer isn't removable, use a spray bottle with a vinegar solution and a brush to reach into crevices.

### Step 3: Address the Door Seal/Gasket (Front-Loaders)

For front-loading machines, pull back the rubber gasket around the door opening. This area is notorious for trapping moisture, lint, and mildew. Thoroughly wipe it down with a cloth dampened with a 1:1 solution of white vinegar and water, paying close attention to all folds and crevices. For stubborn mold, you may need to scrub gently with a brush.

> 💡 **Tip:** After every wash, wipe the gasket dry and leave the door ajar to prevent mildew growth.

### Step 4: Run a Cleaning Cycle with Vinegar/Baking Soda

Add two cups of white vinegar to the detergent dispenser or directly into the drum of your empty machine. Select the hottest water setting and the largest load size, then run a full wash cycle. For an extra boost, you can add half a cup of baking soda directly to the drum at the start of the rinse cycle to help neutralize odors and scrub away grime.

> 💡 **Tip:** Always check your machine's manual for specific cleaning cycle recommendations if available.

### Step 5: Alternatively, Use a Machine Cleaner

If you prefer, you can use a commercially available washing machine cleaner tablet or liquid. Follow the product's instructions precisely, which typically involves adding the cleaner to the dispenser or directly into the drum. Then, run a hot water cycle as recommended by the manufacturer. These cleaners are designed to target buildup and odors effectively.

> 💡 **Tip:** Some machines have a 'Clean Washer' cycle; use this setting when using a commercial cleaner.

### Step 6: Wipe Down Interior and Exterior

Once the cleaning cycle is complete, use a clean, damp cloth to wipe down the inside of the drum, removing any loosened grime or residue. Don't forget to clean the exterior surfaces of your washing machine, including the control panel, top, and sides, with a general-purpose cleaner or a damp cloth to remove dust and smudges.

> 💡 **Tip:** Use a microfiber cloth for a streak-free shine on the exterior.

### Step 7: Leave Door Ajar to Air Dry

After cleaning, and ideally after every wash cycle, leave the washing machine door (and dispenser drawer) open for several hours or overnight. This crucial step allows the interior of the machine to thoroughly air dry. Proper ventilation prevents moisture buildup, which is the primary cause of mold, mildew, and unpleasant odors.

> 💡 **Tip:** Consider placing a small towel under the door if leaving it wide open is an obstruction.


## Tips & Tricks

- Make it a habit to clean your washing machine at least once a month to prevent stubborn buildup and persistent odors.
- Always use high-efficiency (HE) detergent if you have an HE washing machine, as it produces less suds and residue.
- After each use, wipe down the door seal and leave the washing machine door slightly ajar to allow air circulation and prevent moisture from accumulating.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Never mix white vinegar and bleach, as this combination can create dangerous chlorine gas.
- ⚠️ Always unplug your washing machine from the power outlet before cleaning any internal components or reaching into tight spaces.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How often should I clean my laundry machine?**

It's recommended to clean your laundry machine at least once a month, or more frequently if you do a lot of laundry or notice lingering odors. Regular cleaning prevents the accumulation of detergent residue and mildew.

**Can I use bleach to clean my washing machine?**

While bleach can sanitize, it's generally not recommended for regular machine cleaning as it can be harsh on components and may not address all types of buildup. Vinegar or specialized cleaners are often safer and more effective for comprehensive cleaning.

**What causes my washing machine to smell bad?**

A bad smell usually indicates the presence of mold, mildew, or detergent residue accumulating in damp areas like the door seal, dispenser, or drum. Using too much detergent or consistently washing with cold water can contribute to this issue.

**Is it safe to use vinegar and baking soda together to clean my washing machine?**

Yes, you can use them, but it's best not to mix them directly in the same cycle as they can neutralize each other. Run a cycle with vinegar first, then a separate cycle with baking soda, or add baking soda during the rinse cycle of the vinegar wash for best results.

**Do I need special washing machine cleaner tablets?**

While specialized cleaner tablets are convenient and very effective, you can achieve similar results using common household items like white vinegar and baking soda. The choice depends on your preference and what you have on hand.

**Why should I leave the washing machine door open after use?**

Leaving the door ajar allows air to circulate inside the drum, helping it to dry out completely after a wash. This crucial step prevents the growth of mold and mildew, which are common culprits behind unpleasant washing machine odors.

**My front-loader's gasket is really moldy. How can I clean it effectively?**

For stubborn mold, create a thick paste of baking soda and a small amount of water. Apply it generously to the moldy areas, let it sit for 30 minutes, then scrub thoroughly with an old toothbrush. Finish by wiping with a cloth dampened with a vinegar-water solution.
