Washing your pillows regularly helps remove allergens, dust mites, and sweat, contributing to a healthier sleep environment. Most pillows, including down and synthetic, can be machine washed, while memory foam often requires spot cleaning. Always check the care label first for specific instructions to avoid damage.

## What You Need

- Washing machine (for machine washable pillows)
- Mild laundry detergent
- Dryer balls or clean tennis balls
- Stain remover (optional)
- Clean cloths (for spot cleaning)
- Large basin or tub (for hand washing)

## Steps

### Step 1: 1. Consult the Care Label

Before doing anything else, locate and read the care label on your pillow. This label provides crucial information about the material and specific washing instructions, such as whether it's machine washable, dry-clean only, or requires spot cleaning. Following these guidelines is essential to prevent damage and ensure your pillow lasts longer.

### Step 2: 2. Pre-Treat Stains (If Applicable)

If your pillow has any visible stains, such as yellowing from sweat or other marks, pre-treat them before washing. Apply a small amount of stain remover directly to the stained area according to the product instructions. Gently rub it in with a clean cloth or soft brush and let it sit for a few minutes before proceeding with the wash cycle.

### Step 3: 3. Load the Washing Machine Correctly

For machine-washable pillows (down, synthetic), load two pillows into the washing machine to balance the load. This helps the machine agitate properly and ensures even washing and rinsing, preventing the washer from becoming unbalanced. If you only have one pillow, you can add a towel or another item of similar weight to balance the drum.

### Step 4: 4. Add Detergent and Select Cycle

Use a small amount of mild liquid laundry detergent – too much can leave residue that makes the pillow stiff. Set your washing machine to a gentle or delicate cycle with warm water. A second rinse cycle is often beneficial to ensure all detergent is thoroughly removed, which is important for preventing skin irritation and mildew.

### Step 5: 5. Run the Wash Cycle

Allow the washing machine to complete its full cycle, including the extra rinse if you selected it. Monitor the machine during the spin cycle to ensure it remains balanced, especially if you're only washing one pillow. If the machine becomes unbalanced, pause it, redistribute the load, and restart the spin cycle.

### Step 6: 6. Dry Thoroughly on Low Heat

Transfer the wet pillows to the dryer. Place two clean tennis balls or dryer balls inside the dryer with the pillows. These balls help to fluff the pillows and break up any clumps, ensuring they dry evenly and maintain their shape. Dry on a low heat setting, as high heat can damage synthetic fibers or shrink down feathers. This step is crucial for preventing mildew.

### Step 7: 7. Air Out and Fluff

Continue drying the pillows in short cycles, checking periodically to fluff them by hand. It's vital that pillows are completely dry all the way through before you put them back on your bed, which can take several hours. Once dry, let them air out for a few hours in a well-ventilated area to ensure any lingering moisture has evaporated and they smell fresh.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always wash two pillows at a time in the washing machine to balance the load and ensure proper cleaning.
- Use a minimal amount of mild, liquid detergent to prevent residue buildup, which can stiffen pillow fibers.
- Place dryer balls or clean tennis balls in the dryer with pillows to help fluff them and prevent clumping.
- Ensure pillows are completely dry before use to prevent mold and mildew growth, which can take several hours.
- Consider using pillow protectors under your pillowcases to extend the time between washes and keep pillows cleaner.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Never machine wash memory foam, gel, or buckwheat pillows; these require spot cleaning or specific care instructions.
- ⚠️ Avoid using high heat when drying synthetic or down pillows, as it can damage the materials and cause shrinking.
- ⚠️ Do not put a damp or wet pillow back on your bed, as this creates an ideal environment for mold and mildew.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How often should I wash a pillow?**

It's generally recommended to wash your pillows every 3 to 6 months to remove allergens, dust mites, and sweat buildup. Regular cleaning helps maintain hygiene and prolongs the life of your pillows, contributing to a healthier sleep environment.

**Can I machine wash my pillow if it's memory foam?**

No, memory foam pillows should not be machine washed or submerged in water. This can damage the foam structure and lead to mold growth. Instead, spot clean any stains with a mild detergent and water, then air dry thoroughly.

**How do I properly wash a pillow to remove yellow stains?**

To remove yellow stains, pre-treat them with a stain remover or a paste of baking soda and water before washing. For machine-washable pillows, use a gentle cycle with warm water and a good quality detergent, possibly adding a cup of white vinegar to the wash for extra brightening.

**What's the best way to wash my pillow to prevent clumping?**

To prevent clumping, especially with down or synthetic pillows, use a gentle wash cycle and ensure a thorough rinse. During drying, place dryer balls or clean tennis balls in the dryer with the pillows. These help agitate and fluff the filling, distributing it evenly as the pillow dries.

**How to wash a pillow made of down or feathers?**

Down or feather pillows can typically be machine washed on a gentle cycle with warm water and a small amount of mild detergent. It's crucial to dry them thoroughly on a low heat setting with dryer balls, as any lingering moisture can lead to mildew and odor.

**What should I do if the care label for my pillow is missing?**

If the care label is missing, it's safest to assume the pillow is either synthetic or down and proceed with a gentle machine wash and low-heat dry. For foam or specialty pillows where you're unsure, opt for spot cleaning to avoid potential damage.
