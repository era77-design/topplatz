To delete your Facebook account, you'll need to navigate through your account settings on either the desktop site or mobile app. This process involves accessing the 'Settings & Privacy' section, then 'Settings', and finally finding the 'Account Ownership and Control' option to initiate permanent deletion.

## What You Need

- Facebook account login details
- Internet connection
- Device (computer or smartphone)

## Steps

### Step 1: Understand the Difference: Deactivation vs. Deletion

Before proceeding, it's crucial to understand the difference between deactivating and permanently deleting your Facebook account. Deactivating temporarily hides your profile, posts, and comments, allowing you to reactivate it later. Permanent deletion removes all your data, and once confirmed, it cannot be undone after a grace period.

> 💡 **Tip:** Consider deactivation if you just need a break and plan to return to Facebook without losing your data.

### Step 2: Backup Your Facebook Data (Optional but Recommended)

If you have photos, videos, or posts you want to keep, Facebook allows you to download a copy of your information before deletion. Go to 'Settings & Privacy' > 'Settings' > 'Your Facebook Information' > 'Download Your Information' and select the data you wish to save. This ensures you retain precious memories and important data.

> 💡 **Tip:** Downloading your data can take time depending on the amount of content you have.

### Step 3: Navigate to Account Settings

On a desktop, click the downward-facing arrow in the top right corner, then select 'Settings & Privacy', followed by 'Settings'. On the mobile app, tap the three-line menu icon, scroll down to 'Settings & Privacy', and then tap 'Settings'. This is your starting point for managing your account.

> 💡 **Tip:** The exact location of the menu icon might vary slightly between iOS and Android apps.

### Step 4: Find Account Ownership and Control

Within the 'Settings' menu, look for 'Your Facebook Information' on the left sidebar (desktop) or scroll down to find 'Account Ownership and Control' (mobile and desktop). Click or tap on this option to proceed to the next set of choices related to your account's status. This section manages legacy contacts and deletion options.

> 💡 **Tip:** On older versions of Facebook, this might be under 'General Account Settings'.

### Step 5: Select Deactivation and Deletion

Under 'Account Ownership and Control', choose the 'Deactivation and Deletion' option. Facebook will then present you with two choices: 'Deactivate Account' or 'Delete Account'. Make sure to select 'Delete Account' for permanent removal, then click 'Continue to Account Deletion'.

> 💡 **Tip:** Double-check that you've selected 'Delete Account' to avoid accidental deactivation.

### Step 6: Confirm Permanent Account Deletion

After selecting 'Delete Account', Facebook will ask for your password to confirm your identity. It will also provide a final warning about what data will be lost and offer a 30-day grace period during which you can log back in to cancel the deletion request. Confirm your choice to initiate the deletion process.

> 💡 **Tip:** Remember, logging in during the 30-day grace period will cancel the deletion request.

### Step 7: Wait for Deletion to Complete

Once you confirm, your account will be scheduled for permanent deletion after the 30-day grace period. During this time, your profile will be hidden from others on Facebook. After the grace period, your account and all associated data will be permanently removed and cannot be recovered.

> 💡 **Tip:** It may take up to 90 days for all your data to be completely removed from Facebook's backup systems.


## Tips & Tricks

- Consider informing close friends or family on Facebook about your departure before deleting, as they won't be able to contact you via the platform afterward.
- Review any third-party apps or websites you've logged into using Facebook to update your login methods, as these connections will break upon deletion.
- Be patient; the full deletion process takes a bit of time due to the grace period and backend processing.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Once your account is permanently deleted after the grace period, all your data, including photos, posts, and messages, will be irrecoverable. Ensure you have backed up everything you wish to keep.
- ⚠️ Deleting your Facebook account will also delete any Facebook Pages you solely manage. Transfer ownership of Pages before deletion if you wish to keep them.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What's the difference between deactivating and deleting my Facebook account?**

Deactivating your account temporarily hides your profile and content, allowing you to reactivate it at any time. Deleting your account is permanent, removing all your data after a 30-day grace period, and cannot be undone.

**Can I get my account back after I delete it?**

You have a 30-day grace period after initiating deletion during which you can log back into your account to cancel the request. After this period, your account and all data are permanently removed and cannot be recovered.

**How long does it take for my Facebook account to be permanently deleted?**

After you initiate the deletion process, Facebook provides a 30-day grace period. Your account will be permanently deleted after these 30 days have passed, provided you do not log back in.

**Will deleting my Facebook account also delete my Messenger messages?**

Yes, permanently deleting your Facebook account will also delete your Messenger messages, as Messenger is linked to your Facebook profile. If you wish to save any conversations, consider taking screenshots or using Facebook's data download tool.

**What happens to my photos and posts when I delete my account?**

All your photos, posts, videos, and other content shared on Facebook will be permanently deleted along with your account. It is highly recommended to download a copy of your data before initiating the deletion process to preserve anything you want to keep.

**Can I download my Facebook data before deleting my account?**

Yes, Facebook offers a tool to download a copy of your information. You can find this option under 'Settings & Privacy' > 'Settings' > 'Your Facebook Information' > 'Download Your Information'.

**Will my friends be notified if I delete my Facebook account?**

No, Facebook does not send out a specific notification to your friends when you delete your account. Your profile will simply become unavailable to them after the deletion process is complete.
