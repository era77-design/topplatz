To get rid of cat pee smell, act quickly by blotting up fresh urine, then use an enzymatic cleaner specifically designed to break down uric acid crystals. For dried stains, pre-treat with a vinegar solution before applying the enzymatic cleaner to neutralize the odor permanently.

## What You Need

- Paper towels or clean cloths
- Rubber gloves
- Enzymatic cleaner (specifically for pet urine)
- White vinegar
- Baking soda
- Spray bottle
- Stiff brush (for carpets)
- Wet vac or carpet cleaner (optional)

## Steps

### Step 1: Locate and Blot the Fresh Stain

The first and most crucial step is to identify the source of the odor, especially if it's a fresh accident. Use paper towels or clean cloths to blot up as much of the urine as possible without rubbing, which can spread the stain. Apply firm pressure and repeat with fresh towels until no more liquid transfers.

### Step 2: Pre-treat Dried Stains with Vinegar

For dried or older stains, a pre-treatment can help reactivate the uric acid crystals, making them easier for the enzymatic cleaner to break down. Mix equal parts white vinegar and water in a spray bottle. Generously spray the affected area, ensuring it's thoroughly saturated, and let it sit for about 15-20 minutes.

> 💡 **Tip:** Always test any cleaning solution on an inconspicuous area first to ensure it doesn't cause discoloration or damage, especially on delicate fabrics or carpets.

### Step 3: Apply a Pet-Specific Enzymatic Cleaner

Enzymatic cleaners are essential for neutralizing cat urine odor because they contain enzymes that break down the uric acid crystals that cause the persistent smell. Follow the product instructions carefully, typically by saturating the soiled area with the cleaner. Allow it to air dry completely, which can take several hours or even overnight, as the enzymes need time to work. For more in-depth information and alternative methods, you might find our [How to Get Rid of Cat Pee Smell: A Complete Guide](/en/how-to-get-rid-of-cat-pee-smell) helpful.

> 💡 **Tip:** Do not use steam cleaners on cat urine stains before using an enzymatic cleaner, as the heat can permanently set the odor.

### Step 4: Sprinkle Baking Soda for Extra Odor Absorption

Once the enzymatic cleaner has dried, sprinkle a generous amount of baking soda over the treated area. Baking soda is a natural deodorizer that can absorb any lingering odors. Let the baking soda sit for several hours, or ideally overnight, to maximize its effectiveness.

### Step 5: Vacuum the Residue

After the baking soda has had sufficient time to absorb odors, vacuum the area thoroughly. Ensure all baking soda residue is removed. For carpets, you might need to go over the area multiple times from different directions to ensure complete removal and lift any remaining odor particles.

### Step 6: Clean Hard Surfaces and Walls

Cat urine can splash onto walls, baseboards, and seep into grout on hard floors. For these surfaces, clean with a solution of white vinegar and water, or a specialized pet odor remover. Wipe down thoroughly and allow to air dry. For porous surfaces like unsealed wood, the smell can be more challenging to remove and might require sanding and sealing. ## Tips & Tricks

- Act quickly! The sooner you clean a cat urine accident, the easier it will be to remove the smell permanently.
- Invest in a good quality enzymatic cleaner. Generic cleaners often mask the smell temporarily, but don't break down the uric acid crystals responsible for the lingering odor.
- Use a black light (UV flashlight) to locate hidden or dried urine stains that might not be visible to the naked eye, especially in darker areas.
- Ensure proper ventilation when cleaning. Open windows and use fans to air out the area and help with drying.
- Consider the underlying cause. If your cat is repeatedly urinating outside the litter box, consult with your vet to rule out medical issues or discuss behavioral solutions.
- Avoid using ammonia-based cleaners, as the smell can mimic urine and attract your cat back to the same spot.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Always test cleaning solutions on an inconspicuous area first to prevent damage or discoloration to carpets, fabrics, or surfaces.
- ⚠️ Never mix different cleaning products, especially those containing ammonia or bleach, as this can create dangerous fumes.
- ⚠️ Wear rubber gloves when handling cat urine to protect your hands from bacteria and strong cleaning agents.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why is cat pee smell so hard to remove?**

Cat pee is notoriously difficult to remove due to uric acid crystals. These crystals don't dissolve in water and can reactivate with humidity, releasing the strong, unpleasant odor over time. Enzymatic cleaners are designed to break down these crystals.

**Can I use bleach to clean cat urine?**

No, it's strongly advised not to use bleach. Bleach can react with ammonia in urine to create dangerous fumes. Additionally, it doesn't effectively neutralize the uric acid crystals responsible for the odor and can damage surfaces.

**What is an enzymatic cleaner and why is it important?**

An enzymatic cleaner contains beneficial bacteria that produce enzymes. These enzymes break down the uric acid salts, proteins, and other organic matter in pet urine, effectively eliminating the odor at its source rather than just masking it. It's crucial for permanent odor removal.

**How do I get cat pee smell out of a mattress?**

For mattresses, blot as much urine as possible, then saturate the area with an enzymatic cleaner. Sprinkle baking soda once dry and let it sit before vacuuming. You may need to repeat this process multiple times, and consider using a mattress protector afterwards.

**Will the smell ever truly go away?**

Yes, with the right approach and products, particularly a high-quality enzymatic cleaner, the cat pee smell can be completely and permanently eliminated. Persistence and thoroughness are key, especially for older or deeply set stains.

**My cat keeps peeing in the same spot, what should I do?**

First, ensure the area is thoroughly cleaned with an enzymatic cleaner to remove all traces of odor. Then, consult your veterinarian to rule out any medical issues. If medical issues are excluded, behavioral solutions like providing more litter boxes, changing litter type, or reducing stress might be necessary.

**Can I use a steam cleaner for cat urine?**

It's generally not recommended to use a steam cleaner on fresh cat urine stains before treating with an enzymatic cleaner. The heat from the steam can permanently set the protein and uric acid in the urine into the fibers, making the odor even harder to remove. Use an enzymatic cleaner first.
