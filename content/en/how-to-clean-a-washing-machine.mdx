To clean a washing machine, start by running an empty hot water cycle with white vinegar, then follow with a second hot cycle using baking soda. Don't forget to wipe down the exterior, detergent dispenser, and door seal for a thorough clean.

## What You Need

- White vinegar
- Baking soda
- Microfibre cloths
- Old toothbrush
- Spray bottle (optional)

## Steps

### Step 1: Prepare the Machine

First, ensure your washing machine is completely empty of clothes. Use a damp microfibre cloth to wipe down the exterior surfaces, including the control panel and around the detergent dispenser opening. This removes surface dust and grime before the deep clean.

> 💡 **Tip:** Always check your washing machine's manual for specific cleaning instructions or recommendations before you begin.

### Step 2: Run a Vinegar Cycle (Drum Clean)

Pour two cups of white vinegar directly into the detergent dispenser or the drum itself. Set your washing machine to its longest and hottest cycle available, such as 'Drum Clean,' 'Heavy Duty,' or a 'Sanitize' cycle with extra rinse. The vinegar helps to disinfect, break down mineral deposits, and remove soap scum.

> 💡 **Tip:** For very dirty machines, you can pause the cycle halfway through and let the vinegar solution soak for an hour before resuming.

### Step 3: Add a Baking Soda Cycle (Odor Removal)

Once the vinegar cycle has finished, sprinkle half a cup of baking soda directly into the washing machine drum. Run another hot water cycle. Baking soda is excellent at neutralizing lingering odors and further scouring away grime.

> 💡 **Tip:** Baking soda acts as a natural deodorizer and a mild abrasive, making it perfect for a thorough clean.

### Step 4: Clean the Detergent Dispenser

If your detergent dispenser drawer is removable, take it out and wash it thoroughly under warm running water using an old toothbrush to scrub away any built-up soap scum, mold, or mildew. If it's not removable, spray a solution of equal parts water and vinegar into the crevices and scrub with the toothbrush.

> 💡 **Tip:** Cleaning the dispenser regularly prevents detergent residue from hardening and blocking the flow.

### Step 5: Tackle the Door Seal/Gasket

For front-loading machines, carefully pull back the rubber gasket around the door. Use a microfibre cloth dampened with a vinegar solution to wipe away any mold, mildew, and residue that accumulates in the folds. Pay close attention to all crevices where moisture can trap grime.

> 💡 **Tip:** This area is a common breeding ground for mold, so a thorough wipe-down is crucial for preventing odors.

### Step 6: Perform a Final Wipe Down

After both cleaning cycles are complete, use a clean, damp cloth to wipe down the interior of the drum and ensure all visible residue is gone. Give the exterior of the machine another quick wipe for a sparkling finish. This ensures all loosened debris is removed.

> 💡 **Tip:** Use a dry cloth to buff the exterior for a streak-free shine.

### Step 7: Air Dry the Interior

To prevent future mold and mildew growth, always leave the washing machine door and the detergent dispenser drawer ajar for several hours after cleaning, or whenever the machine is not in use. This allows air to circulate and dry out the interior, inhibiting moisture buildup.

> 💡 **Tip:** Making this a habit after every wash cycle will significantly reduce the chances of mold and musty smells.


## Tips & Tricks

- Clean your washing machine at least once a month to prevent soap scum, mineral buildup, and unpleasant odors.
- Always use high-efficiency (HE) detergent if you have an HE washing machine to avoid excessive suds and residue.
- Wipe down the door seal and leave the door ajar after every wash load to minimize moisture and prevent mildew growth.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Never mix white vinegar with bleach, as this combination creates dangerous chlorine gas which can be harmful or fatal.
- ⚠️ Always unplug your washing machine before attempting to clean any electrical components or reaching into tight, difficult-to-see areas.
- ⚠️ If using commercial cleaning products, always follow the manufacturer's instructions carefully and ensure good ventilation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How often should I clean my washing machine?**

It's generally recommended to clean your washing machine at least once a month. If you wash frequently, use cold water often, or notice persistent odors, you might need to clean it every two to three weeks.

**Can I use bleach to clean my washing machine?**

Yes, bleach can sanitize your washing machine. However, many prefer natural alternatives like vinegar and baking soda which also break down mineral deposits and neutralize odors effectively without harsh fumes. Never mix bleach with vinegar.

**Why does my washing machine smell bad?**

A bad smell often indicates a buildup of mold, mildew, bacteria, and detergent residue, especially in the door seal, detergent dispenser, or drum. Regular cleaning, proper detergent use, and air drying can prevent this.

**What's the best way to clean a front-loader gasket?**

For front-loader gaskets, pull back the rubber seal and thoroughly wipe it down with a cloth dampened with a vinegar and water solution. Use an old toothbrush for stubborn grime in the crevices, then dry completely.

**Is vinegar safe for all washing machines?**

Yes, white vinegar is generally safe for most washing machines, including high-efficiency (HE) models. It's a natural disinfectant, descaler, and deodorizer. Always check your appliance manual for any specific contraindications, though they are rare for vinegar.

**Can I use a commercial washing machine cleaner?**

Absolutely, commercial washing machine cleaners are specifically formulated to tackle buildup, odors, and grime. Always follow the product's instructions carefully for the best and safest results.

**What causes mold in a washing machine?**

Mold thrives in damp, dark environments. It's typically caused by leaving the door closed after a wash, using too much detergent, or washing frequently at low temperatures, which don't effectively kill spores or dry out the machine.
