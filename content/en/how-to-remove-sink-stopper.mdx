Removing a sink stopper is a common task for cleaning grime, retrieving dropped items, or performing repairs. Most stoppers, particularly the common lift-rod type, can be removed with basic tools and a little know-how. This guide will walk you through the process safely and efficiently.

## What You Need

- Adjustable wrench or pliers
- Bucket or small container
- Towels or rags
- Flashlight (optional)
- Gloves (optional)
- Old toothbrush (for cleaning)

## Steps

### Step 1: Prepare Your Workspace

Before you begin, clear any items from the cabinet directly underneath the sink. Place a bucket or small container directly beneath the drain pipe to catch any drips or residual water. Lay down old towels or rags to protect the cabinet base from moisture.

### Step 2: Identify Your Stopper Type

Most bathroom sinks utilize a lift-rod stopper, which is connected to a pivot rod assembly located under the sink. Kitchen sinks often feature simpler basket strainers that lift directly out, or push-pull stoppers. This guide primarily focuses on the common lift-rod type, but the principles can apply to others.

### Step 3: Locate the Pivot Rod and Clevis

Underneath the sink, look for the horizontal pivot rod that extends into the drain pipe. This rod is usually connected to the vertical lift rod (the one you push and pull on top of the sink) by a metal strap known as a clevis. The clevis is typically secured to the lift rod by a spring clip or a small nut.

### Step 4: Disconnect the Clevis and Pivot Rod

Gently squeeze the spring clip to release the clevis from the vertical lift rod, then set the clevis aside. Next, use an adjustable wrench or pliers to carefully loosen the large nut (often called the pivot rod nut or retaining nut) that secures the horizontal pivot rod where it enters the drain pipe. Turn it counter-clockwise until it's loose enough to remove by hand.

### Step 5: Remove the Pivot Rod

Once the pivot rod nut is loose or removed, gently pull the pivot rod straight out from the drain pipe. Be prepared for a small amount of water to drip out, which is normal. With the pivot rod completely removed, the sink stopper inside the basin will now be free and disconnected from the plumbing.

### Step 6: Lift Out the Stopper

Return to the top of the sink basin. With the pivot rod disengaged, you can now simply grasp the sink stopper and lift it straight out of the drain opening. You've successfully removed it! This is the perfect time to clean the stopper and the drain area thoroughly, or retrieve any dropped items.

### Step 7: Reassemble the Stopper (Reverse Steps)

When you're ready to put it back, insert the stopper into the drain from the top. Under the sink, carefully reinsert the pivot rod into the drain pipe, ensuring it passes through the hole on the bottom of the stopper's shaft. Tighten the pivot rod nut, reattach the clevis to the vertical lift rod, and test the stopper's operation to ensure it moves freely and seals properly.


## Tips & Tricks

- Take a photo of the assembly under the sink before you start, especially if you're unsure about the reassembly process. This can be a helpful reference.
- While the stopper is out, use an old toothbrush and some cleaning solution to thoroughly scrub any accumulated hair, soap scum, or grime from the stopper and the inside of the drain opening. This is a prime spot for buildup.
- If you notice any mold or mildew around the sink basin or drain area, it's a good opportunity to clean it. For advice on general mold removal, you might find tips in guides like [How to Remove Mold from Walls Safely & Effectively](/en/how-to-remove-mold-from-walls).
- When reattaching the pivot rod nut, consider applying a small amount of plumber's tape to the threads to help ensure a watertight seal and prevent future leaks.
- If the pivot rod nut is difficult to loosen, a penetrating lubricant can help, but apply it sparingly and wipe away any excess immediately.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Always be gentle when using tools like wrenches or pliers on plumbing fixtures, especially if they are plastic. Overtightening or excessive force can strip threads or crack pipes.
- ⚠️ Wear gloves to protect your hands from grime, old water, and any cleaning chemicals you might use.
- ⚠️ While not typically necessary for just removing the stopper, if you plan to disconnect any other drain pipes (like the P-trap), always shut off the main water supply to the sink first.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why would I need to remove my sink stopper?**

People commonly remove sink stoppers to thoroughly clean accumulated hair and grime, retrieve dropped jewelry or small items, or to replace a broken or malfunctioning stopper that no longer holds water effectively.

**Can I remove a sink stopper without tools?**

Some simpler stoppers, like basic drain strainers or push-pull types, can often be removed by hand. However, most common lift-rod bathroom stoppers require an adjustable wrench or pliers to disconnect the pivot rod assembly underneath the sink.

**What if my sink stopper is stuck?**

If your stopper is stuck, it's usually due to excessive grime, hair, or mineral deposits. Try to gently wiggle it while pulling. For lift-rod types, ensure the pivot rod underneath is fully disengaged and removed before attempting to pull the stopper out from the top.

**How do I clean my sink stopper once it's removed?**

You can clean your sink stopper with an old toothbrush, dish soap, and hot water. For stubborn grime or mineral deposits, a vinegar soak or a mild bathroom cleaner can be effective. Always rinse it thoroughly before reinserting.

**What is the purpose of the pivot rod under the sink?**

The pivot rod is a horizontal rod that extends into the drain pipe and engages with a hole on the bottom of the stopper. When you move the vertical lift rod on top of the sink, it pushes or pulls the pivot rod, which in turn raises or lowers the stopper to open or close the drain.

**My kitchen sink has a different stopper. How do I remove that?**

Kitchen sinks often have simpler basket strainers that lift out directly, or push-pull stoppers that you simply pull up to remove. Some might have a garbage disposal flange stopper, which also typically lifts out without needing tools underneath. Check your specific model for the easiest method.

**Do I need to turn off the water to remove the stopper?**

No, you typically do not need to turn off the water supply to the sink just to remove the stopper itself. The process doesn't involve disconnecting water supply lines. However, if you plan on disconnecting any other drain pipes, turning off the water is a good safety precaution.
