Removing a sink stopper, particularly a pop-up type, is a straightforward task often needed for cleaning clogged drains, retrieving dropped items, or performing maintenance. It typically involves disconnecting a pivot rod beneath the sink and then lifting the stopper out from above. This process allows access to the drain for thorough cleaning and upkeep.

## What You Need

- Bucket or small container
- Pliers (slip-joint or channel-lock recommended)
- Adjustable wrench (optional, for stubborn nuts)
- Gloves (optional, for grime)
- Flashlight (optional)
- Old towel/rags
- Pipe cleaner or small brush

## Steps

### Step 1: Prepare Your Workspace

Before you begin, ensure you have enough space to work comfortably under the sink. Clear out any items from the cabinet and place an old towel or rags on the cabinet floor to catch any drips. Position a bucket or small container directly under the drainpipe's pivot rod to collect any residual water that might escape during the process.

### Step 2: Locate and Identify the Pivot Rod

Look directly under the sink, behind the drainpipe. You should see a horizontal metal rod, known as the pivot rod, that enters the drainpipe assembly. This rod is responsible for raising and lowering the sink stopper. It's usually held in place by a retaining nut and a spring clip or another securing mechanism.

### Step 3: Disconnect the Pivot Rod

With the bucket in place, loosen the retaining nut where the pivot rod enters the drainpipe. You can typically do this by hand, but pliers or an adjustable wrench might be needed if it's tight. Once the nut is loose, pull the pivot rod straight out from the drainpipe. Be prepared for a small amount of water to drip out when the rod is removed.

### Step 4: Remove the Sink Stopper

Now that the pivot rod is disconnected, the stopper inside the sink drain is free. From above the sink, simply grasp the top of the stopper and pull it straight up and out of the drain opening. It should lift out easily without resistance. If it feels stuck, gently twist it as you pull.

### Step 5: Clean the Stopper and Drain

Once the stopper is removed, you'll likely find a build-up of hair, soap scum, and other debris around the stopper itself and inside the drain opening. Use a paper towel or old rag to wipe away the grime from the stopper. For the drain opening, use a pipe cleaner or a small brush to reach down and clear out any blockages. If you notice any mold growth around the drain or on the underside of the sink, you might want to refer to our guide on [How to Remove Mold from Walls: A Complete Guide](/en/how-to-remove-mold-from-walls) for effective cleaning methods.

### Step 6: Reinstall the Stopper and Pivot Rod

To reinstall, first drop the clean stopper back into the drain opening from above. Make sure the hole on the side of the stopper faces towards the back of the sink, aligning with where the pivot rod will re-enter. From underneath, reinsert the pivot rod through the drainpipe and into the hole on the stopper. Secure the pivot rod by tightening the retaining nut, ensuring it's snug but not overtightened.

### Step 7: Test for Leaks and Operation

Run water into the sink and close the stopper to ensure it holds water properly. Watch closely under the sink for any signs of leaks around the pivot rod connection. If you see drips, gently tighten the retaining nut a little more. Also, test the lift rod (if applicable) to confirm the stopper moves up and down smoothly.


## Tips & Tricks

- Regularly removing and cleaning your sink stopper can prevent major clogs and unpleasant odors.
- Avoid overtightening the retaining nut, as this can damage the drainpipe threads or the nut itself.
- Use a flashlight when working under the sink to ensure clear visibility of all connections and components.
- Keep a small container of old rags or towels under your sink for quick clean-ups during plumbing tasks.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Always place a bucket or container under the drain assembly to catch any water that may escape when disconnecting pipes or rods.
- ⚠️ Do not use excessive force when loosening or tightening nuts, as this can strip threads or crack plastic components.
- ⚠️ If using any drain cleaning chemicals after removing the stopper, always follow product instructions carefully and ensure proper ventilation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why won't my sink stopper come out?**

Most pop-up sink stoppers are connected to a pivot rod under the sink. If it won't come out, ensure you've fully disconnected and pulled out this horizontal rod from the drainpipe assembly. Sometimes, hair and grime can also cause it to stick, requiring a gentle twist and pull.

**How do I clean a sink stopper?**

Once removed, you can easily clean a sink stopper with warm soapy water and an old brush or sponge. For stubborn grime or hair, a paper towel or pipe cleaner works well. Rinse it thoroughly before reinstalling.

**Do all sink stoppers remove the same way?**

No, not all sink stoppers remove the same way. The method described here is for pop-up stoppers with a pivot rod. Drop-in stoppers simply lift out, while others might involve twisting or have a different mechanism. Always identify your stopper type first.

**What if my sink stopper is stuck?**

If your sink stopper is stuck, first ensure the pivot rod is completely disconnected. If it still won't budge, a build-up of hair, soap scum, or mineral deposits might be holding it. Try wiggling and gently twisting it as you pull, or use a thin tool to clear debris around its base.

**How often should I remove and clean my sink stopper?**

It's a good practice to remove and clean your sink stopper every few months, or whenever you notice slow drainage or unpleasant odors. Regular cleaning helps prevent clogs and keeps your drain functioning efficiently.

**Can I replace my sink stopper with a different type?**

Yes, you can often replace your existing sink stopper with a different type, such as a universal push-and-seal stopper, as long as it fits your drain opening. Just ensure the new stopper is compatible with your sink's drain size and design.

**What is the purpose of the pivot rod?**

The pivot rod is a crucial component of a pop-up sink stopper system. It connects the lift rod (the knob or lever on your faucet) to the stopper itself, allowing you to raise and lower the stopper to open or close the drain without reaching into the water.
