Removing a sink stopper is a common task for cleaning clogs, retrieving dropped items, or replacing a broken stopper. The process typically involves disconnecting a pivot rod under the sink for pop-up stoppers, or simply twisting and lifting for other types.

## What You Need

- Bucket or small container
- Pliers or adjustable wrench
- Gloves (optional)
- Old rags or towels
- Flashlight (optional)
- Cleaning brush or old toothbrush
- All-purpose cleaner or vinegar

## Steps

### Step 1: Prepare Your Workspace

Before you begin, clear out any items from under the sink to give yourself ample room to work. Place a bucket or small container directly under the P-trap assembly to catch any water that might drain when you loosen connections. Lay down old towels or rags to protect the cabinet floor from spills and provide a comfortable kneeling surface.

> 💡 **Tip:** Wearing gloves can protect your hands from grime and cleaning solutions.

### Step 2: Identify Your Stopper Type

Sink stoppers come in several common varieties. For most bathroom sinks, you'll likely have a 'pop-up' stopper connected to a pivot rod. Kitchen sinks often have simpler basket strainers or disposal stoppers that just lift out. Some bathroom stoppers are 'push-pull' or 'lift-and-turn' types, which may only require twisting and lifting from above the drain.

> 💡 **Tip:** If your stopper simply lifts out or twists and pulls out, you can skip directly to step 5 for cleaning.

### Step 3: Locate the Pivot Rod (for Pop-Up Stoppers)

For pop-up stoppers, look directly under the sink, behind the P-trap. You should see a horizontal rod, known as the pivot rod, entering the drainpipe. This rod has a clevis strap attached to it, which connects to the pull-knob on top of your faucet. The pivot rod operates the stopper from below.

> 💡 **Tip:** Use a flashlight to get a better view of the connections in dimly lit cabinet spaces.

### Step 4: Disconnect the Pivot Rod

You'll see a large nut, called the pivot nut or coupling nut, where the pivot rod enters the drainpipe. Carefully loosen this nut using pliers or an adjustable wrench, turning it counter-clockwise. Once the nut is loose, gently pull the pivot rod straight out of the drainpipe. Be prepared for a small amount of water to leak out when the rod is removed.

> 💡 **Tip:** Keep the pivot nut and any washers or gaskets in a safe place so they don't get lost.

### Step 5: Remove the Stopper

With the pivot rod removed, the sink stopper above the drain is now free. Simply reach into the sink and lift the stopper straight up and out of the drain opening. Some stoppers might have a small ball at the bottom that the pivot rod connected to; ensure this comes out with the stopper.

> 💡 **Tip:** If the stopper is stuck, gently wiggle it or use a screwdriver to pry it up, being careful not to scratch the sink surface.

### Step 6: Clean and Inspect the Stopper and Drain

Now that the stopper is removed, thoroughly clean it and the drain opening. Use a cleaning brush, old toothbrush, and an all-purpose cleaner or a solution of vinegar and baking soda to remove hair, soap scum, and grime. This is also a good opportunity to inspect the stopper for damage and the drain for any signs of mold or blockages. If you notice persistent dampness or discoloration, you may want to investigate further. If you find any mold, learning [How to Remove Mold from Walls Safely and Effectively](/en/how-to-remove-mold-from-walls) can offer helpful principles for cleaning surrounding areas.

> 💡 **Tip:** Regularly cleaning your sink stopper can prevent slow drains and unpleasant odors.

### Step 7: Reinstall the Stopper (Optional)

To reinstall, simply reverse the steps. Insert the stopper back into the drain. Guide the pivot rod back into its opening, ensuring it passes through the hole in the stopper's ball joint. Tighten the pivot nut securely by hand, then a quarter turn with pliers, being careful not to overtighten. Test the stopper's operation and check for leaks by running water into the sink.

> 💡 **Tip:** Ensure the pivot rod is correctly aligned with the stopper's opening for proper function.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always place a bucket under the drain connections before loosening them to catch any residual water.
- Take a photo of the under-sink assembly before you start, which can be a helpful reference when reassembling.
- If your stopper is a simple lift-and-turn or push-pull type, you usually just twist the top counter-clockwise to unscrew it from the drain body, then lift it out.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Do not overtighten nuts and connections, as this can strip threads or crack plastic pipes.
- ⚠️ Avoid using harsh chemical drain cleaners, as they can damage pipes and are hazardous to handle. Try natural solutions first.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why is my sink stopper not holding water?**

If your sink stopper isn't holding water, it's likely due to a build-up of grime and hair around the stopper's seal or a misaligned pivot rod. Removing and cleaning the stopper and adjusting the pivot rod often resolves the issue.

**How do I remove a kitchen sink basket stopper?**

Most kitchen sink basket stoppers are designed to simply lift straight out of the drain opening. Some might have a small rubber gasket that creates a tight seal; a gentle twist while pulling up can help loosen it.

**What if my stopper doesn't have a visible pivot rod underneath?**

If your stopper doesn't have a pivot rod, it's likely a push-pull or lift-and-turn type. For these, try twisting the stopper counter-clockwise from above the drain while pulling up. It should unscrew or release from its housing.

**Can I replace a sink stopper without calling a plumber?**

Yes, removing and replacing most standard sink stoppers is a straightforward DIY task that doesn't require a plumber. You can purchase universal replacement stoppers at most hardware stores.

**How often should I clean my sink stopper?**

It's a good idea to remove and clean your sink stopper every few months, or whenever you notice slow drainage or unpleasant odors coming from the sink. Regular cleaning helps prevent clogs and keeps your drain fresh.

**What tools do I need to remove a sink stopper?**

For most pop-up stoppers, you'll need pliers or an adjustable wrench to loosen the pivot nut. For simpler stoppers, no tools are typically required, just your hands. A bucket and rags are always helpful for any potential drips.
