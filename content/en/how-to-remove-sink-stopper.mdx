Removing a sink stopper typically involves disconnecting the pivot rod assembly located under the sink, or simply unscrewing the stopper from above. The method depends on the type of stopper you have, such as a pop-up or a lift-and-turn model. This process is generally straightforward and can help resolve drainage issues or allow for thorough cleaning.

## What You Need

- Pliers (slip-joint or channel-lock)
- Bucket or small basin
- Old towel or rag
- Gloves (optional)
- Screwdriver (for some stoppers)
- Cleaning brush or old toothbrush
- All-purpose cleaner or white vinegar
- Flashlight (optional)

## Steps

### Step 1: Identify Your Sink Stopper Type

First, determine the type of stopper you have. Pop-up stoppers are controlled by a lever behind the faucet and require access to the plumbing underneath. Lift-and-turn or push-pop stoppers are operated directly at the drain and may unscrew or pull out from above without needing to go under the sink. Knowing your stopper type will guide the removal process.

### Step 2: Prepare the Workspace Under the Sink

If you have a pop-up stopper, clear out any items from the cabinet directly below the sink. Place a bucket or small basin directly under the P-trap to catch any residual water that might leak during the process. Lay down an old towel for extra protection against spills, ensuring the area is ready for work.

### Step 3: Disconnect the Pivot Rod (for Pop-Up Stoppers)

Locate the horizontal pivot rod extending from the drain pipe under the sink. You'll see a small nut or clip holding it in place. Loosen this nut with pliers or unscrew the clip by hand, then carefully pull the pivot rod out of the drain assembly. Be prepared for a small amount of water to drain out as the rod is removed.

### Step 4: Remove the Stopper from Above

Once the pivot rod is disconnected (for pop-up stoppers), you can simply pull the stopper straight up and out of the drain from the sink basin. For lift-and-turn stoppers, try turning them counter-clockwise to unscrew them. Push-pop stoppers often just pull straight out or unscrew after pushing them down, so try these methods first if you don't have a pop-up lever.

### Step 5: Clean the Stopper and Drain

With the stopper removed, clean any accumulated hair, soap scum, or debris from the stopper itself and the inside of the drain opening. A cleaning brush or old toothbrush works well with an all-purpose cleaner or white vinegar to thoroughly scrub away grime. If you notice any signs of mold in the damp areas around the drain or under the sink, it's a good opportunity to address it, perhaps by consulting resources like [How to Remove Mold from Walls Safely and Effectively](/en/how-to-remove-mold-from-walls).

### Step 6: Inspect and Maintain Components

While the stopper is out, take a moment to inspect the rubber seals or gaskets on the stopper and pivot rod for any signs of wear, cracking, or damage. Replacing worn seals can prevent future leaks and ensure the stopper functions correctly. This quick check can save you trouble down the line.

### Step 7: Reinstall the Stopper

Carefully guide the stopper back into the drain. For pop-up stoppers, ensure the hole on the stopper aligns with where the pivot rod will re-enter. Reinsert the pivot rod through the drain assembly and the stopper hole, then tighten the nut or reattach the clip securely. Test the stopper's operation by filling the sink with water and checking for leaks under the basin.


## Tips & Tricks

- Take a photo of the under-sink assembly before you start, especially if you're unsure about reassembly.
- Wear gloves to protect your hands from grime and potential bacteria during cleaning.
- Have a flashlight handy to better see components and connections under the sink, especially in dimly lit cabinets.
- If the nut on the pivot rod is stubborn, a small amount of penetrating oil can help loosen it.
- Regularly cleaning your sink stopper and drain can prevent clogs and unpleasant odors from building up.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Always be gentle when removing and reinstalling components to avoid overtightening or stripping threads, which can lead to leaks or damage.
- ⚠️ Ensure the bucket or basin is properly positioned to catch any water during removal, preventing potential water damage to your cabinet or floor.
- ⚠️ Avoid using excessive force on old or corroded plumbing components, as they might break and cause a larger issue.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why would I need to remove my sink stopper?**

You might need to remove your sink stopper to clean out accumulated hair and grime, which often causes slow drains. It's also necessary if you've dropped something valuable down the drain or if the stopper itself is broken and needs replacement.

**What are the main types of sink stoppers?**

The most common types are pop-up stoppers, which are controlled by a lever behind the faucet, and lift-and-turn or push-pop stoppers, which are operated directly by hand at the drain opening. Each type has a slightly different removal method.

**Do I need special tools to remove a sink stopper?**

For most pop-up stoppers, you'll need pliers (slip-joint or channel-lock) to loosen the nut on the pivot rod. Lift-and-turn stoppers might require a screwdriver or no tools at all, simply unscrewing by hand. Basic household tools are usually sufficient.

**What should I do if the pivot rod nut is stuck?**

If the pivot rod nut is stuck, try applying a small amount of penetrating oil or lubricant and letting it sit for a few minutes. You can also try gently tapping the wrench or pliers with a rubber mallet to help loosen it, but be careful not to damage the pipe.

**How often should I clean my sink stopper?**

It's a good practice to clean your sink stopper every few months, or whenever you notice the drain slowing down or an unpleasant odor emanating from the drain. Regular cleaning prevents significant build-up of hair and soap scum.

**Can I remove a sink stopper without going under the sink?**

Some stoppers, like lift-and-turn or push-pop styles, can often be removed by simply unscrewing or pulling them directly from the drain opening. However, pop-up stoppers controlled by a lever typically require access to the pivot rod assembly under the sink for proper removal.

**What if my sink still drains slowly after removing and cleaning the stopper?**

If your sink still drains slowly after cleaning the stopper, the clog is likely further down the drainpipe beyond the stopper assembly. In this case, you may need to use a drain snake, a chemical drain cleaner (with caution), or consider calling a plumber for more extensive clogs.
