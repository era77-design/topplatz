To delete your Facebook account, log in, navigate to Settings & Privacy > Settings > Your Facebook Information > Deactivation and Deletion. Choose "Delete Account," confirm your decision, and be aware of the 30-day grace period before permanent deletion.

## What You Need

- Computer or mobile device with internet access
- Your Facebook login credentials (email/phone and password)

## Steps

### Step 1: Log In to Your Facebook Account

Access Facebook using a web browser on your computer or the Facebook app on your mobile device. Enter your registered email address or phone number and password to log in securely. Ensure you are logged into the specific account you intend to delete before proceeding.

> 💡 **Tip:** If you've forgotten your password, use the 'Forgot password?' link to reset it before attempting deletion.

### Step 2: Access Settings & Privacy

Once logged in, click or tap on your profile picture or the down arrow icon in the top right corner (on desktop) or the menu icon (three horizontal lines) in the bottom right (on mobile app). From the dropdown menu that appears, select "Settings & Privacy," then proceed to click on "Settings."

### Step 3: Navigate to 'Your Facebook Information'

In the Settings menu, locate the "Your Facebook Information" option. On a desktop, this is typically found in the left-hand sidebar. If you're on a mobile device, you may need to scroll down to find this section. Click on this to reveal various data management options related to your account.

### Step 4: Select 'Deactivation and Deletion'

Within the "Your Facebook Information" section, you will find an option labeled "Deactivation and Deletion." Click on this to open a new page. Facebook will then present you with two distinct choices: "Deactivate Account" (temporary) or "Delete Account" (permanent).

### Step 5: Choose 'Delete Account' and Continue

Carefully select the "Delete Account" option, as this action is permanent and irreversible after a set grace period. After making your selection, click on the "Continue to Account Deletion" button to move forward. Facebook may offer you a last chance to download your information or consider deactivation as an alternative.

### Step 6: Confirm Your Deletion

Facebook will prompt you to re-enter your password to confirm your identity and ensure that you are the legitimate account owner initiating this request. After securely entering your password, click "Continue" or "Submit." You will then be presented with a final confirmation screen, outlining what will happen.

### Step 7: Understand the Grace Period

Upon confirming deletion, your account is scheduled for permanent removal, but it is not deleted instantly. Facebook typically provides a 30-day grace period during which your account is deactivated and hidden. If you log back into your account at any point within this 30-day window, your deletion request will be automatically canceled, and your account will be fully restored. After 30 days, your account and all associated data will be permanently deleted and cannot be recovered.


## Tips & Tricks

- **Download Your Data**: Before initiating deletion, visit the 'Download Your Information' section under 'Your Facebook Information' to save a copy of your photos, posts, and messages.
- **Review Connected Apps**: Check which third-party apps and websites you've logged into using your Facebook account, as deleting your account might affect your access to these services.
- **Inform Your Network**: If you wish to stay in touch, consider notifying close friends and family about your departure from Facebook and share alternative contact methods.
- **Consider Deactivation First**: If you're uncertain about permanent deletion, deactivating your account is a reversible option that hides your profile while retaining all your data for future reactivation.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ **Permanent and Irreversible**: Once your account is permanently deleted after the 30-day grace period, it cannot be recovered. All your posts, photos, videos, and other data will be permanently removed.
- ⚠️ **Loss of Messenger Access**: Deleting your Facebook account will also delete your Messenger account, meaning you will no longer be able to use Messenger linked to that profile.
- ⚠️ **Impact on Linked Accounts**: Any apps, websites, or services where you used 'Log in with Facebook' will lose that connection. You may need to create new logins or update existing ones for these services.
- ⚠️ **Deletion Timeframe**: While your account is immediately hidden upon confirmation, it can take up to 90 days for all your data to be completely removed from Facebook's backup systems.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What's the difference between deactivating and deleting a Facebook account?**

Deactivating temporarily hides your profile, posts, and information from others, making it seem like you've left, but you can reactivate it anytime. Deleting is a permanent action that removes your account and all its data after a grace period, making it unrecoverable.

**Can I recover my Facebook account after I've deleted it?**

You have a 30-day grace period after initiating deletion. If you log back into your account within these 30 days, your deletion request will be canceled, and your account will be restored. After this period, deletion is permanent and cannot be reversed.

**What happens to my photos, posts, and messages after deletion?**

Once your account is permanently deleted after the grace period, all your content, including photos, posts, videos, and messages, will be removed from Facebook and become unrecoverable. It's highly advisable to download your data beforehand if you wish to keep it.

**Does deleting my Facebook account also delete my Messenger account?**

Yes, deleting your Facebook account will also delete your Messenger account. You will no longer be able to send or receive messages through Messenger linked to that specific Facebook profile.

**What happens to apps and websites I logged into using Facebook?**

Deleting your Facebook account will sever the 'Log in with Facebook' connection for any third-party apps or websites. You may lose access to these services or need to create new accounts or update your login methods for them.

**How long does it take for my account to be permanently deleted from Facebook's servers?**

After you initiate the deletion process, your account is scheduled for permanent deletion, with a 30-day deactivation grace period. After these 30 days, it can take up to 90 days for all your data to be completely removed from Facebook's backup systems.

**Can someone else delete my Facebook account for me if I pass away?**

Facebook has a 'Legacy Contact' feature where you can designate someone to manage your memorialized account or request its deletion after you pass away. Without a legacy contact, a verified request from a close family member or executor may be required, often with proof of death.
