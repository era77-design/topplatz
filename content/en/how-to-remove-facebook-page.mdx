Removing a Facebook page can be done through your page's settings. You can choose to unpublish it temporarily or initiate a permanent deletion, which comes with a 14-day grace period for reconsideration.

## What You Need

- Facebook Account with Admin Access to the Page
- Internet Connection
- Web Browser or Facebook App

## Steps

### Step 1: Access Your Facebook Page

Start by logging into your Facebook account that has admin access to the page you wish to remove. From your News Feed, navigate to your page. You can usually find your pages listed in the left sidebar under 'Pages' or by using the search bar. Ensure you are interacting with the page as the page itself, not your personal profile.

### Step 2: Navigate to Page Settings

Once on your page, look for the 'Settings' option. This is typically found in the left-hand menu when viewing your page on a desktop browser. Click on 'Settings' to open the page configuration options. If using the mobile app, you might need to tap the gear icon or 'More' to find settings.

> 💡 **Tip:** On the mobile app, settings might be under the 'More' tab (three dots) or a gear icon.

### Step 3: Find the 'Remove Page' Section

In the Page Settings menu, scroll down or look for a section related to 'General' settings. Within this section, you should find an option labeled 'Remove Page' or 'Delete Page'. This option is usually located towards the bottom of the general settings list.

### Step 4: Confirm Permanent Deletion

Click on the 'Remove Page' or 'Delete Page' option. Facebook will then present you with a confirmation prompt, explaining that deleting the page is permanent. You will typically be asked to confirm your decision by clicking 'Delete [Page Name]' again. Be absolutely sure before proceeding, as this action cannot be immediately undone.

### Step 5: Understand the 14-Day Grace Period

After confirming deletion, your page will not be immediately removed from Facebook. Instead, it enters a 14-day grace period. During this time, the page will be unpublished and invisible to others, but you can still cancel the deletion process by revisiting the page and clicking 'Cancel Deletion' in the settings. If no action is taken within 14 days, the page will be permanently removed.


## Tips & Tricks

- Before deleting, download any photos, videos, or posts you wish to keep by going to 'Page Settings' > 'General' > 'Download Page'.
- Consider unpublishing your page first if you're unsure about permanent deletion. This makes it invisible to the public but keeps all content intact.
- If you have other admins, ensure they are aware of the deletion plan or remove them beforehand if you prefer.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Deleting a Facebook page is a permanent action after the 14-day grace period. All content, posts, photos, and followers will be irretrievably lost.
- ⚠️ Only a Facebook page admin can initiate the deletion process. If you are not an admin, you cannot delete the page.
- ⚠️ Make sure you are deleting the correct page to avoid accidental loss of important content or communities.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How to remove an admin from a Facebook page?**

To remove an admin, go to your Page Settings, then 'Page Access' or 'Page Roles'. Find the admin you wish to remove, click the three dots next to their name, and select 'Remove access' or 'Remove Role'. You may need to enter your password to confirm.

**How to remove admin from Facebook page if I'm the only one?**

If you are the only admin on a Facebook page, you cannot remove yourself without first assigning another person admin access. Facebook requires at least one active admin for a page. If you wish to leave the page, you must either delete the page entirely or assign another admin.

**What's the difference between unpublishing and deleting a Facebook page?**

Unpublishing a page makes it invisible to the public, but all its content and data remain intact, and you can republish it at any time. Deleting a page is a permanent action that removes all content, followers, and data after a 14-day grace period, making it unrecoverable.

**What happens after I delete my Facebook page?**

After you initiate deletion, your page becomes unpublished and invisible for 14 days. During this period, you can cancel the deletion. If you don't, the page, along with all its posts, photos, videos, and followers, will be permanently removed from Facebook and cannot be recovered.

**Can I recover a deleted Facebook page?**

You can only recover a Facebook page during the 14-day grace period after you initiate its deletion. After this period, the deletion becomes permanent, and the page, along with all its content, cannot be recovered.

**Who can delete a Facebook page?**

Only a Facebook user with full administrative access (an admin) to the page can initiate the deletion process. Editors, moderators, advertisers, or analysts do not have the necessary permissions to delete a page.

**Will my personal profile be affected if I delete a Facebook page?**

Deleting a Facebook page will not affect your personal Facebook profile. Your profile and any other pages or groups you manage will remain active and unchanged. The deletion only applies to the specific page you choose to remove.
