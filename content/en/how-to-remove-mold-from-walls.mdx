Removing surface mold from walls typically involves cleaning with a bleach solution or vinegar, followed by proper drying and addressing the moisture source. Always wear protective gear and ensure good ventilation during the process.

## What You Need

- Protective gloves
- Safety goggles
- N95 face mask
- Bucket
- Spray bottle
- Stiff brush or scrub sponge
- Microfiber cloths or old rags
- White vinegar
- Household bleach (optional, for non-porous surfaces)
- Water
- Fan or dehumidifier
- Drop cloth or old towels

## Steps

### Step 1: Prepare the Area and Yourself

Before you begin, ensure the area is well-ventilated by opening windows and using fans. Lay down a drop cloth or old towels to protect the floor from cleaning solutions and falling mold spores. Most importantly, don your protective gear: gloves, safety goggles, and an N95 mask to prevent inhaling spores or skin contact with mold or cleaners.

### Step 2: Identify the Mold and Its Source

Determine if the mold is surface-level or indicates a deeper issue. Small, superficial patches can often be handled yourself. If you suspect the mold is black mold or covers a large area (more than 10 square feet), or if you can't identify the moisture source, it's best to consult a professional. Addressing the underlying moisture problem (e.g., a leaky pipe, high humidity) is crucial to prevent recurrence. For more serious cases, refer to [How to Get Rid of Black Mold Safely and Effectively](/en/how-to-get-rid-of-black-mold).

### Step 3: Choose Your Cleaning Solution

For porous surfaces like painted drywall, a white vinegar solution is often preferred. Mix equal parts white vinegar and water in a spray bottle. For non-porous surfaces like tile or sealed concrete, a bleach solution can be effective: mix one cup of household bleach with one gallon of water. Never mix bleach with ammonia or other cleaners, as this can create dangerous fumes. Test your chosen solution on an inconspicuous area first to ensure it doesn't damage or discolor the wall.

### Step 4: Apply the Solution to the Mold

Generously spray or apply your chosen cleaning solution directly onto the moldy area. Ensure the mold is thoroughly saturated. Allow the solution to sit on the mold for at least 10-15 minutes. This dwell time allows the cleaner to penetrate and kill the mold spores effectively.

### Step 5: Scrub and Wipe Away the Mold

Using a stiff brush or a scrub sponge, gently scrub the moldy area to loosen and remove the mold. Once the mold appears to be gone, wipe the area clean with a damp microfiber cloth or old rag. Avoid scrubbing dry mold, as this can release spores into the air. If necessary, repeat the application and scrubbing steps until all visible mold is removed.

### Step 6: Rinse and Dry Thoroughly

After removing the mold, lightly rinse the area with clean water using a damp cloth. It's critical to dry the wall thoroughly to prevent mold from returning. Use clean, dry cloths to blot the area, then use fans or a dehumidifier to ensure the wall is completely dry. Good air circulation is key during this stage.

### Step 7: Prevent Future Mold Growth

Once the wall is dry, take steps to prevent future mold growth. This includes fixing any leaks, improving ventilation in humid areas like bathrooms and kitchens, and maintaining indoor humidity levels below 60%. Regular cleaning and prompt attention to moisture can keep your walls mold-free.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always test cleaning solutions on an inconspicuous area first to ensure they don't damage or discolor your wall.
- For persistent odors after cleaning, an enzymatic cleaner designed for mold can help break down residual spores and smells.
- Consider using mold-resistant paint in areas prone to dampness to add an extra layer of protection against future growth.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Never mix bleach with ammonia or any other household cleaners, as this can create toxic and potentially lethal fumes.
- ⚠️ Always wear protective gear, including gloves, safety goggles, and an N95 mask, to avoid contact with mold spores or cleaning chemicals.
- ⚠️ If mold covers a large area (more than 10 square feet), or if you suspect it's black mold or a result of sewage or contaminated water, it is best to consult a professional mold remediation specialist.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How to remove mold from painted walls?**

To remove mold from painted walls, opt for a milder cleaning solution like a 50/50 white vinegar and water mixture. Spray it on, let it sit for 10-15 minutes, then gently scrub with a soft brush or sponge. Always test the solution on an inconspicuous area first to ensure it doesn't strip the paint.

**How to remove mold from inside walls?**

Removing mold from inside walls is a more complex issue, typically indicating a significant moisture problem behind the surface. This usually requires opening up the wall to properly assess and remediate the mold and fix the source of moisture. It's often best to consult a professional mold remediation specialist for this type of extensive mold growth.

**What kills mold on walls permanently?**

No single product kills mold permanently without addressing the underlying cause. While bleach or vinegar kill mold spores, preventing recurrence requires eliminating the moisture source, improving ventilation, and controlling humidity levels. Without these steps, mold is likely to return.

**Is it safe to remove mold from walls yourself?**

It is generally safe to remove small patches of surface mold (less than 10 square feet) from walls yourself, provided you wear appropriate protective gear (gloves, mask, goggles) and ensure good ventilation. For larger infestations, black mold, or mold resulting from contaminated water, professional help is recommended.

**Can I use bleach to remove mold from walls?**

Yes, bleach can be used to remove mold from non-porous walls, such as tiled surfaces. Mix one cup of bleach with one gallon of water, apply it, let it sit, and then scrub. However, for porous surfaces like drywall, vinegar is often preferred as it can penetrate better and is less harsh on materials.

**How do I prevent mold from growing back on walls?**

To prevent mold from regrowing, address the root cause of moisture. Fix any leaks, ensure proper ventilation in bathrooms and kitchens, use exhaust fans, and maintain indoor humidity levels below 60% with a dehumidifier if needed. Regularly clean and inspect walls for early signs of moisture or mold.

**What are the signs of mold on walls?**

Signs of mold on walls include visible fuzzy or slimy patches, which can be black, green, white, or orange. You might also notice a musty, earthy odor, even if the mold isn't visible. Peeling paint, discoloration, or water stains can also indicate hidden mold growth behind the wall surface.
