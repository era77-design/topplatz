To remove mold from walls, start by identifying the source of moisture and addressing it. Then, prepare the area and use a mild cleaning solution like distilled white vinegar or a bleach solution (for non-porous surfaces) to scrub and disinfect the affected area, ensuring proper ventilation throughout the process.

## What You Need

- Rubber gloves
- N95 respirator mask
- Eye protection
- Spray bottle
- Two buckets
- Clean cloths or sponges
- Stiff brush (non-metallic)
- Distilled white vinegar
- Household bleach (optional, for non-porous surfaces)
- Dish soap (optional, for pre-cleaning)
- Fan or dehumidifier
- Old towels or plastic sheeting

## Steps

### Step 1: 1. Prioritize Safety and Ventilation

Before you begin, ensure the area is well-ventilated by opening windows and doors, or by using fans to circulate air. Always wear personal protective equipment, including rubber gloves, an N95 respirator mask, and eye protection, to prevent contact with mold spores and cleaning solutions.

> 💡 **Tip:** If the mold growth is extensive, consider sealing off the work area with plastic sheeting to prevent spores from spreading to other parts of your home.

### Step 2: 2. Identify and Address the Moisture Source

Mold cannot grow without moisture. The most crucial step in mold removal is to find and fix the source of the moisture problem, whether it's a leaky pipe, a roof leak, or excessive condensation. If the moisture source isn't resolved, the mold will inevitably return.

> 💡 **Tip:** Check for condensation on walls, especially in bathrooms and kitchens, and address any ventilation issues.

### Step 3: 3. Prepare the Area

Remove any furniture, rugs, or decorative items from the affected area. Cover the floor with old towels or plastic sheeting to protect it from cleaning solutions and falling mold spores. Gently scrape off any loose, dry mold with a stiff brush into a disposable bag, taking care not to spread the spores.

### Step 4: 4. Choose Your Cleaning Solution

For most mold on walls, particularly painted walls, distilled white vinegar is an effective and safer option. Spray undiluted white vinegar directly onto the moldy area and let it sit for at least an hour. For non-porous surfaces, a diluted bleach solution (1 cup bleach to 1 gallon water) can be used, but always test in an inconspicuous spot first, as bleach can discolor paint or wallpaper. For persistent or extensive black mold, consult [How to Get Rid of Black Mold Safely and Effectively](/en/how-to-get-rid-of-black-mold) or a professional.

> 💡 **Tip:** Never mix bleach with ammonia or other household cleaners, as this can create toxic fumes.

### Step 5: 5. Apply and Scrub

After the chosen solution has had time to penetrate the mold, use a stiff brush or a clean sponge to scrub the mold from the wall. Apply gentle but firm pressure, working in small sections. For heavily soiled areas, you might need to reapply the solution and scrub again.

> 💡 **Tip:** Use a separate bucket of clean water and a fresh cloth for rinsing to avoid spreading mold spores and contaminating your cleaning solution.

### Step 6: 6. Rinse and Dry Thoroughly

Once the mold is removed, wipe down the wall with a clean, damp cloth to remove any remaining cleaning solution or mold residue. The most critical step is to ensure the wall dries completely. Use fans, open windows, or a dehumidifier to speed up the drying process. The area must be bone dry before you consider any repairs or repainting.

> 💡 **Tip:** Allow the area to air dry for at least 24-48 hours, or until completely dry to the touch, to prevent immediate recurrence.

### Step 7: 7. Prevent Recurrence

To prevent mold from returning, maintain good ventilation in your home, especially in high-moisture areas like bathrooms and kitchens. Use exhaust fans, fix leaks promptly, and consider using a dehumidifier to keep indoor humidity levels below 60%. Regular cleaning and inspection can also help catch new growth early.

> 💡 **Tip:** Consider applying a mold-inhibiting primer before repainting walls in areas prone to moisture.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always test your cleaning solution in an inconspicuous area of the wall first to ensure it doesn't cause discoloration or damage.
- Do not attempt to scrub dry mold vigorously, as this can release a large number of spores into the air, potentially worsening the problem and posing health risks.
- For porous materials like drywall or insulation with extensive mold growth, removal and replacement are often the safest and most effective options, as mold can penetrate deep into these materials.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Never mix bleach with ammonia, vinegar, or other household cleaners, as this can create dangerous and toxic fumes.
- ⚠️ If the mold covers an area larger than 10 square feet (roughly 3 feet by 3 feet), or if you suspect mold is behind walls or in HVAC systems, it is best to consult a professional mold remediation specialist.
- ⚠️ Individuals with allergies, asthma, or compromised immune systems should avoid mold cleanup and should consult a medical professional if mold exposure is suspected.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How to remove mold from painted walls?**

For painted walls, a solution of distilled white vinegar is often preferred over bleach, as bleach can strip paint or discolor it. Spray the vinegar directly onto the mold, let it sit for an hour, then scrub gently with a soft brush or sponge and rinse thoroughly before drying completely.

**What kills mold on walls?**

Distilled white vinegar is a highly effective natural fungicide that kills most mold species on both porous and non-porous surfaces. For non-porous surfaces, a diluted bleach solution (1 cup bleach to 1 gallon water) can also be used, but always test in an inconspicuous area first.

**How do you prevent mold from growing back on walls?**

Prevention is key. Address any moisture sources immediately, maintain indoor humidity levels below 60% with a dehumidifier, ensure good ventilation in bathrooms and kitchens, and clean up spills promptly. Regularly inspect walls for signs of moisture or new mold growth.

**When should I call a professional for mold removal?**

You should call a professional if the mold covers an area larger than 10 square feet, if you suspect mold is growing behind walls or in HVAC systems, or if anyone in the household has severe mold allergies or respiratory issues. Professionals have specialized equipment for safe and thorough removal.

**How to remove mold from inside walls?**

Removing mold from inside walls is generally a job for professionals due to the need to open up walls, properly contain spores, and ensure complete removal and drying. Attempting this yourself without proper equipment and knowledge can spread spores throughout your home and worsen the problem, potentially requiring costly structural repairs.

**Can I just paint over mold?**

No, you should never paint over mold. Painting over mold will not kill it; the mold will continue to grow beneath the paint, eventually reappearing and potentially causing more extensive damage to the wall structure and air quality. Always remove mold completely and address the moisture source before repainting.

**Is it safe to remove mold yourself?**

Yes, for small areas (less than 10 square feet) and surface mold, it is generally safe to remove yourself with proper safety precautions like wearing gloves, an N95 mask, and eye protection, and ensuring good ventilation. For larger or persistent infestations, professional help is recommended to ensure complete and safe remediation.
