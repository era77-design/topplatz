To remove mold from walls, start by identifying the source of moisture and addressing it. Then, don appropriate safety gear, prepare a cleaning solution like diluted bleach or vinegar, and gently scrub the affected area. Ensure proper ventilation during and after cleaning to prevent recurrence.

## What You Need

- Rubber gloves
- Safety goggles
- N95 respirator mask
- Spray bottle
- Stiff-bristled scrub brush or sponge
- Two buckets
- Cleaning solution (diluted bleach or white vinegar)
- Water
- Old towels or rags
- Fan or dehumidifier (optional)

## Steps

### Step 1: Identify and Address Moisture Source

Before you begin cleaning, it's vital to find and fix the source of moisture that allowed the mold to grow. Common culprits include leaky pipes, condensation, or poor ventilation. Repairing the leak or improving airflow will prevent mold from returning after your cleaning efforts.

> 💡 **Tip:** Consider using a hygrometer to monitor indoor humidity levels, aiming for below 60%.

### Step 2: Gather Safety Gear and Ventilate

Mold spores can be irritating or harmful, so protect yourself. Don rubber gloves, safety goggles, and an N95 respirator mask. Open windows and doors to ensure good ventilation in the work area, or use a fan to direct air outdoors to minimize spore inhalation.

### Step 3: Prepare Your Cleaning Solution

For non-porous surfaces like painted walls, a solution of 1 cup bleach to 1 gallon of water is effective. Alternatively, for a natural approach, mix equal parts white vinegar and water in a spray bottle. Avoid mixing bleach and vinegar, as this creates toxic fumes. If you're dealing with extensive black mold, it's important to understand the risks and potentially consult a professional. For more detailed information, see [How to Get Rid of Black Mold Safely and Effectively](/en/how-to-get-rid-of-black-mold).

### Step 4: Pre-Clean the Area (Optional)

Before applying the main cleaning solution, you can gently wipe down the visible mold with a damp cloth to remove loose spores. Be careful not to scrub too hard, as this can release more spores into the air. Dispose of the cloth immediately after use to prevent spreading.

### Step 5: Apply and Scrub the Solution

Spray your chosen cleaning solution directly onto the moldy area, ensuring it's thoroughly saturated. Let it sit for 10-15 minutes to allow the solution to penetrate and kill the mold. Then, use a stiff-bristled scrub brush or sponge to gently scrub away the mold. For painted walls, be mindful not to damage the paint.

> 💡 **Tip:** Work in small sections to prevent the solution from drying out before you can scrub it effectively.

### Step 6: Rinse and Dry Thoroughly

After scrubbing, wipe the wall with a clean, damp cloth to remove any residue from the cleaning solution and dead mold spores. It's crucial to dry the area completely afterward. Use old towels, a fan, or a dehumidifier to speed up the drying process and prevent mold from regrowing, as moisture is its primary fuel.

### Step 7: Prevent Future Mold Growth

To prevent mold from returning, maintain good ventilation in your home, especially in bathrooms and kitchens. Fix any leaks promptly, and consider using mold-resistant paint in damp areas. Keep indoor humidity levels below 60% using dehumidifiers if necessary to create an environment unfavorable for mold growth.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always address the moisture source first to prevent mold recurrence.
- Wear appropriate personal protective equipment, including gloves, goggles, and an N95 mask, to protect against mold spores and cleaning chemicals.
- Ensure the area is well-ventilated during and after cleaning by opening windows or using fans.
- Avoid mixing bleach with ammonia or vinegar, as this creates dangerous toxic fumes.
- For extensive mold growth or mold inside walls, consider consulting a professional mold remediation specialist.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Never mix bleach with ammonia, vinegar, or other household cleaners, as this can create dangerous and toxic gases.
- ⚠️ If you experience respiratory issues, dizziness, or skin irritation while cleaning mold, stop immediately and seek fresh air. Consult a doctor if symptoms persist.
- ⚠️ Do not attempt to clean large areas of mold (more than 10 square feet) yourself, as this may require professional remediation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do you remove mold from walls?**

To remove mold from walls, first fix any moisture issues. Then, wearing protective gear, apply a cleaning solution like diluted bleach or vinegar, scrub the mold away, rinse, and thoroughly dry the area to prevent recurrence.

**How do you remove mold from painted walls?**

Removing mold from painted walls involves gently scrubbing with a mild solution like diluted bleach or vinegar. Be careful not to damage the paint, and always ensure the wall is completely dry afterward to prevent the mold from returning.

**What kills mold on walls permanently?**

Killing mold on walls permanently requires addressing the underlying moisture source. While bleach or vinegar solutions kill surface mold, preventing its return depends on fixing leaks, improving ventilation, and maintaining low humidity levels.

**When should I worry about mold on walls?**

You should worry about mold on walls if it covers a large area (more than 10 square feet), if you or household members experience unexplained health issues, or if the mold keeps returning after cleaning. In such cases, professional assessment is recommended.

**How do you remove mold from inside walls?**

Removing mold from inside walls typically requires professional intervention, as it often involves opening up the wall cavity to properly clean, dry, and treat the affected structural materials. Attempting to clean hidden mold without addressing the source can lead to further damage and health risks.

**Can I paint over mold on walls?**

No, you should never paint over mold on walls. Painting over mold will not kill it; the mold will continue to grow beneath the paint, potentially causing further damage and releasing spores. Always clean and thoroughly dry the moldy area before repainting.

**What is the best homemade solution to kill mold on walls?**

A popular and effective homemade solution for killing mold on non-porous walls is a 50/50 mixture of white vinegar and water. For more stubborn mold on non-porous surfaces, a solution of one cup of bleach per gallon of water also works well.
