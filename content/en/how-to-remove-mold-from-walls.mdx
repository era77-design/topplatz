To remove mold from walls, first ensure proper ventilation and wear protective gear. Then, clean the affected area with a diluted bleach solution or white vinegar, scrubbing gently before rinsing and drying thoroughly.

## What You Need

- Rubber gloves
- N95 mask or respirator
- Eye protection (goggles)
- Old clothes
- Spray bottle
- Stiff-bristled scrub brush or sponge
- Two buckets
- Clean towels or rags
- White vinegar
- Household bleach (optional)
- Water

## Steps

### Step 1: Prepare the Area and Yourself

Before you begin, ensure the room is well-ventilated by opening windows and using fans. Put on your protective gear, including rubber gloves, an N95 mask or respirator, and eye protection. Lay down old sheets or plastic tarps to protect the floor and furniture from mold spores and cleaning solutions.

### Step 2: Identify the Cause and Choose Your Solution

Mold grows in damp environments, so identifying and fixing the moisture source is crucial for long-term prevention. For cleaning, you can use a solution of one part bleach to three parts water, or undiluted white vinegar for a less harsh option. Vinegar is often preferred for porous surfaces or if you prefer to avoid bleach fumes.

### Step 3: Apply the Cleaning Solution

Carefully spray or wipe your chosen cleaning solution onto the moldy area. If using bleach, ensure good ventilation. Allow the solution to sit for 10-15 minutes to penetrate the mold and kill the spores. Do not rinse immediately, as the solution needs time to work effectively.

### Step 4: Scrub Away the Mold

Using a stiff-bristled scrub brush or sponge, gently but firmly scrub the mold from the wall. Work in small sections to ensure thorough cleaning. For stubborn spots, reapply the solution and let it sit for a few more minutes before scrubbing again. Be careful not to damage painted surfaces by scrubbing too aggressively.

### Step 5: Rinse and Dry Thoroughly

Once the mold is removed, use a clean cloth dampened with plain water to wipe down the cleaned area, rinsing away any residue from the cleaning solution. Immediately dry the wall thoroughly with clean towels or by using fans. Ensuring the wall is completely dry prevents immediate re-growth of mold.

### Step 6: Dispose of Materials and Prevent Recurrence

Bag and dispose of all contaminated materials like sponges, rags, and tarps. Clean your protective gear. To prevent future mold, fix any leaks, reduce indoor humidity (ideally below 60%), and ensure good ventilation, especially in bathrooms and kitchens. Regularly inspect areas prone to moisture.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always test your cleaning solution on an inconspicuous area of the wall first to ensure it doesn't damage or discolor the surface.
- For small, superficial mold growth, a simple mixture of dish soap and water can sometimes be effective, followed by thorough drying.
- Consider running a dehumidifier in humid areas to keep moisture levels low and discourage mold growth.
- Never mix bleach with ammonia or other household cleaners, as this can create dangerous toxic fumes.
- If you're dealing with a large mold infestation (larger than 10 square feet) or suspect black mold, it's best to call a professional. For more on this, see [How to Get Rid of Black Mold Safely and Effectively](/en/how-to-get-rid-of-black-mold).

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Always wear protective gear (gloves, mask, eye protection) to prevent contact with mold spores and cleaning chemicals.
- ⚠️ Ensure adequate ventilation when working with bleach or strong cleaning solutions.
- ⚠️ Do not scrape or sand dry mold, as this can release a high concentration of spores into the air.
- ⚠️ If you experience respiratory issues, skin irritation, or other adverse reactions, stop immediately and seek fresh air.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How to remove mold from painted walls?**

To remove mold from painted walls, use a mild solution like diluted white vinegar or a gentle bleach solution (1 part bleach to 10 parts water). Apply with a soft sponge, let it sit for 10-15 minutes, then gently scrub and rinse. Always test a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure the paint isn't damaged or discolored.

**How to remove mold from inside walls?**

Removing mold from inside walls typically requires professional intervention, as it indicates a significant moisture problem and potentially extensive hidden mold growth. This often involves opening the wall to identify the source of moisture, remove affected materials, and properly dry the area to prevent recurrence.

**What is the best way to remove mold from walls?**

The best way depends on the severity and type of mold. For small, superficial areas, a solution of white vinegar or diluted bleach is effective. Ensure proper ventilation, wear protective gear, and thoroughly dry the area afterward to prevent recurrence. Addressing the underlying moisture source is crucial.

**Is it safe to remove mold from walls yourself?**

Yes, for small areas of mold (less than 10 square feet), it is generally safe to remove yourself with proper precautions. Always wear an N95 mask, gloves, and eye protection, and ensure good ventilation. For larger infestations or if you have health concerns, consult a professional.

**How can I prevent mold from returning to my walls?**

To prevent mold from returning, control humidity levels in your home, ideally below 60%, using dehumidifiers if necessary. Promptly fix any leaks in pipes, roofs, or foundations. Ensure good ventilation in bathrooms and kitchens, and immediately dry any wet surfaces or materials.

**When should I call a professional for mold removal?**

You should call a professional for mold removal if the mold covers an area larger than 10 square feet, if you suspect mold is growing behind walls or in HVAC systems, or if you or family members experience health issues related to mold exposure. Professionals have the expertise and equipment to safely remediate large infestations.
