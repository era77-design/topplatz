To remove mold from walls, begin by ensuring proper ventilation and wearing protective gear. Use a suitable cleaning solution like diluted bleach or vinegar to scrub the affected area, then rinse and thoroughly dry the surface to prevent the mold from returning.

## What You Need

- Safety goggles
- Rubber gloves
- N95 mask
- Spray bottle
- Stiff scrub brush or sponge
- Bucket
- Clean cloths or towels
- Household bleach (or white vinegar)
- Water
- Fan or dehumidifier (optional)

## Steps

### Step 1: Prepare Your Workspace and Yourself

Before you begin, open windows and doors to ensure adequate ventilation in the room. This helps disperse mold spores and fumes from cleaning agents. Don your protective gear, including safety goggles, rubber gloves, and an N95 mask, to shield yourself from mold spores and chemical irritants. Move any furniture or items away from the wall to create a clear working area.

### Step 2: Identify the Mold and Its Source

Assess the extent and type of mold growth. For small, superficial mold patches, you can likely handle it yourself. However, if you suspect extensive mold growth behind walls, a large area is affected (more than 10 square feet), or if you see [How to Get Rid of Black Mold Safely and Effectively](/en/how-to-get-rid-of-black-mold), it's best to consult a professional. Identifying and addressing the moisture source (e.g., a leak, condensation) is crucial to prevent future mold growth.

### Step 3: Choose and Prepare Your Cleaning Solution

For non-porous surfaces like painted walls, a bleach solution is effective. Mix one cup of household bleach with one gallon of water in a bucket. Alternatively, for a natural approach or if you prefer to avoid bleach, use undiluted white vinegar in a spray bottle. Never mix bleach with ammonia or other cleaners, as this can create dangerous toxic fumes.

### Step 4: Apply and Scrub the Mold Away

Dip your scrub brush or sponge into the bleach solution, or spray the vinegar directly onto the moldy area. Allow the solution to sit for 5-10 minutes to penetrate the mold. Vigorously scrub the affected area to loosen and remove the mold. Be thorough, ensuring you cover the entire mold-affected surface.

### Step 5: Rinse and Dry the Wall Thoroughly

After scrubbing, use a clean cloth dampened with plain water to wipe down the cleaned area, removing any residual cleaning solution and dead mold spores. It's critical to dry the wall completely to prevent mold from returning. Use clean, dry towels, or if possible, use a fan or dehumidifier to speed up the drying process. Ensure the wall is bone dry before proceeding or repainting.

### Step 6: Dispose of Materials and Clean Up

Carefully place all used cloths, sponges, and disposable masks into a sealed plastic bag. Dispose of this bag immediately in an outdoor trash can to prevent the spread of mold spores. Clean any reusable tools with a disinfectant solution. Wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water after removing your gloves and mask.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always test your cleaning solution on an inconspicuous area of the wall first to ensure it doesn't damage or discolor the paint.
- For stubborn mold stains, a paste made of baking soda and water can be applied, allowed to dry, then scrubbed away.
- Consider using mold-resistant paint in areas prone to moisture, such as bathrooms and basements, to help deter future growth.
- Regularly check areas prone to mold, like behind furniture or in damp corners, to catch new growth early.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Never mix bleach with ammonia, vinegar, or any other cleaning products, as this can create highly toxic and dangerous fumes.
- ⚠️ Ensure the room is well-ventilated during and after cleaning to minimize exposure to mold spores and cleaning fumes.
- ⚠️ If you experience respiratory issues, dizziness, or eye irritation while cleaning mold, stop immediately and seek fresh air.
- ⚠️ For extensive mold growth, especially if it covers more than 10 square feet or appears to be black mold, consult a professional mold remediation specialist.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do you remove mold from painted walls?**

To remove mold from painted walls, first ensure good ventilation and wear protective gear. Apply a solution of 1 cup bleach to 1 gallon of water, or undiluted white vinegar, to the affected area. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes, then scrub with a stiff brush, rinse with clean water, and dry thoroughly.

**What kills mold on walls permanently?**

While bleach and vinegar effectively kill surface mold, preventing its return permanently requires addressing the underlying moisture issue. Fix leaks, reduce humidity with ventilation or dehumidifiers, and ensure proper airflow to keep walls dry and inhospitable to mold growth.

**When should I call a professional for mold removal?**

You should call a professional for mold removal if the mold covers an area larger than 10 square feet, if you suspect it's black mold, or if it's growing behind walls and requires demolition to access. Professionals have the expertise and equipment to safely and effectively remove large or hazardous mold infestations.

**Can I remove mold from inside walls myself?**

Removing mold from inside walls is generally not recommended for DIY. This usually indicates a significant moisture problem requiring professional assessment and remediation. Opening walls without proper containment can release a large number of mold spores into your home, posing health risks.

**How do I prevent mold from returning to my walls?**

To prevent mold from returning, identify and fix any sources of moisture, such as leaky pipes or condensation. Improve ventilation in damp areas like bathrooms and kitchens, use a dehumidifier if humidity is high, and ensure consistent air circulation to keep wall surfaces dry.

**Is it safe to remove mold yourself?**

Yes, it is generally safe to remove small patches of surface mold (less than 10 square feet) yourself, provided you take proper safety precautions. Always wear an N95 mask, gloves, and eye protection, and ensure the area is well-ventilated to minimize exposure to spores and cleaning fumes.

**What's the best cleaner for mold on walls?**

For non-porous surfaces like painted walls, a solution of 1 part bleach to 10 parts water is highly effective for killing mold. For a non-toxic alternative, undiluted white vinegar also works well. Always choose the cleaner appropriate for your wall material and wear protective gear.
