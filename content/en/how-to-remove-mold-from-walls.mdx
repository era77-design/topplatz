Removing mold from walls typically involves identifying the source of moisture, preparing the area with protective gear, and cleaning the affected surface with appropriate solutions like bleach or vinegar. Ensure proper ventilation throughout the process to prevent spore spread and protect your health.

## What You Need

- Safety goggles
- Rubber gloves
- N95 respirator mask
- Old clothes
- Plastic sheeting or tarp
- Bucket
- Stiff-bristled brush or scrub sponge
- Spray bottle
- White vinegar
- Household bleach (optional, for non-porous surfaces)
- Water
- Microfiber cloths
- Fan or dehumidifier

## Steps

### Step 1: Identify the Source & Assess Damage

Before you begin cleaning, it's crucial to locate and fix the underlying moisture problem that is causing the mold growth. This could be a leaky pipe, a poorly sealed window, or high humidity. If the moisture source isn't addressed, the mold will likely return. Assess the extent of the mold; if it covers a large area or appears to be deep-seated, professional help might be necessary.

### Step 2: Prepare the Area & Protect Yourself

Clear any furniture or items away from the affected wall. Lay down plastic sheeting or a tarp on the floor to catch any falling mold spores or cleaning solutions, making cleanup easier. Don your personal protective equipment, including safety goggles, rubber gloves, and an N95 respirator mask, to safeguard your eyes, skin, and respiratory system from mold spores and cleaning chemicals.

### Step 3: Ventilate the Room Thoroughly

Open windows and doors (to the outside) in the affected room to ensure maximum air circulation. You can also use fans to help direct airflow outwards, which aids in dispersing mold spores and chemical fumes. Close doors to other interior rooms to prevent mold spores from spreading to unaffected areas of your home.

### Step 4: Choose Your Cleaning Solution

For non-porous surfaces like painted walls, a solution of 1 cup household bleach mixed with 1 gallon of water can be effective. Alternatively, a natural and effective option for various surfaces is undiluted white vinegar in a spray bottle. Always test your chosen solution on an inconspicuous spot first to ensure it doesn't damage or discolor the wall surface.

### Step 5: Apply and Scrub the Mold

Spray your chosen cleaning solution directly onto the moldy area, making sure to saturate it completely. Allow the solution to sit for 10-15 minutes to penetrate and kill the mold. Using a stiff-bristled brush or a scrub sponge, firmly scrub the mold away from the wall. For stubborn spots, you may need to reapply the solution and scrub again.

### Step 6: Rinse and Dry Completely

After scrubbing, wipe down the wall with clean, damp microfiber cloths to remove any remaining mold residue or cleaning solution. It is absolutely vital to dry the wall surface thoroughly to prevent immediate mold regrowth. Use fans, open windows, or a dehumidifier to accelerate the drying process, ensuring no moisture remains.

### Step 7: Prevent Future Mold Growth

Once the wall is completely dry, consider applying a mold-inhibiting primer before repainting, especially if the area is prone to moisture. Regularly inspect your home for leaks, maintain indoor humidity levels below 60%, and ensure proper ventilation in high-moisture areas like bathrooms and kitchens to prevent mold from returning.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always test cleaning solutions on a small, hidden area of the wall first to check for discoloration or damage.
- For extensive mold growth (larger than 10 square feet) or if you suspect mold behind walls, consider consulting a professional mold remediation specialist.
- Improve ventilation in bathrooms and kitchens by using exhaust fans during and after showering or cooking.
- Never paint over mold without cleaning it first, as this will only hide the problem and not stop its growth.
- Consider using a dehumidifier in damp basements or crawl spaces to control moisture levels.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Never mix bleach with ammonia or other household cleaners, as this can create dangerous toxic fumes.
- ⚠️ Always wear appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) including an N95 respirator, gloves, and eye protection when cleaning mold.
- ⚠️ If you have allergies, asthma, or a compromised immune system, avoid direct contact with mold and consider having someone else clean it or hiring a professional.
- ⚠️ If the mold covers an area larger than 10 square feet or is suspected to be 'black mold,' it's advisable to call a professional. For more information, see [How to Get Rid of Black Mold Safely and Effectively](/en/how-to-get-rid-of-black-mold).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How to remove mold from painted walls?**

For painted walls, mild solutions like a 1:1 white vinegar and water mix or a diluted bleach solution (1 cup bleach per gallon of water) are generally safe. Always test a small, hidden area first to ensure the paint isn't damaged or discolored. Gently scrub with a soft brush or sponge to avoid stripping the paint.

**How to remove mold from inside walls?**

Removing mold from inside walls typically requires opening up the wall to access the affected area and fix the moisture source. This is a more complex task that often warrants professional mold remediation, especially if the mold is extensive. Attempting to clean it without proper containment can spread spores and cause further damage.

**What kills mold on walls permanently?**

The key to permanent mold removal is not just killing the mold, but also addressing and fixing the underlying moisture source that allowed it to grow. While bleach and vinegar can kill mold spores, if the damp conditions persist, the mold will almost certainly return. Ensure proper ventilation, fix all leaks, and control humidity levels.

**Can I paint over mold on walls?**

No, you should never paint over mold. Painting over mold will not kill it; the mold will continue to grow beneath the paint, eventually causing the paint to peel, crack, and the mold to reappear. Always thoroughly clean and dry the moldy area completely before applying any primer or paint.

**When should I call a professional for mold removal?**

You should call a professional if the mold covers an area larger than 10 square feet, if you suspect mold is hidden behind walls or within HVAC systems, or if you or family members have health concerns like allergies or respiratory issues. Professionals have specialized equipment and expertise to safely contain and remove large infestations.

**Is it safe to remove mold yourself?**

For small areas of surface mold (less than 10 square feet), it is generally safe to remove yourself using proper protective equipment and ensuring good ventilation. However, for larger areas, suspected 'black mold', or if you have health sensitivities, professional remediation is highly recommended to ensure safety and effectiveness. For more details on this, refer to [How to Get Rid of Black Mold Safely and Effectively](/en/how-to-get-rid-of-black-mold).

**What is the best solution to remove mold from walls?**

The 'best' solution depends on the wall surface and severity. White vinegar is excellent for porous surfaces and a natural, non-toxic option. A diluted household bleach solution (1 cup bleach to 1 gallon water) is highly effective on non-porous surfaces. For severe cases or specific types of mold, specialized commercial mold removers are also available.
