Removing mold from walls typically involves carefully cleaning the affected area with a mold-killing solution, scrubbing away the growth, and ensuring the surface is thoroughly dried to prevent recurrence. Always wear appropriate protective gear to prevent exposure to mold spores during the cleaning process.

## What You Need

- N95 mask
- Rubber gloves
- Safety glasses
- Old clothes or long sleeves/pants
- Scrub brush or sturdy sponge
- Spray bottle
- Bucket
- White vinegar
- Borax
- Dish soap (optional)
- Clean cloths or old towels
- Fan or dehumidifier

## Steps

### Step 1: Prepare the Area and Gather Supplies

Before you begin, clear the area around the moldy wall. Remove any furniture, rugs, or decorations to prevent them from getting contaminated or damaged by mold spores or cleaning solutions. Ensure you have all your protective gear and cleaning solutions readily available.

> 💡 **Tip:** Open windows and doors to ensure good ventilation during the entire cleaning process. This helps disperse mold spores and fumes from cleaning solutions.

### Step 2: Don Your Protective Gear

Mold spores can be harmful when inhaled or come into contact with skin and eyes. Always wear an N95 mask, rubber gloves, and safety glasses to protect yourself from exposure. Wearing old clothes, long sleeves, and pants is also recommended to minimize skin contact with mold and cleaning agents.

### Step 3: Choose and Prepare Your Cleaning Solution

For most mold, a mixture of white vinegar and water (50/50 ratio) in a spray bottle works effectively. Alternatively, you can use a solution of 1 cup of borax dissolved in 1 gallon of hot water, with a few drops of dish soap. Avoid using bleach on porous surfaces like drywall, as it may not kill the mold's roots and can damage the material.

### Step 4: Apply the Solution and Let it Sit

Generously spray or wipe your chosen cleaning solution onto the moldy area, ensuring the entire affected surface is thoroughly saturated. Allow the solution to sit for at least 10-15 minutes. This gives the active ingredients sufficient time to penetrate and kill the mold spores and roots.

### Step 5: Scrub Away the Mold

Using a scrub brush or a sturdy sponge, firmly scrub the mold off the wall. Apply consistent pressure to effectively remove the mold without damaging the wall surface. For stubborn or deeply embedded mold, you may need to reapply the solution and scrub again until the mold is completely gone.

### Step 6: Rinse and Thoroughly Dry the Area

After scrubbing, wipe the area with a clean, damp cloth to remove any remaining mold residue and cleaning solution. It is crucial to thoroughly dry the wall to prevent mold from returning. Use a fan or dehumidifier, and keep windows open to promote maximum airflow, ensuring the wall dries completely within 24-48 hours.

### Step 7: Address the Root Cause of Mold Growth

Removing the mold is only a temporary fix if the underlying moisture problem isn't addressed. Identify and repair any leaks, reduce humidity levels in the affected area, and improve overall ventilation. This crucial step prevents future mold growth and ensures a long-term solution.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always test your chosen cleaning solution on an inconspicuous spot of the wall first to ensure it doesn't cause discoloration or damage.
- For extensive mold growth (larger than 10 square feet) or if you suspect mold behind walls, consider consulting a professional mold remediation specialist.
- Maintain good ventilation in your home, especially in bathrooms and kitchens, by using exhaust fans and opening windows to reduce humidity and prevent mold.
- If you're dealing with what appears to be black mold, refer to our specific guide on [How to Get Rid of Black Mold Safely and Effectively](/en/how-to-get-rid-of-black-mold) for more detailed instructions and precautions.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Never mix bleach with ammonia or any other household cleaners, as this can create toxic and potentially lethal fumes.
- ⚠️ If you experience respiratory issues, skin irritation, or other adverse reactions while cleaning mold, stop immediately and seek fresh air or medical attention.
- ⚠️ Do not attempt to clean mold if you have a compromised immune system, severe respiratory conditions, or known mold allergies; consult a professional.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I remove mold from walls safely?**

To safely remove mold, always wear protective gear including an N95 mask, rubber gloves, and safety glasses. Use a natural cleaning solution like white vinegar or borax, ensure good ventilation in the area, and dry the surface thoroughly afterward.

**How do you remove mold from painted walls?**

Removing mold from painted walls typically involves gently scrubbing with a mild solution of white vinegar and water or borax and water. Be careful not to scrub too aggressively to avoid damaging the paint, and always test the solution on an inconspicuous spot first to check for discoloration.

**What kills mold on walls instantly?**

While no solution 'instantly' kills all mold, a strong concentration of white vinegar or a borax solution can effectively kill mold spores on contact over a short period. It's crucial to let the solution sit for 10-15 minutes to penetrate thoroughly before scrubbing.

**How to remove mold from inside walls?**

Removing mold from inside walls is more complex and often requires opening up the wall to access the affected area. This usually indicates a significant underlying moisture problem, and it's highly recommended to consult a professional mold remediation specialist for proper assessment and safe removal.

**Can I use bleach to remove mold from walls?**

While bleach can kill mold on non-porous surfaces, it's generally not recommended for porous walls like drywall. Bleach may not penetrate deep enough to kill the mold roots, and its corrosive nature can damage the wall material and release harsh fumes.

**How can I prevent mold from returning after cleaning?**

To prevent mold from returning, address the underlying moisture source immediately, whether it's a leak or high humidity. Improve ventilation, use dehumidifiers in damp areas, and ensure surfaces are kept dry, especially in bathrooms and kitchens, to inhibit mold growth.

**When should I call a professional for mold removal?**

You should call a professional if the mold covers an area larger than 10 square feet, if you suspect mold behind walls, or if you have health concerns like allergies or respiratory issues that make personal cleaning unsafe. Professionals have the expertise and equipment to safely handle extensive mold infestations.
