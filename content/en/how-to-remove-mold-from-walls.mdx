To remove mold from walls, always start by ensuring proper ventilation and wearing protective gear like gloves, a mask, and safety goggles. For small areas, a simple solution of white vinegar or baking soda and water can effectively kill and remove mold spores. Always address the underlying moisture issue to prevent recurrence.

## What You Need

- Rubber gloves
- N95 mask or respirator
- Safety goggles
- Spray bottle
- White distilled vinegar
- Baking soda
- Stiff-bristled scrub brush (non-metallic)
- Clean cloths or sponges
- Bucket
- Fan or dehumidifier
- Plastic sheeting or old towels

## Steps

### Step 1: Prepare the Area and Yourself

Before you begin, ensure the room is well-ventilated by opening windows and using a fan. Lay down plastic sheeting or old towels to protect the floor and furniture from mold spores and cleaning solutions. Put on your rubber gloves, N95 mask or respirator, and safety goggles to protect yourself from mold spores and cleaning agents. This is crucial for your health and safety during the cleaning process.

### Step 2: Isolate the Mold Area (if possible)

If the mold is in a contained area, consider temporarily sealing off the room by closing doors and blocking air vents. This helps prevent spores from spreading to other parts of your home during cleaning. Use painter's tape and plastic sheeting for a more effective, temporary barrier if needed. This step is especially important if you have allergies or respiratory sensitivities.

### Step 3: Choose Your Cleaning Solution

For non-porous surfaces like painted walls, white distilled vinegar is highly effective. Pour undiluted white vinegar into a spray bottle. Alternatively, you can create a paste with baking soda and water (1/4 cup baking soda to a small amount of water) for a gentle abrasive scrub. Avoid using bleach on porous surfaces or mixing it with other cleaners.

### Step 4: Apply the Solution to the Mold

Spray the undiluted white vinegar directly onto the moldy area, ensuring it's thoroughly saturated. If using the baking soda paste, apply a generous layer over the mold. Allow the solution to sit for at least 10-15 minutes; this gives the vinegar time to penetrate and kill the mold spores. For tougher spots, you might let it sit longer.

### Step 5: Scrub and Clean the Wall

Using a stiff-bristled scrub brush or an old sponge, scrub the mold away from the wall. Apply firm but gentle pressure to avoid damaging the wall surface. For painted walls, be careful not to scrub too aggressively, which could remove paint. Dispose of any heavily soiled cloths or sponges immediately after use in a sealed bag.

### Step 6: Rinse and Dry Thoroughly

Once the mold is removed, wipe down the area with a clean, damp cloth to remove any residue from the cleaning solution and remaining spores. It's essential to dry the wall completely to prevent mold from returning. Use a fan or a dehumidifier to speed up the drying process. Ensure the wall is bone dry before considering any repainting.

### Step 7: Address the Moisture Source

Cleaning the mold is only a temporary fix if the moisture source remains. Identify and fix any leaks, condensation issues, or high humidity levels that contributed to the mold growth. This might involve repairing plumbing, improving ventilation, or using a dehumidifier. Preventing future moisture is key to long-term mold prevention.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always test your cleaning solution on an inconspicuous area of the wall first to ensure it doesn't cause discoloration or damage.
- Never paint over mold without thoroughly cleaning and drying the area first; the mold will continue to grow underneath.
- Improve air circulation in your home, especially in damp areas like bathrooms and kitchens, to reduce humidity.
- For extensive mold growth or if you suspect black mold, consider contacting a professional. For more details, see [How to Get Rid of Black Mold Safely and Effectively](/en/how-to-get-rid-of-black-mold).

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Never mix bleach with vinegar or ammonia-based cleaners, as this can create dangerous toxic fumes.
- ⚠️ Always wear appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) including gloves, an N95 mask, and safety goggles.
- ⚠️ If you have a large area of mold (more than 10 square feet) or suspect mold behind walls, it's best to consult a professional mold remediation specialist.
- ⚠️ If you experience respiratory issues, headaches, or dizziness while cleaning mold, stop immediately and seek fresh air. Consult a doctor if symptoms persist.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How to remove mold from walls?**

To remove mold from walls, wear protective gear, ensure ventilation, and apply a solution like undiluted white vinegar or a baking soda paste. Scrub the area, then rinse and thoroughly dry the wall. Always address the underlying moisture problem to prevent recurrence.

**How to remove mold from painted walls?**

Removing mold from painted walls involves similar steps but requires gentler scrubbing to avoid damaging the paint. Use undiluted white vinegar in a spray bottle, let it sit, then gently scrub with a soft brush or sponge. Rinse and dry completely, and test in an inconspicuous area first.

**What kills mold on walls permanently?**

While cleaning solutions like vinegar kill mold, permanent removal requires addressing the moisture source that allowed it to grow. Fix leaks, reduce humidity, and ensure good ventilation. Without eliminating the water source, mold is likely to return.

**Is it safe to remove mold myself?**

It is generally safe to remove small patches of mold (less than 10 square feet) yourself, provided you wear proper protective gear and have good ventilation. For larger infestations, or if you have health concerns, it's safer to hire a professional mold remediation service.

**How do I prevent mold from returning after cleaning?**

To prevent mold from returning, identify and fix the source of moisture, whether it's a leak, condensation, or high humidity. Improve ventilation, use a dehumidifier in damp areas, and ensure surfaces are kept dry. Regular cleaning and monitoring can also help.

**How to remove mold from inside walls?**

Removing mold from inside walls typically requires professional remediation. This often involves opening up walls to assess the extent of the damage, safely remove contaminated materials, and address the hidden moisture source. Attempting this yourself without proper equipment and expertise can be risky and ineffective.

**Can I use bleach to remove mold from walls?**

While bleach can kill surface mold on non-porous materials, it's often not recommended for porous surfaces like drywall as it doesn't penetrate to kill the roots. Undiluted white vinegar is a safer and often more effective alternative for killing mold on walls, without the harsh fumes or risk of discoloration.
