To remove mold from walls, start by identifying the source of moisture and fixing it. Then, thoroughly clean the affected area using a mild bleach solution or vinegar, ensuring proper ventilation and protective gear. Finish by drying the wall completely to prevent recurrence.

## What You Need

- Gloves (rubber or nitrile)
- Safety goggles
- N95 respirator mask
- Bucket
- Spray bottle
- Stiff brush or scrub sponge
- Microfiber cloths or old rags
- White vinegar
- Household bleach (optional, for non-porous surfaces)
- Water
- Fan or dehumidifier

## Steps

### Step 1: Prepare the Area and Yourself

Before tackling the mold, ensure the area is well-ventilated by opening windows and using a fan. Move furniture away from the wall to give yourself ample space. Don protective gear, including gloves, safety goggles, and an N95 respirator mask, to prevent contact with mold spores and cleaning solutions.

> 💡 **Tip:** Isolate the area if possible by closing doors to other rooms to prevent mold spores from spreading.

### Step 2: Identify and Fix the Moisture Source

Mold thrives on moisture, so removing it permanently means addressing the underlying cause. Look for leaky pipes, condensation, or poor ventilation that might be contributing to the dampness. Repair any leaks, improve airflow, or use a dehumidifier to control humidity levels.

> 💡 **Tip:** Without fixing the moisture source, mold will almost certainly return.

### Step 3: Choose Your Cleaning Solution

For most surface mold on non-porous walls, a solution of one part bleach to three parts water is effective. For a less harsh, natural alternative, or for use on painted walls where bleach might cause discoloration, undiluted white vinegar in a spray bottle is an excellent choice. Never mix bleach and vinegar, as this creates toxic fumes.

> 💡 **Tip:** Always test your chosen solution on an inconspicuous spot first to ensure it doesn't damage or discolor the wall surface.

### Step 4: Apply the Cleaning Solution

Lightly mist the moldy area with your chosen solution – either the bleach solution or undiluted white vinegar. Allow the solution to sit for 10-15 minutes to penetrate and kill the mold spores. Avoid scrubbing dry mold, as this can release spores into the air and spread them.

> 💡 **Tip:** For stubborn mold, you might need to reapply the solution and allow it to sit for a longer period before scrubbing.

### Step 5: Scrub and Wipe Away the Mold

Using a stiff brush or scrub sponge, gently scrub the mold from the wall. Apply light pressure to avoid damaging the surface. Once the mold is loosened, wipe the area clean with a damp microfiber cloth. Rinse the cloth frequently in a separate bucket of clean water, ensuring you don't over-saturate the wall, especially if it's drywall.

> 💡 **Tip:** Dispose of any cloths or sponges used for cleaning mold in a sealed bag to prevent spreading spores.

### Step 6: Dry the Area Thoroughly

After cleaning, it's crucial to dry the wall completely to prevent immediate mold regrowth. Use clean, dry cloths to blot away excess moisture. You can also use a fan or a dehumidifier to speed up the drying process. Ensure the wall is bone dry before proceeding with any repairs or repainting.

> 💡 **Tip:** Proper drying is as important as cleaning for long-term mold prevention; even slight dampness can encourage regrowth.

### Step 7: Inspect and Prevent Recurrence

Once the wall is dry, inspect the area carefully to ensure all mold has been removed and there are no lingering stains. Continue to monitor the area for any signs of regrowth. Maintain good ventilation, manage indoor humidity, and promptly address any new moisture issues to keep your walls mold-free.

> 💡 **Tip:** Consider applying a mold-inhibiting primer before repainting walls in areas prone to dampness.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always wear protective gear, including gloves, goggles, and an N95 mask, to prevent exposure to mold spores and cleaning chemicals.
- Ensure adequate ventilation by opening windows and using fans during and after the cleaning process.
- Never paint over mold without thoroughly cleaning and drying the surface first, as this will not kill the mold and it will eventually reappear.
- For persistent or widespread mold issues, especially those covering more than 10 square feet, consider consulting a professional mold remediation specialist.
- Address the source of moisture immediately; otherwise, the mold will likely return quickly.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Never mix bleach with ammonia or other household cleaners, as this can create dangerous, toxic fumes.
- ⚠️ If you suspect the mold is black mold or covers a large area (more than 10 square feet), it's best to consult a professional mold remediation service. See [How to Get Rid of Black Mold Safely and Effectively](/en/how-to-get-rid-of-black-mold) for more information on identifying and safely addressing black mold.
- ⚠️ Avoid over-saturating drywall with liquid, as it can damage the material and create a larger problem.
- ⚠️ If mold is inside the walls, more extensive remediation may be required, potentially involving opening up the wall.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do you remove mold from painted walls?**

To remove mold from painted walls, use a mild cleaning solution like undiluted white vinegar in a spray bottle. Spray the affected area, let it sit for 10-15 minutes, then gently scrub with a soft brush or sponge. Wipe clean with a damp cloth and dry thoroughly to avoid damaging the paint.

**What is the best way to remove mold from walls?**

The best way involves identifying and fixing the moisture source, then cleaning with either a bleach solution (1 part bleach to 3 parts water) for non-porous surfaces or white vinegar for a natural alternative. Always wear protective gear and ensure good ventilation during the process.

**How do you remove mold from inside walls?**

Removing mold from inside walls typically requires opening up the wall to access the affected area and properly remediate it. This is often a job for a professional mold remediation specialist, as it can indicate a significant moisture problem and widespread hidden mold.

**Can I paint over mold on walls?**

No, you should never paint over mold. Painting over mold will not kill it; the mold will continue to grow underneath the paint and eventually reappear, potentially causing further damage and health issues. Always clean and dry the wall thoroughly before painting or making any repairs.

**How can I prevent mold from returning to my walls?**

To prevent mold recurrence, diligently control moisture levels in your home. This includes fixing leaks promptly, ensuring good ventilation, especially in bathrooms and kitchens, and using a dehumidifier in humid environments to keep indoor humidity below 60%.

**When should I call a professional for mold removal?**

You should call a professional mold remediation service if the mold covers a large area (more than 10 square feet), if it's black mold, or if you suspect it's deep within your walls or HVAC system. Professionals have the expertise and specialized equipment to safely and effectively handle extensive mold issues.
