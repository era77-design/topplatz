To remove mold from walls, start by identifying the mold type and ensuring proper ventilation. Then, prepare a cleaning solution, such as diluted bleach or vinegar, and carefully scrub the affected area. Always wear protective gear to prevent exposure.

## What You Need

- Safety goggles
- Rubber gloves
- N95 mask
- Scrub brush or sponge
- Spray bottle
- White vinegar
- Household bleach (optional)
- Bucket
- Clean cloths or towels
- Fan or dehumidifier

## Steps

### Step 1: Prioritize Safety and Ventilation

Before tackling mold, don protective gear including safety goggles, rubber gloves, and an N95 mask to prevent inhaling spores or skin contact. Open windows and doors to ensure adequate ventilation, or use a fan to circulate air out of the room. This minimizes exposure and helps dry the area after cleaning.

### Step 2: Identify the Mold Type and Extent

Visually inspect the mold to determine its color and spread. Most surface mold can be cleaned with household solutions. If you suspect black mold, or if the mold covers a large area (more than 10 square feet), consider professional help. For more serious cases, refer to [How to Get Rid of Black Mold Safely and Effectively](/en/how-to-get-rid-of-black-mold).

> 💡 **Tip:** Small patches of mold often appear as fuzzy, discolored spots in damp areas. Always address the underlying moisture issue.

### Step 3: Prepare Your Cleaning Solution

For non-porous surfaces like painted walls, a solution of one part bleach to three parts water is effective. Alternatively, for a natural approach, use undiluted white vinegar in a spray bottle. For tougher mold, a mixture of 1 cup borax and 1 gallon hot water can also be used.

### Step 4: Apply the Solution and Let it Sit

Spray or apply your chosen cleaning solution directly onto the moldy area, ensuring it's thoroughly saturated. Allow the solution to sit for 10-15 minutes. This gives the active ingredients time to penetrate and kill the mold spores, making it easier to scrub away.

### Step 5: Scrub Away the Mold

Using a stiff-bristled scrub brush or a rough sponge, vigorously scrub the mold from the wall. Apply consistent pressure to lift the mold from the surface. For painted walls, be mindful not to scrub so hard that you damage the paint finish.

### Step 6: Rinse and Dry Thoroughly

After scrubbing, use a clean cloth dampened with plain water to wipe down the cleaned area, removing any remaining mold residue and cleaning solution. It's crucial to dry the wall completely to prevent immediate re-growth. Use a fan, open windows, or a dehumidifier to speed up the drying process.

> 💡 **Tip:** Ensure the wall is bone dry before considering repainting or reapplying any finishes.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always test your cleaning solution on an inconspicuous area of the wall first to ensure it doesn't cause discoloration or damage.
- To prevent future mold growth, address the source of moisture immediately, such as a leaky pipe, condensation, or high humidity.
- Consider using mold-resistant paint in areas prone to dampness, like bathrooms or basements, for added protection.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Never mix bleach with ammonia or other household cleaners, as this can create dangerous toxic fumes.
- ⚠️ If mold covers a large area (more than 10 square feet) or you suspect it's structural, consult a professional mold remediation specialist.
- ⚠️ Do not paint over mold without cleaning it first, as it will continue to grow underneath the paint and potentially spread.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do you remove mold from painted walls?**

Removing mold from painted walls involves gently scrubbing with a mild solution like diluted white vinegar or a bleach-and-water mix. Be careful not to damage the paint finish by scrubbing too aggressively, and always rinse and dry the area thoroughly afterward.

**What kills mold on walls permanently?**

To permanently kill mold on walls, you must eliminate its moisture source. After cleaning the visible mold with solutions like bleach or vinegar, ensure the area remains dry and well-ventilated to prevent re-growth. Addressing leaks or humidity issues is key to preventing its return.

**Can I clean mold off a wall myself?**

Yes, for small areas of surface mold (less than 10 square feet), you can typically clean it yourself using appropriate safety gear and common household cleaning solutions. However, for larger infestations, black mold, or mold inside walls, professional remediation is recommended.

**How do you remove mold from inside walls?**

Removing mold from inside walls often requires opening up the wall to access the affected area. This is a more complex task that may involve removing drywall, cleaning the studs, and addressing the moisture source, usually best handled by a professional mold remediation specialist.

**What is the best homemade solution to clean mold off walls?**

A highly effective homemade solution for cleaning mold off walls is a mix of one part household bleach to three parts water for non-porous surfaces. For a natural alternative, undiluted white vinegar sprayed directly onto the mold also works well.

**When should I call a professional for mold removal?**

You should call a professional for mold removal if the mold covers an area larger than 10 square feet, if you suspect it's black mold, if it's growing inside walls, or if you experience persistent mold-related health issues. Professionals have the tools and expertise for safe and thorough remediation.
