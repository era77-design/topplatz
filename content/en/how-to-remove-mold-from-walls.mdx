Mold on walls is a common issue caused by excess moisture, but removing it is a manageable DIY task for small areas. The process involves identifying the mold, preparing the area for cleaning, applying an appropriate mold-killing solution, and ensuring the wall dries completely to prevent recurrence. Addressing the source of moisture is critical for long-term prevention.

## What You Need

- Safety goggles
- Rubber gloves
- N95 respirator mask
- Spray bottle
- Stiff-bristled scrub brush or old toothbrush
- Clean cloths or sponges
- Bucket
- White vinegar
- Borax (optional)
- Bleach (optional, for non-porous surfaces)
- Water
- Fan or dehumidifier
- Plastic sheeting or old towels

## Steps

### Step 1: Assess the Mold and Area

Identify the extent of the mold growth and its type. Ensure the area is well-ventilated by opening windows and using fans. If the mold covers a large area (more than 10 square feet) or appears to be black mold, consider professional help, especially if you suspect it's [How to Get Rid of Black Mold Safely and Effectively](/en/how-to-get-rid-of-black-mold). For small, superficial mold, proceed with cleaning.

> 💡 **Tip:** If the mold is extensive or you have health concerns, consult a professional mold remediation specialist.

### Step 2: Protect Yourself and the Surroundings

Before you begin, put on your safety goggles, rubber gloves, and an N95 respirator mask to avoid inhaling mold spores or coming into direct contact with the mold and cleaning solutions. Cover nearby furniture and flooring with plastic sheeting or old towels to protect them from spores and cleaning agents. This containment helps prevent the spread of mold during cleaning.

### Step 3: Choose and Prepare Your Cleaning Solution

For most common mold, a simple solution of undiluted white vinegar in a spray bottle is effective. Alternatively, you can mix 1 cup of borax with 1 gallon of water. For stubborn mold on non-porous surfaces like tiles, a bleach solution (1 cup bleach per gallon of water) can be used, but never mix bleach with vinegar or ammonia as it creates toxic fumes.

### Step 4: Apply the Solution and Scrub

Spray your chosen cleaning solution directly onto the moldy area, ensuring it's thoroughly saturated. Allow the solution to sit for 10-15 minutes to penetrate the mold and kill the spores. Using a stiff-bristled brush or an old toothbrush for smaller spots, gently scrub the mold away from the wall until it's gone. Avoid excessive scrubbing that could damage the wall surface.

### Step 5: Rinse and Dry Thoroughly

Once the mold is removed, wipe the cleaned area with a clean, damp cloth to remove any remaining residue and mold spores. It's crucial to dry the wall completely to prevent immediate regrowth. Use a clean, dry cloth, open windows, run fans, or use a dehumidifier to ensure the wall is bone dry within 24-48 hours.

### Step 6: Dispose of Contaminated Materials

Carefully place all used cloths, sponges, and protective coverings into a sealed plastic bag. Dispose of the bag immediately outside your home to prevent the spread of mold spores. Clean any reusable tools thoroughly with a disinfectant to eliminate any lingering mold residue.

### Step 7: Address the Moisture Source and Prevent Recurrence

Mold grows where there's moisture, so identifying and fixing the underlying issue is paramount. Fix any leaks, address condensation problems, or improve ventilation that contributed to the mold growth. Using moisture absorbers, improving air circulation, and maintaining indoor humidity levels below 60% are key steps to prevent future mold problems.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always ventilate the area well during and after cleaning to disperse spores and fumes safely.
- Test your cleaning solution on an inconspicuous area of the wall first to ensure it doesn't cause discoloration or damage to the paint or surface.
- For recurring mold issues, the source of moisture must be addressed; merely cleaning the surface won't solve the underlying problem.
- Consider using mold-resistant paint in areas prone to dampness after cleaning and drying the wall completely.
- Never paint over mold without cleaning it first, as this will not kill the mold and it will eventually grow through the paint.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Never mix bleach with ammonia or vinegar, as this creates highly toxic and dangerous fumes.
- ⚠️ If mold covers a large area (more than 10 square feet), or if you suspect it's black mold, it's best to call a professional mold remediation specialist.
- ⚠️ If you experience respiratory issues, headaches, or dizziness while cleaning mold, stop immediately, get fresh air, and consult a medical professional.
- ⚠️ Avoid scrubbing dry mold, as this can release a large number of spores into the air, potentially spreading the problem or causing health issues. Always dampen the mold first with your cleaning solution.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What causes mold to grow on walls?**

Mold thrives in damp, humid environments. Common causes include water leaks from pipes or roofs, condensation on cold surfaces, high indoor humidity levels, and poor ventilation, especially in bathrooms and kitchens where moisture accumulates.

**Is mold on walls dangerous to my health?**

Exposure to mold can cause various health problems, particularly for individuals with allergies, asthma, or weakened immune systems. Symptoms can include respiratory issues, skin irritation, headaches, and eye irritation, making prompt removal important.

**How to remove mold from painted walls safely?**

To remove mold from painted walls, gently scrub the affected area with a solution of white vinegar or borax and water. Avoid harsh scrubbing or abrasive cleaners that could damage the paint, and always test your solution on an inconspicuous spot first to ensure it doesn't discolor the surface.

**How do you remove mold from inside walls?**

Removing mold from inside walls is more complex as it often requires opening up the wall to access the affected area. This usually indicates a significant moisture problem that needs to be resolved, and it's best to consult a professional mold remediation specialist to identify the source and safely remove the mold.

**Can I use bleach to remove mold from walls?**

Bleach can be effective on non-porous surfaces like tile or fiberglass, but it's generally not recommended for porous surfaces like drywall or painted walls. While it kills surface mold, it doesn't penetrate deeply, and the water in the bleach solution can actually feed deeper mold growth, making it less effective in the long run.

**How can I prevent mold from returning to my walls?**

To prevent mold recurrence, address the underlying moisture issue. Ensure proper ventilation, fix leaks promptly, use dehumidifiers in damp areas, and maintain indoor humidity levels below 60%. Additionally, consider using mold-inhibiting paints in high-moisture zones after thorough cleaning.

**When should I call a professional for mold removal?**

You should call a professional if the mold covers an area larger than 10 square feet, if you suspect black mold, if the mold returns repeatedly despite your efforts, or if you have health concerns that are exacerbated by mold exposure. Professionals can safely identify the source and remediate the issue effectively.
