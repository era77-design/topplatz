Removing mold from walls is a manageable task for small to moderate infestations, requiring proper safety gear and cleaning solutions. Act quickly to prevent further spread and protect your home's air quality. Always ensure good ventilation during the cleaning process.

## What You Need

- Rubber gloves
- Safety glasses
- N95 mask or respirator
- Old clothes
- Spray bottle
- Scrub brush or sponge
- Microfiber cloths
- Bucket
- White vinegar
- Hydrogen peroxide (3%)
- Mild dish soap (optional)
- Fan
- Dehumidifier (optional)

## Steps

### Step 1: Prepare the Area and Yourself

Before you begin, ensure the area is well-ventilated. Open windows and doors, and if possible, use a fan to direct airflow outdoors. Put on all your protective gear: rubber gloves, safety glasses, and an N95 mask or respirator to avoid inhaling mold spores. Lay down old sheets or plastic sheeting to protect the floor from falling spores or cleaning solutions.

> 💡 **Tip:** Isolate the area if possible by closing interior doors to prevent spores from spreading to other rooms.

### Step 2: Choose Your Cleaning Solution

For porous surfaces like drywall, white vinegar is an excellent choice as it can kill up to 82% of mold species. Fill a spray bottle with undiluted white vinegar. For a stronger solution on non-porous surfaces, you can use a mixture of one part bleach to ten parts water, but never mix bleach with vinegar or ammonia. Hydrogen peroxide (3%) is another effective, non-toxic alternative.

> 💡 **Tip:** Avoid using harsh chemicals on delicate or painted surfaces unless you've tested them in an inconspicuous spot first.

### Step 3: Apply the Solution to the Mold

Generously spray your chosen cleaning solution directly onto the moldy area. Ensure the entire affected surface is saturated. For vinegar, let it sit for at least an hour to allow it to penetrate and kill the mold spores. If using hydrogen peroxide, let it sit for 10-15 minutes.

> 💡 **Tip:** Do not rinse the solution immediately. Allowing it to sit gives it time to work effectively against the mold.

### Step 4: Scrub and Wipe Away the Mold

After the solution has had time to work, use a stiff scrub brush or a durable sponge to scrub the mold from the wall. Apply firm but gentle pressure to avoid damaging the wall surface. Once the mold is dislodged, wipe the area clean with a damp microfiber cloth, rinsing the cloth frequently in a separate bucket of clean water.

> 💡 **Tip:** For extensive black mold infestations, it's often best to consult professionals. For more general advice on dealing with serious mold, refer to our guide on [How to Get Rid of Black Mold Safely and Effectively](/en/how-to-get-rid-of-black-mold).

### Step 5: Dry the Area Thoroughly

After cleaning, it's crucial to dry the wall completely to prevent the mold from returning. Use clean, dry microfiber cloths to blot the area, then allow it to air dry fully. You can use a fan or a dehumidifier to speed up the drying process and remove any remaining moisture from the air. Ensure the area is bone dry before considering any repainting or repairs.

> 💡 **Tip:** Proper ventilation is key during and after cleaning. Keep windows open and use fans to circulate air.

### Step 6: Dispose of Contaminated Materials

Carefully place all used cloths, sponges, and any protective sheeting into a heavy-duty garbage bag. Seal the bag tightly and dispose of it immediately to prevent mold spores from spreading. Thoroughly clean any reusable tools, like your scrub brush, with a disinfectant solution before storing them.

> 💡 **Tip:** Always wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water after handling mold and cleaning supplies.


## Tips & Tricks

- Prevent mold by controlling moisture: fix leaks promptly, use exhaust fans in bathrooms and kitchens, and consider a dehumidifier in humid areas.
- Improve air circulation: keep furniture slightly away from walls and open windows regularly to air out your home.
- Regularly inspect areas prone to moisture, such as bathrooms, basements, and areas around windows, for early signs of mold growth.
- If you're sensitive to mold, have respiratory issues, or if the mold covers an area larger than 10 square feet, consider hiring a professional mold remediation specialist.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Never mix bleach with ammonia, vinegar, or other household cleaners, as this can create dangerous, toxic fumes.
- ⚠️ Always wear appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE), including an N95 mask, gloves, and eye protection, to avoid inhaling spores or skin contact.
- ⚠️ If you suspect mold is behind walls or in HVAC systems, do not attempt to remove it yourself, as this requires professional assessment and remediation.
- ⚠️ Do not paint over mold without cleaning it first, as this will not kill the mold and it will eventually grow through the paint.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do you remove mold from painted walls?**

To remove mold from painted walls, first wear protective gear. Gently spray the affected area with undiluted white vinegar or a 3% hydrogen peroxide solution. Let it sit for an hour, then lightly scrub with a soft brush or sponge and wipe clean with a damp cloth, being careful not to damage the paint. Ensure the wall dries completely afterwards.

**What is the best way to clean mold off walls?**

The best way to clean mold off walls depends on the surface. For most wall types, a solution of undiluted white vinegar or 3% hydrogen peroxide applied with a spray bottle, allowed to sit, and then scrubbed away, is effective. Always prioritize safety with protective gear and good ventilation.

**How do you get rid of mold on walls permanently?**

To get rid of mold on walls permanently, you must address and eliminate the underlying moisture source. Clean the existing mold thoroughly, then fix any leaks, reduce humidity with dehumidifiers, improve ventilation, and ensure the area stays dry. Without addressing the moisture, mold is likely to return.

**When should I call a professional for mold removal?**

You should call a professional for mold removal if the mold covers an area larger than 10 square feet, if you suspect mold is hidden behind walls or in your HVAC system, or if you have health issues that make you particularly sensitive to mold exposure. Professionals have the expertise and equipment for safe and effective remediation.

**How to remove mold from inside walls?**

Removing mold from inside walls typically requires professional intervention. If you suspect mold behind drywall due to a persistent musty smell or visible water damage, it often means a larger, hidden problem. Professionals can safely open walls, identify the moisture source, and remediate the mold without spreading spores throughout your home.

**Can I use bleach to remove mold from walls?**

Bleach can be used on non-porous surfaces like tiles or sealed concrete, but it's generally not recommended for porous walls like drywall. While it kills surface mold, the water in the bleach can soak into porous materials, potentially feeding hidden mold growth. If used, mix one part bleach with ten parts water and ensure excellent ventilation.
