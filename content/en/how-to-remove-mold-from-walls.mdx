To remove mold from walls, first identify and fix the moisture source. For small, non-porous areas, a solution of distilled white vinegar or a mild bleach solution can effectively kill and clean the mold. Thoroughly dry the area to prevent recurrence.

## What You Need

- Rubber gloves
- N95 respirator mask
- Safety goggles
- Spray bottle
- Stiff-bristled scrub brush or sponge
- Bucket
- Old towels or rags
- Distilled white vinegar (undiluted)
- OR mild bleach solution (1 cup bleach to 1 gallon water)
- Clean water
- Fan or dehumidifier
- Drop cloths or plastic sheeting (optional)

## Steps

### Step 1: Prepare the Area and Yourself

Before starting, ensure the room is well-ventilated by opening windows and using fans. Don all your protective gear: rubber gloves, an N95 respirator mask, and safety goggles to protect against mold spores and cleaning solutions. Place drop cloths or plastic sheeting on the floor and over furniture to protect them from spores and spills.

### Step 2: Identify and Fix the Moisture Source

Mold cannot grow without moisture. It's crucial to find and fix the source of the dampness, whether it's a leaky pipe, condensation, or high humidity. If you don't address the moisture problem, the mold will likely return, making your cleaning efforts temporary. Repair any leaks promptly and consider using a dehumidifier in humid areas.

### Step 3: Choose Your Cleaning Solution

For most types of mold on walls, especially painted surfaces, distilled white vinegar is an excellent natural option. Fill a spray bottle with undiluted white vinegar. For non-porous surfaces like tile or sealed concrete, a mild bleach solution (1 cup of bleach mixed with 1 gallon of water) can be effective. Never mix bleach with vinegar or other cleaners, as this can create dangerous fumes. Always test your chosen solution on an inconspicuous area first to ensure it doesn't damage the wall finish.

### Step 4: Apply and Scrub the Mold

Spray your chosen cleaning solution directly onto the moldy area, ensuring it's thoroughly saturated. Allow the solution to sit for at least 10-15 minutes to penetrate and kill the mold spores. Using a stiff-bristled scrub brush or a sponge, scrub the mold away in small circular motions. For stubborn spots, you may need to reapply the solution and scrub again.

### Step 5: Rinse and Dry Thoroughly

Once the mold is removed, wipe the area with a clean, damp cloth to rinse away any residue from the cleaning solution and dead mold spores. It's critical to dry the wall completely to prevent future mold growth. Use old towels or rags to blot the area dry, then use a fan or dehumidifier to ensure no moisture remains. Good air circulation is key.

### Step 6: Prevent Future Mold Growth

After cleaning, take steps to prevent mold from returning. Maintain good ventilation in your home, especially in bathrooms and kitchens. Address any leaks immediately, keep indoor humidity levels below 60% with a dehumidifier, and ensure proper drainage around your home's foundation. Regular cleaning can also help catch new growth early.

### Step 7: Know When to Call a Professional

While small mold patches can be a DIY job, larger infestations or certain types of mold require professional attention. If the mold covers an area larger than 10 square feet, if it's growing behind walls, or if you suspect it's [How to Get Rid of Black Mold Safely and Effectively](/en/how-to-get-rid-of-black-mold), it's best to consult a certified mold remediation specialist. Professionals have the tools and expertise to safely remove extensive mold and prevent its return.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always test your cleaning solution on an inconspicuous area of the wall first to ensure it doesn't cause discoloration or damage.
- Improve air circulation in your home by opening windows, using exhaust fans, and running a dehumidifier, especially in damp areas.
- Address any water leaks, no matter how small, immediately to prevent mold from taking hold.
- For minor mold, a baking soda paste (baking soda and water) can also be used as a gentle abrasive cleaner.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Always wear protective gear (gloves, N95 mask, goggles) to avoid contact with mold spores and cleaning chemicals.
- ⚠️ Never mix bleach with ammonia or any other household cleaners, as this can create dangerous, toxic fumes.
- ⚠️ If the mold covers a large area (more than 10 square feet), or if you suspect it's [How to Get Rid of Black Mold Safely and Effectively](/en/how-to-get-rid-of-black-mold), it is safer to consult a professional mold remediation service.
- ⚠️ Mold can cause health problems, especially for those with allergies, asthma, or compromised immune systems. If you experience symptoms, leave the area and seek medical advice.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How to remove mold from painted walls?**

For painted walls, use a gentler approach to avoid damaging the paint. Spray undiluted distilled white vinegar directly onto the mold, let it sit for 10-15 minutes, then gently scrub with a soft brush or sponge. Rinse with a clean, damp cloth and dry thoroughly with a fan.

**How to remove mold from inside walls?**

Mold inside walls typically indicates a significant hidden moisture problem and often requires professional intervention. This usually involves opening up the wall to find and fix the leak, remove affected materials, and properly dry the structure. Attempting to clean it yourself without addressing the source can lead to recurrence or spread of spores.

**What is the best household product to remove mold from walls?**

Distilled white vinegar is highly effective for killing mold on most surfaces, including painted walls, and is a safer alternative to bleach for many applications. For non-porous surfaces, a diluted bleach solution (1 cup bleach to 1 gallon water) can also be used, but always ensure proper ventilation and never mix it with other cleaners.

**Can I paint over mold on walls?**

No, you should never paint over mold. Painting over mold will not kill it; the mold will continue to grow beneath the paint, potentially causing more damage to your walls and continuing to release spores into the air. Always clean and thoroughly dry the moldy area before applying any primer or paint.

**How can I prevent mold from growing back on walls?**

Preventing mold recurrence involves controlling moisture. Ensure good ventilation, especially in bathrooms and kitchens. Fix any leaks promptly, use a dehumidifier in humid environments to keep indoor humidity below 60%, and ensure proper insulation to prevent condensation on walls.

**When should I call a professional for mold removal?**

You should call a professional if the mold covers a large area (more than 10 square feet), if it's recurrent, if you suspect it's [How to Get Rid of Black Mold Safely and Effectively](/en/how-to-get-rid-of-black-mold), or if you experience health issues related to mold exposure. Professionals have the equipment and expertise for safe and effective remediation.
