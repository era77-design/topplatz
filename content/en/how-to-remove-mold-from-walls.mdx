Removing mold from walls is crucial for maintaining a healthy home environment and preventing further structural damage. Small, superficial patches can often be tackled with common household cleaners and proper safety precautions. Always identify and address the underlying moisture source to prevent mold from returning.

## What You Need

- Safety goggles
- Rubber gloves
- N95 respirator mask
- Old clothes
- Spray bottle
- Stiff brush or scrub sponge
- Microfiber cloths
- Bucket
- White vinegar
- Borax
- Mild dish soap
- Water
- Fan or dehumidifier
- Plastic sheeting (optional)

## Steps

### Step 1: Prepare the Area and Yourself

Before you begin, ensure the area is well-ventilated by opening windows and doors. Put on your safety goggles, rubber gloves, and N95 respirator mask to protect yourself from mold spores and cleaning solutions. If possible, remove furniture or cover it with plastic sheeting to protect it from spores and splatters.

### Step 2: Identify and Fix the Moisture Source

Mold cannot grow without moisture, so finding and fixing the source is paramount to preventing its return. Look for leaky pipes, roof leaks, condensation issues, or poor ventilation. Repairing these problems before cleaning will ensure your efforts are long-lasting.

### Step 3: Choose and Apply Your Cleaning Solution

For non-porous surfaces like painted walls, white vinegar is an effective natural fungicide. Fill a spray bottle with undiluted white vinegar and spray it directly onto the moldy area. For tougher mold, a solution of 1 cup borax dissolved in 1 gallon of hot water, or a mixture of warm water and mild dish soap, can also be effective. Let the solution sit for 10-15 minutes to penetrate the mold.

### Step 4: Scrub Away the Mold

Using a stiff brush or scrub sponge, scrub the moldy surface thoroughly. Work in small sections, making sure to remove all visible traces of mold. For painted walls, be gentle to avoid damaging the paint, but firm enough to lift the mold. Dispose of any heavily contaminated cloths or sponges immediately after use.

### Step 5: Rinse and Dry the Wall Completely

After scrubbing, wipe down the area with a clean, damp microfiber cloth to remove any remaining cleaning solution and mold residues. It's crucial to dry the wall completely to prevent immediate re-growth. Use a fan, dehumidifier, or open windows to speed up the drying process. Ensure the wall is bone-dry before considering any repainting.

### Step 6: Dispose of Contaminated Materials Safely

Place all used cloths, sponges, and disposable protective gear in a sealed plastic bag. Take the bag outside immediately to prevent mold spores from spreading within your home. Wash any reusable items, like rubber gloves, thoroughly with hot, soapy water.

### Step 7: Prevent Future Mold Growth

Maintain good ventilation in your home, especially in bathrooms, kitchens, and basements. Use exhaust fans and dehumidifiers to keep indoor humidity levels below 60%. Regularly inspect walls for any signs of moisture or new mold growth, addressing issues promptly.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always test your chosen cleaning solution on an inconspicuous area of the wall first to ensure it doesn't cause discoloration or damage.
- Never mix different cleaning products, especially bleach with ammonia-based cleaners, as this can create dangerous toxic fumes.
- For areas larger than 10 square feet or if you suspect extensive hidden mold, consider consulting a professional mold remediation specialist.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Always wear appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE), including an N95 mask, safety goggles, and rubber gloves, to protect against mold spores and cleaning chemicals.
- ⚠️ Do not use bleach on porous surfaces like drywall, as it may not kill the mold's roots and can damage the material.
- ⚠️ If you encounter what appears to be black mold, or if you experience respiratory issues during cleaning, it's best to stop and consult a professional. [How to Get Rid of Black Mold Safely and Effectively](/en/how-to-get-rid-of-black-mold) offers more insights.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How to remove mold from walls?**

To remove mold from walls, first ensure proper ventilation and wear protective gear. Identify and fix the moisture source, then apply a cleaning solution like white vinegar or borax. Scrub the mold away, rinse, and thoroughly dry the area to prevent recurrence.

**How to remove mold from painted walls?**

Removing mold from painted walls involves gently scrubbing with a mild cleaning solution like white vinegar or a mix of water and dish soap. Be careful not to damage the paint. After cleaning, rinse the area and dry it completely with a fan or dehumidifier to prevent the mold from returning.

**How to remove mold from inside walls?**

Removing mold from inside walls typically requires opening up the wall to access the affected area. This is a more complex task that often indicates a significant moisture problem. It's usually best to consult a professional mold remediation specialist for hidden mold to ensure it's fully addressed and the underlying issue is resolved.

**What causes mold to grow on walls?**

Mold thrives in damp, dark environments with poor ventilation. Common causes include water leaks from pipes or roofs, condensation from humidity, flooding, and insufficient airflow in certain areas of a room. Addressing these moisture sources is key to prevention.

**When should I call a professional for mold removal?**

You should call a professional if the moldy area is larger than 10 square feet, if you suspect mold behind walls or in HVAC systems, or if anyone in your household has severe allergies or respiratory issues. Professionals can safely assess and remediate extensive or hidden mold growth.

**Is it safe to remove mold yourself?**

It is generally safe to remove small patches of surface mold yourself, provided you wear appropriate personal protective equipment like an N95 mask, goggles, and gloves. However, if the mold covers a large area, is black mold, or you have health concerns, professional help is recommended.

**How can I prevent mold from returning to my walls?**

To prevent mold from returning, control indoor humidity levels using dehumidifiers and proper ventilation, especially in moisture-prone areas. Promptly repair any leaks, ensure good air circulation, and clean spills or wet areas immediately to eliminate moisture sources.
