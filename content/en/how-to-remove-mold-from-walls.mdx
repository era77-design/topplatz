To remove mold from walls, start by identifying the source of moisture and addressing it. Then, don protective gear and clean the affected area with a mold-killing solution like diluted bleach or white vinegar, scrubbing gently before rinsing and drying thoroughly to prevent regrowth.

## What You Need

- Rubber gloves
- Safety goggles
- N95 respirator mask
- Drop cloths or old sheets
- Spray bottle
- Stiff-bristled scrub brush or sponge
- Two buckets
- Clean cloths
- White vinegar (undiluted) or household bleach
- Water
- Fan or dehumidifier

## Steps

### Step 1: Prepare the Area and Yourself

Before you begin, ensure the area is well-ventilated by opening windows and doors. Lay down drop cloths or old sheets to protect floors and furniture from mold spores and cleaning solutions. Crucially, wear protective gear including rubber gloves, safety goggles, and an N95 respirator mask to prevent inhaling spores or skin contact with mold or cleaning agents.

### Step 2: Address the Moisture Source

Mold thrives on moisture, so effectively removing it requires eliminating its water source. Inspect the wall for leaks, condensation, or other sources of dampness. Repair any leaky pipes, seal cracks, or improve ventilation to reduce humidity. Failing to address the moisture issue will likely lead to mold returning quickly.

### Step 3: Choose Your Cleaning Solution

For non-porous surfaces like painted walls, you have a couple of effective options. A diluted bleach solution (1 cup bleach per gallon of water) is strong, or you can use undiluted white vinegar in a spray bottle as a natural alternative. Always test your chosen solution on an inconspicuous spot first to ensure it doesn't discolor or damage the wall surface. For specific guidance on dealing with black mold, refer to `[How to Get Rid of Black Mold Safely and Effectively](/en/how-to-get-rid-of-black-mold)`.

### Step 4: Apply and Scrub the Mold

Spray the moldy area thoroughly with your chosen cleaning solution, ensuring complete saturation. Allow the solution to sit for 10-15 minutes to penetrate the mold and kill the spores. Using a stiff-bristled scrub brush or sponge, gently scrub the mold away in small sections. Avoid aggressive scrubbing that could damage the wall's finish.

### Step 5: Rinse and Dry Thoroughly

After scrubbing, use a clean cloth dampened with plain water to wipe down the cleaned area, removing any residue from the mold and cleaning solution. It's critical that the wall dries completely to prevent immediate mold regrowth. Use a fan, open windows, or a dehumidifier to speed up the drying process. Do not paint or seal the wall until it is bone dry.

### Step 6: Prevent Future Mold Growth

To deter mold from returning, maintain low humidity levels in your home, ideally between 30-50%. Use exhaust fans in bathrooms and kitchens, ensure proper ventilation, and promptly fix any new leaks or water damage. Regularly inspect susceptible areas for early signs of moisture or mold growth, addressing them quickly before they become widespread.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always work in a well-ventilated area to minimize exposure to mold spores and cleaning fumes.
- For persistent mold or very large affected areas (greater than 10 square feet), consider consulting a professional mold remediation service.
- If using bleach, never mix it with ammonia or other household cleaners, as this can create toxic fumes.
- After cleaning, wash all contaminated cloths, sponges, and protective gear, or dispose of them properly.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Never mix bleach with ammonia or other cleaning products; it creates dangerous, toxic fumes.
- ⚠️ Always wear appropriate personal protective equipment, including an N95 mask, gloves, and eye protection, to avoid inhaling mold spores or contact with cleaning chemicals.
- ⚠️ If you suspect mold is growing behind walls or in insulation, it may require professional removal as disturbance can release a large number of spores.
- ⚠️ If you or anyone in your household has respiratory issues or allergies, it's best to have someone else handle mold removal or consult a professional.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do you remove mold from walls?**

To remove mold from walls, first eliminate the moisture source. Then, wearing protective gear, clean the affected area with a diluted bleach solution (1 cup bleach per gallon of water) or undiluted white vinegar. Scrub gently, rinse with clean water, and ensure the wall dries completely to prevent recurrence.

**How do you remove mold from painted walls?**

Removing mold from painted walls follows the same general steps. Use a diluted bleach solution or undiluted white vinegar, but always test on an inconspicuous area first to ensure it won't damage the paint. Scrub gently to avoid stripping the paint, then rinse and dry thoroughly with fans or a dehumidifier.

**What kills mold on walls permanently?**

There's no single permanent killer for mold without addressing its underlying cause. While solutions like bleach and vinegar kill surface mold, preventing its return permanently requires eliminating the moisture source, improving ventilation, and maintaining low indoor humidity levels to stop new growth.

**How do you remove mold from inside walls?**

Removing mold from inside walls is a complex task that typically requires opening up the wall to access and remediate the affected area. This usually indicates a significant moisture problem that needs professional assessment and remediation to ensure thorough removal and prevent structural damage or health hazards.

**When should I call a professional for mold removal?**

You should call a professional for mold removal if the mold covers an area larger than 10 square feet, if it's due to contaminated water (like sewage), if you suspect hidden mold behind walls, or if you or family members experience health issues related to mold exposure. Professionals have the tools and expertise for safe and effective remediation.

**How can I prevent mold from returning to my walls?**

To prevent mold from returning, control indoor humidity levels (ideally 30-50%) using dehumidifiers and proper ventilation, especially in moisture-prone areas like bathrooms and kitchens. Promptly repair any leaks, ensure good air circulation, and use mold-inhibiting paints in high-risk areas.
