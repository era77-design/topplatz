Removing mold from walls is a manageable task if addressed promptly and safely. The key is to eliminate the mold, clean the affected surface thoroughly, and prevent its recurrence by addressing the underlying moisture source.

## What You Need

- Safety goggles
- Rubber gloves
- N95 mask
- Spray bottle
- Stiff-bristled scrub brush or sponge
- Bucket
- White vinegar or 3% hydrogen peroxide or borax
- Water
- Old towels or rags
- Fan or dehumidifier

## Steps

### Step 1: Prepare the Area and Yourself

Before you begin, ensure the area is well-ventilated by opening windows and using fans. Put on your safety goggles, rubber gloves, and N95 mask to protect yourself from mold spores and cleaning solutions. Clear the area of furniture or cover it with plastic sheeting to prevent contamination.

> 💡 **Tip:** Never try to scrape or brush dry mold, as this can release spores into the air.

### Step 2: Identify the Mold and Assess the Damage

Determine the extent of the mold growth. For small, superficial mold patches, DIY removal is usually fine. If you suspect black mold, or if the mold covers a large area (more than 10 square feet), consider consulting a professional mold remediation service. For more detailed guidance on specific types, refer to [How to Get Rid of Black Mold Safely and Effectively](/en/how-to-get-rid-of-black-mold).

> 💡 **Tip:** Always address the source of moisture causing the mold, or it will return.

### Step 3: Choose Your Cleaning Solution

For non-porous surfaces like painted walls, white vinegar, hydrogen peroxide, or a borax solution are effective. Fill a spray bottle with undiluted white vinegar, or a 50/50 mixture of 3% hydrogen peroxide and water. If using borax, dissolve one cup of borax in a gallon of hot water.

> 💡 **Tip:** Avoid using bleach on porous surfaces as it often doesn't kill mold roots and can damage materials. Never mix different cleaning solutions together.

### Step 4: Apply the Solution to the Mold

Liberally spray or apply your chosen cleaning solution directly onto the moldy area. Ensure the entire affected surface is saturated. Allow the solution to sit for at least 10-15 minutes to penetrate the mold and start breaking it down.

> 💡 **Tip:** Test your chosen solution on an inconspicuous spot first to ensure it doesn't discolor or damage your wall paint.

### Step 5: Scrub and Wipe Away the Mold

Using a stiff-bristled scrub brush or sponge, gently scrub the mold from the wall. Apply consistent pressure to remove all visible traces. Once scrubbed, wipe down the area with a clean, damp cloth to remove any remaining mold spores and cleaning residue.

> 💡 **Tip:** Rinse your brush or sponge frequently in a separate bucket of clean water to avoid spreading mold spores.

### Step 6: Dry the Wall Thoroughly

Thorough drying is crucial to prevent mold from returning. Use old towels or rags to blot the area dry as much as possible. Then, use fans, a dehumidifier, or open windows to ensure the wall is completely dry. This process can take several hours.

> 💡 **Tip:** Ensure the relative humidity in your home stays below 60% to deter mold growth.

### Step 7: Prevent Future Mold Growth

After cleaning, take steps to prevent mold from recurring. Address any leaks, improve ventilation in damp areas like bathrooms and kitchens, and consider using mold-resistant primer and paint in susceptible areas. Regularly inspect walls for new signs of moisture or mold.

> 💡 **Tip:** Maintain good air circulation throughout your home, especially in areas prone to dampness.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always wear protective gear, including an N95 mask, safety goggles, and rubber gloves.
- Never mix cleaning chemicals, especially bleach with ammonia or vinegar, as it can create toxic fumes.
- Address the underlying moisture source immediately to prevent mold from returning.
- For severe or widespread mold, or if you have health concerns, consult a professional mold remediation specialist.
- Consider using mold-resistant paint or primer on walls in high-humidity areas after cleaning.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Do not attempt to clean mold if you have respiratory issues or a compromised immune system; hire a professional.
- ⚠️ If mold covers an area larger than 10 square feet or is deeply embedded, professional remediation is recommended.
- ⚠️ Avoid using bleach on porous surfaces like drywall, as it often only bleaches the surface mold, leaving the roots intact and potentially promoting regrowth.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How to remove mold from walls?**

To remove mold from walls, first don protective gear, then clean the area with a solution like white vinegar or hydrogen peroxide. Scrub the mold away, wipe clean, and ensure the wall is completely dry to prevent recurrence.

**How to remove mold from painted walls?**

When removing mold from painted walls, use a gentle cleaning solution like white vinegar or a diluted hydrogen peroxide solution to avoid stripping the paint. Test the solution on an inconspicuous area first, then gently scrub and rinse, ensuring thorough drying.

**How to remove mold from inside walls?**

Removing mold from inside walls often requires opening up the wall to access the affected area, which can be a complex task. If you suspect mold behind drywall, it's best to consult a professional mold remediation specialist, as it indicates a significant moisture problem.

**What kills mold on walls permanently?**

No single product kills mold permanently without addressing the root cause. Effective mold removal involves cleaning with solutions like vinegar or hydrogen peroxide, followed by eliminating the moisture source, ensuring good ventilation, and maintaining low indoor humidity.

**Is it safe to remove mold from walls yourself?**

It can be safe to remove small patches of surface mold yourself, provided you wear appropriate protective gear like an N95 mask, gloves, and safety goggles. However, for large areas, black mold, or if you have respiratory sensitivities, it's safer to hire professionals.

**Can mold on walls make you sick?**

Yes, mold on walls can potentially make some people sick, especially those with allergies, asthma, or compromised immune systems. Exposure can lead to respiratory problems, skin irritation, headaches, and other health issues, making prompt removal important.
