To delete your Facebook account, you must navigate to your settings, choose 'Account Ownership and Control,' and then select 'Deactivation and Deletion.' From there, you can choose 'Permanent Account Deletion' and follow the prompts. Alternatively, you can deactivate your account for a temporary break.

## What You Need

- Internet access
- Your Facebook login credentials
- A computer or mobile device

## Steps

### Step 1: Understand Deactivation vs. Permanent Deletion

Before proceeding, it's crucial to understand the difference between deactivating and permanently deleting your Facebook account. Deactivation is a temporary measure that hides your profile and content, allowing you to reactivate it later. Permanent deletion, however, removes all your data and content from Facebook after a grace period, making it unrecoverable.

> 💡 **Tip:** Consider deactivating your account first if you're unsure about permanent deletion; you can always reactivate it.

### Step 2: Download Your Facebook Data

Facebook allows you to download a copy of your information, including photos, posts, videos, and messages. This is an important step if you wish to preserve your memories or data before permanent deletion. Navigate to 'Settings & Privacy' > 'Settings' > 'Your Facebook Information' > 'Download Your Information' and follow the instructions to create a file for download.

> 💡 **Tip:** Ensure you download all desired data before initiating deletion, as it will be lost permanently otherwise.

### Step 3: Navigate to Account Deletion Settings

Once you've backed up your data, go to 'Settings & Privacy' > 'Settings'. In the left-hand menu, click on 'Your Facebook Information' (or 'Privacy' > 'Your Facebook Information' on mobile). Then, select 'Account Ownership and Control' and click on 'Deactivation and Deletion'.

> 💡 **Tip:** The exact menu wording might vary slightly between desktop and mobile versions, but the path is generally similar.

### Step 4: Choose Permanent Account Deletion

Within 'Deactivation and Deletion', you will be presented with two options: 'Deactivate Account' and 'Delete Account'. Select 'Delete Account' for permanent removal. Click 'Continue to Account Deletion' to proceed to the next step, where Facebook will offer some suggestions to stay or provide reasons for leaving.

> 💡 **Tip:** Read through any prompts carefully to ensure you are selecting the correct option for permanent deletion.

### Step 5: Confirm Deletion and Enter Password

Facebook will ask you for a final confirmation to delete your account. You might be prompted to enter your password to verify your identity. After entering your password and clicking 'Delete Account', your deletion request will be registered.

> 💡 **Tip:** Double-check that this is what you want to do, as this is the final confirmation before the grace period begins.

### Step 6: Understand the Deletion Grace Period

After confirming deletion, Facebook provides a 30-day grace period. During this time, your account is scheduled for permanent deletion, but it's not yet gone. If you log back into your Facebook account within these 30 days, your deletion request will be canceled, and your account will be reactivated. After 30 days, the deletion process becomes irreversible.

> 💡 **Tip:** If you change your mind within 30 days, simply log back in to cancel the deletion.

### Step 7: Disconnect Third-Party Apps (Optional but Recommended)

Before or after initiating deletion, it's a good practice to review and remove any third-party applications or websites that you've used your Facebook account to log into. This helps ensure that your data isn't retained by other services linked to your Facebook profile. You can usually find this under 'Settings & Privacy' > 'Settings' > 'Apps and Websites'.

> 💡 **Tip:** Removing linked apps prevents them from potentially retaining or accessing residual data after your Facebook account is gone.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always download a copy of your data before initiating account deletion to preserve memories and information.
- If you're unsure, try deactivating your account first. You can reactivate it anytime, unlike permanent deletion.
- Remember to delete any third-party apps or websites that use your Facebook login to prevent data retention issues elsewhere.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Once your Facebook account is permanently deleted, it cannot be recovered, and all your data, posts, photos, and friends lists will be gone forever after the grace period.
- ⚠️ Be aware that some data, like messages sent to friends, may remain visible to recipients even after your account is deleted.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the difference between deactivating and deleting my Facebook account?**

Deactivating your account temporarily hides your profile and content, allowing you to reactivate it later. Deleting your account is permanent and removes all your data after a 30-day grace period, making it unrecoverable.

**Can I recover my Facebook account after I've requested deletion?**

Yes, you have a 30-day grace period after requesting permanent deletion to log back into your account and cancel the deletion request. After this period, your account and all its data will be permanently removed.

**How long does it take for my Facebook account to be permanently deleted?**

After you confirm permanent deletion, Facebook provides a 30-day grace period during which your account is still technically active. After these 30 days, it can take up to 90 days for all your data to be completely removed from Facebook's backup systems.

**What happens to my photos, posts, and messages after deletion?**

All your photos, posts, videos, and other content you've shared on Facebook will be permanently deleted. However, messages you've sent to friends may remain in their inboxes, as these are considered part of their conversations.

**Will my friends be notified if I delete my Facebook account?**

No, Facebook does not send out a notification to your friends when you delete your account. Your profile will simply disappear from their friends list and search results, and they will no longer be able to view your content.

**Does deleting my Facebook account also delete my Messenger account?**

Yes, deleting your Facebook account also deletes your Messenger account. If you only want to use Messenger without Facebook, you should deactivate your Facebook account instead of deleting it.

**Can I delete my Facebook account using a mobile device?**

Yes, the process for deleting your Facebook account is similar whether you are using a computer browser or the Facebook app on a mobile device. Navigate to 'Settings & Privacy' and follow the steps for 'Account Ownership and Control'.
