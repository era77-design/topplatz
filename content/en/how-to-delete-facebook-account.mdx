Deleting your Facebook account involves either temporary deactivation or permanent deletion. Permanent deletion removes your profile, photos, posts, videos, and all other data associated with your account from Facebook's servers. It's a final step, so ensure you're ready to proceed.

## What You Need

- Facebook account login credentials
- Stable internet connection
- Device with Facebook access (computer or mobile)

## Steps

### Step 1: Understand Deactivation vs. Deletion

Before you begin, understand the difference between deactivating and deleting your account. Deactivation is temporary; your profile is hidden, but you can reactivate it anytime. Permanent deletion removes all your data, and the process is irreversible after a grace period. Make sure you choose the option that best suits your needs.

> 💡 **Tip:** If you're unsure, consider deactivating your account first to see if you miss Facebook.

### Step 2: Backup Your Facebook Data (Optional but Recommended)

If there are photos, posts, or information you want to keep, download a copy of your data before proceeding. Navigate to 'Settings & Privacy,' then 'Settings,' and look for 'Your Facebook Information.' Select 'Download Your Information' to save a copy of your data to your device. This ensures you retain any cherished memories or important information.

> 💡 **Tip:** Downloading your data can take some time depending on how much content you have.

### Step 3: Access Deactivation and Deletion Settings

On a desktop, click your profile picture in the top right, then 'Settings & Privacy,' followed by 'Settings.' From the left menu, select 'Your Facebook Information' (or 'Privacy' then 'Your Facebook Information' in some layouts). On mobile, tap the menu icon (three lines), scroll down to 'Settings & Privacy,' then 'Settings,' and find 'Account Ownership and Control.'

> 💡 **Tip:** You can also try using the direct link: facebook.com/help/delete_account

### Step 4: Choose Permanent Account Deletion

Within 'Your Facebook Information' or 'Account Ownership and Control,' find 'Deactivation and Deletion.' Click or tap on this option, and you will be presented with choices for deactivating or deleting your account. Select 'Delete Account' and then 'Continue to Account Deletion.' Read the information carefully to understand what will be removed.

> 💡 **Tip:** Ensure you do not select 'Deactivate Account' if your intention is permanent deletion.

### Step 5: Confirm Your Deletion Request

Facebook will offer options to deactivate or download your information one last time. If you're certain, click 'Delete Account.' You will then be prompted to enter your password for security verification. Once confirmed, your account will be scheduled for permanent deletion, initiating a grace period.

> 💡 **Tip:** Remember your password; you'll need it for this final step.

### Step 6: Avoid Logging In During the Grace Period

After confirming, your account enters a grace period, typically 30 days. During this time, your account is hidden from others. It is crucial NOT to log back into your Facebook account, including Messenger, during this period. Logging in will automatically cancel your deletion request, and you'll have to start the process over.

> 💡 **Tip:** Consider logging out of all Facebook-related apps on all devices to avoid accidental logins.

### Step 7: Verify Deletion After the Grace Period

Once the 30-day grace period has passed, your account should be permanently deleted. You will no longer be able to log in, and your data will be purged from Facebook's servers. If you attempt to log in after this period and are unsuccessful, it indicates the deletion was successful. Be aware that some data, like messages sent to friends, may persist in their inboxes.

> 💡 **Tip:** Allow a few extra days beyond the grace period for full system processing.


## Tips & Tricks

- Consider deactivating first if you're unsure about permanent deletion.
- Ensure you've canceled any subscriptions or services linked to your Facebook account.
- Inform friends and family if you're leaving Facebook permanently so they know how to contact you.
- Review apps and websites connected to your Facebook login before deleting your account.
- Deleting your Facebook account will also delete your associated Messenger account.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Once your account is permanently deleted after the grace period, you cannot recover it, and all your data will be gone.
- ⚠️ Logging into your Facebook account during the 30-day grace period will cancel your deletion request.
- ⚠️ Some information, like messages you've sent to friends, may remain visible to them even after your account is deleted.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What's the difference between deactivating and deleting my Facebook account?**

Deactivating hides your profile temporarily, allowing you to reactivate it anytime. Deleting is a permanent action that removes all your data from Facebook's servers after a grace period, making it irreversible.

**Can I recover my Facebook account after I've requested deletion?**

Yes, you typically have a grace period of 30 days to log back into your account and cancel the deletion request. After this period, the deletion process becomes permanent and irreversible.

**What happens to my photos and posts after deletion?**

All your photos, posts, videos, and other content will be permanently removed from Facebook's servers once the deletion process is complete after the grace period. It's highly recommended to download your data beforehand if you wish to keep anything.

**Will deleting my Facebook account also delete my Messenger account?**

Yes, deleting your Facebook account will also delete your Messenger account and all associated messages. You will lose access to Messenger conversations linked to that Facebook profile.

**Does deleting Facebook delete my linked Instagram account?**

No, deleting your Facebook account does not automatically delete your Instagram account, even if they were linked. Instagram is a separate platform, and you will need to delete it separately if desired.

**Can I still use Facebook-linked apps after deleting my account?**

No, if you used your Facebook account to log into other apps or websites, you will lose access to them. You may need to create new login credentials for those services or find alternative ways to access them.

**How long does it take for my account to be completely deleted?**

While the deletion request is initiated immediately, Facebook typically takes 30 days (the grace period) before beginning the permanent deletion process. It can take up to 90 days for all your data to be removed from Facebook's backup systems.
