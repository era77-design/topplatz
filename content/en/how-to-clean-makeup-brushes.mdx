To clean makeup brushes, first, wet the bristles with lukewarm water. Then, gently swirl them on a cleaning mat or in the palm of your hand with a mild cleanser. Rinse thoroughly and reshape before allowing them to air dry completely.

## What You Need

- Mild soap (baby shampoo, gentle face cleanser, or specialized brush cleaner)
- Lukewarm water
- Cleaning mat or textured glove (optional)
- Clean towel

## Steps

### Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you begin, collect all necessary items: a mild cleanser (such as baby shampoo, gentle face wash, or a dedicated brush cleaner), lukewarm water, a clean towel, and optionally, a silicone cleaning mat or glove. Having everything ready will make the process smooth and efficient. Ensure your sink is clean and ready for use.

### Step 2: Wet the Bristles (Carefully)

Hold your brush with the bristles pointing downwards under lukewarm running water. Be careful to only wet the bristles and avoid submerging the ferrule (the metal part connecting the bristles to the handle). Water entering the ferrule can weaken the glue, causing bristles to shed or the handle to detach over time.

### Step 3: Apply Cleanser

Dispense a small amount of your chosen cleanser into the palm of your hand or onto a cleaning mat. Gently swirl the wet brush bristles into the cleanser, working up a lather. Be gentle; avoid pressing too hard or splaying the bristles unnecessarily, which can damage their shape.

> 💡 **Tip:** For stubborn makeup, like cream products or foundation, you might need to repeat this step or use a slightly stronger dedicated brush cleaner.

### Step 4: Rinse Thoroughly

Continue to hold the brush bristles-down under lukewarm running water, gently rinsing away all traces of soap and makeup. Lightly squeeze the bristles from the ferrule downwards to help push out any remaining residue. Keep rinsing until the water runs clear and no suds are visible.

### Step 5: Reshape and Squeeze Out Excess Water

Gently squeeze the excess water from the bristles using your fingers. Reshape the brush head back to its original form. This helps maintain the brush's integrity and ensures it dries correctly, preventing splayed or misshapen bristles.

### Step 6: Lay Flat to Dry

Lay the clean, reshaped brushes flat on a clean, dry towel or hang them bristles-down if you have a brush drying rack. Ensure there is good air circulation around them. Avoid standing them upright, as water can seep into the ferrule and loosen the glue. Allow them to air dry completely, which can take several hours or overnight depending on the brush density.

> 💡 **Tip:** For faster drying, place brushes near an open window or in a well-ventilated room, but avoid direct sunlight or heat, which can damage bristles.


## Tips & Tricks

- Clean brushes used with liquid or cream products (like foundation and concealer) weekly, and powder/eyeshadow brushes every 1-2 weeks.
- Spot clean brushes daily with a quick-drying brush spray for immediate use between deep cleans.
- Never use harsh detergents, extremely hot water, or blow dryers, as these can damage bristles and glue.
- Store brushes properly after drying to prevent dust accumulation and maintain their shape.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Do not get water into the ferrule (the metal part) of the brush, as it can loosen the glue and cause bristles to shed.
- ⚠️ Never dry brushes upright, as water can seep into the ferrule and weaken the adhesive.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do you clean makeup brushes effectively?**

To effectively clean makeup brushes, use a mild soap or specialized cleaner with lukewarm water. Gently swirl the bristles on a textured surface or your palm, then rinse thoroughly until the water runs clear. Reshape the bristles and lay them flat to air dry completely.

**How often should you clean your makeup brushes?**

For brushes used with liquid or cream products like foundation and concealer, clean them weekly to prevent product buildup and bacterial growth. Powder and eyeshadow brushes can typically be cleaned every 1-2 weeks, depending on usage. Regular cleaning ensures hygiene and better makeup application.

**What can I use to clean makeup brushes at home if I don't have a special cleaner?**

You can easily clean makeup brushes at home using common household items. Baby shampoo is an excellent gentle option, as is a mild, fragrance-free face cleanser. Both effectively remove makeup without damaging the bristles.

**What's the best way to dry your makeup brushes after cleaning them?**

After cleaning, gently squeeze out excess water and reshape the bristles to their original form. Lay the brushes flat on a clean, dry towel with the bristles slightly hanging over the edge of a counter, or use a brush drying rack to hang them bristles-down. This prevents water from seeping into the ferrule and ensures even drying.

**Why is it important to clean your makeup brushes regularly?**

Cleaning your makeup brushes regularly is crucial for hygiene and skin health. Dirty brushes accumulate bacteria, dead skin cells, and old makeup, which can lead to breakouts, irritation, and even infections. Additionally, clean brushes perform better, allowing for smoother and more even makeup application.

**Can I use dish soap to clean my makeup brushes?**

While dish soap is effective at cutting through grease, it can be too harsh for natural hair brushes and may strip them of their natural oils or dry out synthetic bristles over time. It's generally better to stick to milder cleansers like baby shampoo or dedicated brush cleaners to preserve the longevity and softness of your brushes.
