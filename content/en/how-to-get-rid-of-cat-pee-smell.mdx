To get rid of cat pee smell, identify the source, blot fresh urine immediately, and clean thoroughly with an enzymatic cleaner. Enzymatic cleaners break down the uric acid crystals responsible for the persistent odor, effectively neutralizing the smell rather than just masking it.

## What You Need

- Paper towels or clean cloths
- Rubber gloves
- Enzymatic pet odor cleaner
- White vinegar (optional, for pre-treatment)
- Baking soda
- Spray bottle (optional)
- UV blacklight (optional, for locating old stains)
- Wet vac or carpet cleaner (optional, for large areas)

## Steps

### Step 1: Locate the Source of the Smell

Before you can eliminate the cat pee smell, you must find all affected areas. Even dried urine can emit a strong odor. Use your nose, get down low, or, for older, invisible stains, use a UV blacklight in a darkened room to make dried urine glow. Mark these spots with tape or chalk.

> 💡 **Tip:** Check common areas like carpets, rugs, furniture, baseboards, and even walls or potted plants. Cats often return to the same spot if it still smells like urine.

### Step 2: Blot Fresh Urine Immediately

For fresh accidents, speed is key. Don rubber gloves and use a stack of paper towels or a clean, absorbent cloth to blot up as much urine as possible. Press down firmly and replace towels until no more moisture transfers. Avoid rubbing, as this can spread the urine and push it deeper into fibers.

> 💡 **Tip:** Stand on the paper towels to apply more pressure and absorb maximum liquid from carpets or upholstery.

### Step 3: Pre-Treat with Vinegar Solution (Optional but Recommended)

If the stain is fresh or particularly stubborn, a vinegar solution can help neutralize some of the ammonia smell before applying an enzymatic cleaner. Mix equal parts white vinegar and water in a spray bottle. Lightly spray the affected area, allowing it to sit for 5-10 minutes, then blot thoroughly with clean paper towels.

> 💡 **Tip:** Always test any cleaning solution on an inconspicuous spot first to ensure it doesn't cause discoloration or damage.

### Step 4: Apply an Enzymatic Cleaner

Enzymatic cleaners are crucial because they contain enzymes that break down the uric acid crystals in cat urine, which are responsible for the lingering odor. Follow the product instructions carefully, typically by saturating the soiled area. Allow the cleaner to sit for the recommended dwell time, which can vary from 15 minutes to several hours, or even overnight.

> 💡 **Tip:** Ensure the enzymatic cleaner penetrates as deeply as the urine did, especially in carpets or upholstery, for complete odor elimination.

### Step 5: Allow to Air Dry Completely

After the enzymatic cleaner has worked its magic, do not rinse the area immediately. Allow it to air dry completely. This allows the enzymes to continue breaking down the urine components. You may want to cover the area with plastic wrap during the drying process to prevent your cat from returning to the spot.

> 💡 **Tip:** Open windows or use fans to improve air circulation and speed up the drying process.

### Step 6: Repeat If Necessary & Deodorize

For persistent or old odors, you may need to repeat the enzymatic cleaning process multiple times. Once the area is completely dry and the smell has significantly diminished, sprinkle baking soda generously over the spot. Let it sit for several hours or overnight to absorb any remaining odors, then vacuum it up.

> 💡 **Tip:** If the smell persists after multiple treatments, consider professional cleaning or replacing heavily saturated items like carpet padding.

### Step 7: Prevent Future Accidents

Address the root cause of your cat's inappropriate urination. This could be stress, a dirty litter box, a medical issue (consult a vet), or marking behavior. Ensure litter boxes are clean, accessible, and there are enough for all cats in the household (one per cat plus one extra).

> 💡 **Tip:** Provide scratching posts, toys, and plenty of attention to reduce stress. Consider Feliway diffusers or sprays to create a calming environment.


## Tips & Tricks

- Act Fast: The quicker you clean a fresh accident, the easier it is to eliminate the odor.
- Never Use Ammonia-Based Cleaners: Ammonia smells similar to urine and can attract your cat back to the same spot.
- Ventilate Well: Open windows and use fans to air out the area during and after cleaning.
- Check for Hidden Spots: Urine can seep into subflooring, baseboards, or even drywall. A professional may be needed for severe cases.
- Consider a Vet Visit: If your cat is suddenly urinating outside the litter box, it could be a medical issue. A vet check-up is always recommended.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Always test cleaning solutions on an inconspicuous area first to prevent damage or discoloration.
- ⚠️ Wear rubber gloves to protect your hands from urine and cleaning chemicals.
- ⚠️ Ensure good ventilation when using cleaning products.
- ⚠️ Keep pets and children away from treated areas until they are dry and safe.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why does cat pee smell so strong and last so long?**

Cat urine contains uric acid crystals that are very difficult to break down with regular cleaners. When these crystals come into contact with moisture, they reactivate, releasing a strong, pungent ammonia-like odor that can persist for a long time.

**Can I use bleach to clean cat pee?**

No, do not use bleach. Bleach can react with the ammonia in cat urine to create dangerous fumes. It also won't effectively break down the uric acid crystals and can damage surfaces.

**What if the smell is in my couch or mattress?**

For couches and mattresses, follow the same steps: blot, pre-treat with vinegar, and saturate with an enzymatic cleaner, ensuring it reaches as deep as the urine. Allow ample time to air dry completely. You may need to repeat treatments.

**How do enzymatic cleaners work?**

Enzymatic cleaners contain specific enzymes that act as catalysts to break down the organic matter in urine, including the uric acid crystals, proteins, and other odor-causing compounds. This process neutralizes the smell at its source rather than just masking it.

**Will the smell ever truly go away?**

Yes, with proper and thorough cleaning using enzymatic cleaners, the smell can be completely eliminated. Persistence is key, especially for old or deeply saturated stains, sometimes requiring multiple treatments.

**My cat keeps peeing in the same spot. What should I do?**

Thoroughly clean the area with an enzymatic cleaner to remove all traces of odor. Then, address the underlying cause of the inappropriate urination, which could be a medical issue (vet visit), litter box aversion, or stress.

**Can I make my own cat pee odor remover?**

While white vinegar and baking soda can help neutralize some odors, they won't fully break down the uric acid crystals responsible for the deep-seated cat pee smell. For complete removal, a commercial enzymatic cleaner is highly recommended.
