To get rid of cat pee smell, act quickly to blot up urine, then treat the affected area with an enzymatic cleaner designed to break down uric acid crystals. Follow up with thorough cleaning and ventilation to eliminate lingering odors effectively.

## What You Need

- Paper towels or old rags
- Rubber gloves
- Enzymatic cleaner for pet odors
- UV black light (optional, for locating old stains)
- Baking soda (optional)
- Vacuum cleaner

## Steps

### Step 1: Locate the Source of the Odor

Cat pee smell can be tricky to pinpoint, especially if it's dried. Use your nose or a black light (UV light) in a darkened room to find dried urine stains, which will glow. Check carpets, upholstery, walls, and even hard floors, as urine can seep into grout or floorboards. Identifying all affected areas is key to complete odor removal and ensuring you don't miss any hidden spots.

> 💡 **Tip:** A black light can reveal hidden stains that are invisible to the naked eye, making your cleaning efforts much more targeted and effective.

### Step 2: Blot Up Fresh Urine Immediately

If the stain is fresh, immediate action is crucial to prevent the odor from setting in. Wear gloves and use clean paper towels or an old cloth to blot up as much urine as possible. Press firmly, but do not rub, as rubbing can push the urine deeper into the fibers. Continue blotting until no more moisture transfers to the paper towel, ensuring you absorb as much liquid as possible.

### Step 3: Apply an Enzymatic Cleaner

Enzymatic cleaners are specifically formulated to break down the uric acid crystals in cat urine, which are responsible for the lingering odor. Saturate the affected area generously with the enzymatic cleaner, ensuring it reaches as deep as the urine did. Follow the product instructions for application and dwell time. For a comprehensive approach to tackling tough odors, you might find more detailed guidance in [How to Get Rid of Cat Pee Smell Effectively & Permanently](/en/how-to-get-rid-of-cat-pee-smell-permanently).

> 💡 **Tip:** Do not use steam cleaners on cat urine, as the heat can permanently bond the protein to the fibers, making the odor impossible to remove.

### Step 4: Allow the Cleaner to Dwell and Air Dry

After applying the enzymatic cleaner, allow it to sit for the recommended time, usually 10-30 minutes, or even longer for severe odors. This allows the enzymes to fully break down the urine components. Once the dwell time is complete, blot up any excess moisture with clean paper towels. Let the area air dry completely, which may take several hours. Good ventilation can help speed up the drying process.

### Step 5: Rinse and Reapply if Necessary

For stubborn or older stains, a single application might not be enough. Once the area is completely dry, smell it again. If a faint odor persists, you may need to lightly rinse the area with water (blotting thoroughly afterwards) and reapply the enzymatic cleaner. Sometimes, multiple treatments are required to fully eliminate the embedded odor, especially in porous materials.

### Step 6: Deodorize with Baking Soda (Optional)

Once the area is dry and the enzymatic cleaner has done its work, you can sprinkle a generous amount of baking soda over the treated spot. Baking soda is a natural deodorizer that can absorb any residual odors. Let it sit for several hours or overnight, then vacuum it up thoroughly. This step provides an extra layer of freshness and helps to ensure all lingering scents are banished.

### Step 7: Address Lingering Air Odors & Prevent Future Accidents

Even after cleaning the spot, the general air might still carry a faint scent. Open windows, use air purifiers, or place bowls of white vinegar or activated charcoal around the room to absorb airborne odors. To prevent future accidents, ensure your cat's litter box is clean and accessible, and consider if there's an underlying medical or behavioral reason for the urination outside the box. > 💡 **Tip:** Consult a vet if your cat suddenly starts urinating outside the litter box, as it could indicate a health issue requiring professional attention.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always test cleaning solutions on an inconspicuous area first to ensure they don't stain or damage your surfaces.
- Consider investing in a good quality enzymatic cleaner; cheaper alternatives may not be as effective in breaking down uric acid.
- Ensure adequate ventilation when cleaning to disperse fumes and aid drying, helping to clear the air more quickly.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Never use ammonia-based cleaners, as their scent can mimic urine and encourage your cat to mark the spot again.
- ⚠️ Avoid using steam cleaners on cat urine, as the heat can permanently set the odor and stain into fabrics and carpets.
- ⚠️ Always wear gloves when handling urine to maintain hygiene and protect your hands from contaminants.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why is cat pee smell so hard to remove?**

Cat urine contains uric acid crystals that are very difficult to break down with ordinary cleaners. These crystals reactivate with moisture, causing the odor to return even after cleaning. Enzymatic cleaners are designed to specifically target and break down these crystals, offering a more permanent solution.

**Can I use homemade remedies like vinegar or baking soda?**

While white vinegar and baking soda can help neutralize some odors and absorb moisture, they are generally not powerful enough to fully eliminate the uric acid crystals in cat urine. They can be used as a supplementary step after an enzymatic cleaner has been applied, but should not be the primary cleaning agent for cat urine.

**How do I find old, dried cat pee stains?**

Old, dried cat pee stains are often invisible to the naked eye but will fluoresce under a UV black light in a dark room. This tool is invaluable for locating all affected areas that need treatment, ensuring you don't miss any hidden sources of odor.

**What if the cat pee smell is in my wooden floorboards?**

If urine has soaked into wooden floorboards, the process is more challenging. You'll need to thoroughly clean the surface with an enzymatic cleaner, potentially multiple times. For severe cases, sanding and sealing the wood or even replacing affected sections might be necessary to fully eliminate the embedded odor.

**Is the smell harmful to humans or pets?**

While unpleasant, the smell itself is not typically harmful in small amounts. However, prolonged exposure to strong ammonia fumes can be irritating to respiratory systems. The bigger concern is often the underlying reason for the cat's inappropriate urination, which could indicate a health problem that needs veterinary attention.

**How can I prevent my cat from peeing outside the litter box?**

Ensure the litter box is clean, accessible, and there are enough boxes for all cats in the household (one per cat plus one extra). Rule out medical issues with a vet visit. Address any stress factors in the environment, and provide appropriate scratching posts and play opportunities to keep your cat happy and reduce anxiety.

**How long does it take for cat pee smell to go away?**

With immediate and proper treatment using an enzymatic cleaner, the smell can be significantly reduced or eliminated within a few hours to a day. However, for older, deeply set stains, it may require multiple treatments and several days for the odor to completely dissipate as the enzymes work through the uric acid crystals.
