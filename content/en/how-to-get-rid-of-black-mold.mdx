To get rid of black mold, especially on non-porous surfaces, you'll need to wear protective gear and use a suitable cleaning solution like white vinegar or borax. Thoroughly scrub the affected area, ensure it's completely dry, and address the underlying moisture issue to prevent its return.

## What You Need

- N-95 respirator mask
- Rubber gloves
- Eye protection (goggles)
- Spray bottle
- Stiff-bristled scrub brush or old toothbrush
- Microfiber cloths or old rags
- White distilled vinegar
- Borax (optional, for tougher stains)
- Hydrogen peroxide (optional)
- Fan or dehumidifier
- Heavy-duty trash bags

## Steps

### Step 1: Assess the Mold and Prepare the Area

Before you begin, determine the extent of the mold. This guide is for small, surface-level mold patches (less than 10 square feet). If the mold covers a large area, appears after water damage, or you have health concerns, it's best to call a professional. Ensure the area is well-ventilated by opening windows and using a fan. Close off the affected room from other parts of the house to prevent spores from spreading.

### Step 2: Gather Your Safety Gear

Mold spores can be harmful, so personal protection is crucial. Don a N-95 respirator mask to avoid inhaling spores, wear rubber gloves to protect your skin, and put on eye protection (goggles) to prevent spores from irritating your eyes. Do not skip these steps, as exposure to mold can cause respiratory issues or allergic reactions.

### Step 3: Choose and Prepare Your Cleaning Solution

For non-porous surfaces, white distilled vinegar is highly effective. Pour undiluted white vinegar into a spray bottle. For a stronger solution, you can mix one cup of borax with a gallon of hot water, dissolving completely. Hydrogen peroxide (3%) can also be sprayed directly and allowed to sit before scrubbing. Avoid mixing different cleaners, especially bleach with ammonia-based products, as this can create dangerous fumes.

### Step 4: Apply Solution and Scrub the Mold

Generously spray the moldy surface with your chosen cleaning solution. Allow it to sit for at least 15-30 minutes to penetrate the mold and kill the spores. Using a stiff-bristled brush or an old toothbrush for tight spots, vigorously scrub the mold away. Apply firm pressure to ensure you're removing the mold from the surface.

### Step 5: Wipe, Rinse, and Dry Thoroughly

After scrubbing, wipe the area clean with old rags or microfiber cloths. If using borax, there's no need to rinse as it leaves a residue that inhibits future mold growth. For vinegar or hydrogen peroxide, you can lightly rinse the surface with clean water if desired, then immediately dry the area completely. Use a fan or dehumidifier to speed up drying, as any remaining moisture can lead to mold regrowth.

### Step 6: Dispose of Contaminated Materials

Place all used rags, brushes, and any other contaminated disposable materials into a heavy-duty trash bag. Seal the bag tightly and immediately dispose of it outside your home. Clean your reusable safety gear (gloves, goggles) with soap and water, and allow them to air dry.

### Step 7: Prevent Future Mold Growth

The key to preventing mold is controlling moisture. Fix any leaks promptly, improve ventilation in bathrooms and kitchens, and use exhaust fans during and after showers or cooking. Keep indoor humidity levels below 60% using a dehumidifier if necessary. Regularly clean areas prone to moisture, and ensure surfaces are always thoroughly dried.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always test your cleaning solution on an inconspicuous area first to ensure it doesn't damage or discolor the surface.
- For porous materials like drywall or insulation, professional removal is often required as mold roots can grow deep.
- Consider using mold-resistant paint in high-humidity areas like bathrooms and basements.
- Increase air circulation by opening doors between rooms and moving furniture away from walls.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Never attempt to clean large areas of mold (more than 10 square feet) yourself; contact a professional mold remediation specialist.
- ⚠️ Do not mix bleach with ammonia or other household cleaners, as this creates toxic gases.
- ⚠️ If you experience any health symptoms like difficulty breathing, headaches, or skin irritation while cleaning mold, stop immediately and seek fresh air and medical attention.
- ⚠️ Avoid using bleach for mold removal on porous surfaces like wood, as it only kills surface mold and doesn't penetrate to kill the roots.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do you get rid of black mold effectively?**

Effectively getting rid of black mold involves wearing protective gear, using a strong anti-fungal cleaner like undiluted white vinegar or a borax solution, scrubbing the mold away, and ensuring the area is completely dried. Addressing the source of moisture is crucial for long-term prevention.

**What is the best way to get rid of mold on walls?**

To get rid of mold on walls, especially on painted, non-porous surfaces, spray the affected area with white vinegar or a borax solution, let it sit, then scrub thoroughly with a brush. Wipe clean and ensure the wall is completely dry afterwards. For drywall, if the mold has penetrated, replacement might be necessary.

**How to get rid of mold in shower?**

To get rid of mold in the shower, spray the moldy grout, tiles, or caulk with undiluted white vinegar or hydrogen peroxide. Let it sit for at least 30 minutes, then scrub with a stiff brush. Rinse thoroughly and dry the shower completely, improving ventilation to prevent reoccurrence.

**Can I remove black mold myself?**

Yes, you can remove small patches of black mold (less than 10 square feet) yourself if it's on a non-porous surface and you take proper safety precautions. For larger areas, recurring mold, or mold on porous materials, it's safer and more effective to hire a professional.

**How to get rid of mold on wood?**

To get rid of mold on wood, gently scrub the surface with a solution of borax and water or a diluted white vinegar solution. Avoid over-saturating the wood. After scrubbing, wipe the area dry and use a fan to ensure the wood is completely moisture-free, as wood is porous and prone to deep mold penetration.

**What kills black mold instantly?**

While no solution truly kills black mold 'instantly' and completely without scrubbing, undiluted white vinegar, hydrogen peroxide, or a borax solution are highly effective at killing mold spores and roots when applied and allowed to sit. Thorough scrubbing and drying are essential for complete removal.

**How do you prevent black mold from coming back?**

Preventing black mold from returning involves eliminating moisture sources, improving ventilation, and maintaining low indoor humidity (below 60%). Promptly fix leaks, use exhaust fans, and ensure all surfaces are thoroughly dried after cleaning or exposure to water.
