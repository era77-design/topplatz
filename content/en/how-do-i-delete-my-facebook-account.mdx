Deleting your Facebook account is a permanent action that removes all your data, photos, and posts from the platform. Alternatively, you can deactivate your account for a temporary break. This guide will walk you through the process for permanent deletion, ensuring you understand the implications.

## What You Need

- Active Facebook account
- Internet access
- Computer or mobile device

## Steps

### Step 1: Understand Deactivation vs. Permanent Deletion

Before you begin, it's crucial to understand the difference. Deactivating your account temporarily hides your profile, posts, and comments, but you can reactivate it at any time. Permanent deletion, however, removes all your data irrevocably after a grace period. Make sure you are certain about choosing permanent deletion.

> 💡 **Tip:** If you're unsure, try deactivating your account first to see if a temporary break is sufficient.

### Step 2: Backup Your Facebook Data (Optional but Recommended)

Facebook allows you to download a copy of your information, including photos, posts, and messages, before you delete your account. This is a crucial step if you want to retain any memories or data from your time on the platform. To do this, go to 'Settings & Privacy' > 'Settings' > 'Your Facebook Information' > 'Download Your Information' and follow the prompts.

> 💡 **Tip:** Download your data to a secure location on your computer or an external drive.

### Step 3: Navigate to Settings & Privacy

On a computer, click the down arrow icon in the top right corner of Facebook, then select 'Settings & Privacy,' followed by 'Settings.' On a mobile device, tap the three-line menu icon, scroll down, and tap 'Settings & Privacy,' then 'Settings.' This will take you to the main settings page where you can manage your account.

> 💡 **Tip:** The exact menu wording might vary slightly between Facebook versions or devices, but 'Settings & Privacy' is always the starting point.

### Step 4: Find Account Ownership and Control

Within the 'Settings' menu, look for a section or option called 'Account Ownership and Control.' On a computer, this is usually found in the left-hand navigation pane. On mobile, you might need to scroll down further in the settings list. Click or tap on this option to proceed to the next stage of managing your account's status.

> 💡 **Tip:** If you can't find it, use the search bar within the settings menu and type 'delete account' or 'ownership'.

### Step 5: Choose Deactivation and Deletion

Under 'Account Ownership and Control,' you will see an option titled 'Deactivation and Deletion.' Click or tap on this to open a new page presenting you with two choices: 'Deactivate Account' or 'Delete Account.' Select 'Delete Account' to proceed with permanent removal. Facebook will then prompt you to continue to account deletion.

> 💡 **Tip:** Read the information provided by Facebook carefully at this stage to confirm your choice.

### Step 6: Confirm Permanent Account Deletion

After selecting 'Delete Account,' Facebook will ask you to confirm your decision and may offer reasons why you're leaving or suggest deactivation instead. You'll need to enter your password to finalize the request. Once confirmed, your account will be scheduled for permanent deletion, typically after a 30-day grace period.

> 💡 **Tip:** Log out of all devices immediately after requesting deletion to avoid accidentally logging back in during the grace period.

### Step 7: Avoid Logging In During the Grace Period

After you request deletion, your account enters a grace period (usually 30 days) during which it is invisible to others but can still be canceled if you log back in. To ensure permanent deletion, do not log into your Facebook account or use any associated Facebook services (like Messenger) during this entire period. After the grace period, your account and all its data will be permanently deleted.

> 💡 **Tip:** Consider deleting the Facebook app from your phone to remove the temptation to log back in.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always download your personal data before deleting any social media account.
- Review all apps and services linked to your Facebook account and update their login methods before deletion.
- Inform friends or family on Facebook that you are leaving the platform if you wish to maintain contact outside of it.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Permanent deletion means all your posts, photos, videos, and other data will be erased and cannot be recovered.
- ⚠️ Some messages you sent to friends may remain visible in their inboxes, even after your account is deleted.
- ⚠️ If you log into your account during the 30-day grace period, your deletion request will be canceled, and you'll have to start the process over.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I delete my Facebook account permanently?**

To permanently delete your Facebook account, go to 'Settings & Privacy' > 'Settings' > 'Account Ownership and Control' > 'Deactivation and Deletion,' then select 'Delete Account' and follow the prompts to confirm your decision.

**What is the difference between deactivating and deleting my Facebook account?**

Deactivating your account temporarily hides your profile and content, allowing you to reactivate it later. Deleting your account is permanent, removing all your data after a grace period, and cannot be undone once completed.

**How can I delete an old Facebook account I no longer use?**

To delete an old Facebook account, you must first regain access to it. Use the 'Forgot Password' option if needed. Once logged in, follow the standard permanent deletion steps via 'Settings & Privacy' > 'Account Ownership and Control'.

**Can I delete my Facebook account on a computer?**

Yes, you can absolutely delete your Facebook account on a computer. Navigate to 'Settings & Privacy' from the top-right menu, then proceed to 'Settings' and 'Account Ownership and Control' to initiate the deletion process.

**What happens to my data after I delete my Facebook account?**

After the 30-day grace period, Facebook permanently deletes your account and all associated data, including photos, posts, and videos. Some data, like messages sent to others, might remain visible to the recipients.

**Is there a grace period after I request to delete my Facebook account?**

Yes, Facebook typically provides a 30-day grace period after you request to delete your account. During this time, your account is hidden, but you can cancel the deletion request by logging back in.

**Can I recover my account after I've deleted it?**

Once your account has been permanently deleted after the 30-day grace period, it cannot be recovered. All your data will be gone forever, so ensure you're certain before proceeding.
