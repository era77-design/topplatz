To get rid of cat pee smell, you must promptly clean the affected area using an enzymatic cleaner specifically designed for pet odors. These cleaners break down uric acid crystals, which are responsible for the lingering smell. ## What You Need

- Paper towels or old rags
- Rubber gloves
- Enzymatic pet odor cleaner
- White vinegar (optional, for laundry or pre-treatment)
- Baking soda
- Spray bottle (optional)
- UV blacklight (optional, for locating old stains)
- Stiff brush (for carpets)
- Wet/dry vacuum or carpet cleaner (optional)

## Steps

### Step 1: Locate All Affected Areas

Even if you know the general location, cat pee can spread, or there might be multiple spots. Use your nose to sniff out faint odors, or for dried, invisible stains, employ a UV blacklight in a darkened room. The uric acid crystals will glow under the UV light, revealing all affected surfaces.

> 💡 **Tip:** Old stains can be particularly stubborn. Thoroughly identifying all contaminated areas is crucial for complete odor removal.

### Step 2: Blot Fresh Urine Promptly

For fresh urine, immediate action is key to preventing deep saturation. Put on rubber gloves and use a stack of clean paper towels or old rags to blot up as much of the liquid as possible. Press down firmly, but do not rub, as rubbing can spread the urine and push it deeper into fibers. Replace saturated towels until no more liquid is absorbed.

### Step 3: Apply Enzymatic Cleaner Generously

Once excess urine is blotted, liberally apply a high-quality enzymatic pet odor cleaner to the affected area. These cleaners contain enzymes that break down the uric acid crystals in cat urine, which are the primary cause of the persistent smell. Ensure the cleaner thoroughly saturates the area, reaching as deep as the urine penetrated, especially on carpets or upholstery.

### Step 4: Allow the Cleaner to Work

Follow the instructions on your enzymatic cleaner for the recommended dwell time. This typically ranges from 15 minutes to several hours, or even overnight, depending on the product and the severity of the stain. Do not allow the area to dry out completely during this period; you can cover it with plastic wrap to keep it moist if needed.

### Step 5: Blot and Extract Excess Cleaner

After the cleaner has had sufficient time to work, use clean paper towels or a wet/dry vacuum to blot or extract the excess liquid. Again, press firmly without rubbing. If using a wet/dry vacuum or carpet cleaner, follow the manufacturer's instructions for removing cleaning solutions. This step helps to lift away the broken-down urine residue and cleaner.

### Step 6: Air Dry and Ventilate Thoroughly

Allow the treated area to air dry completely. This can take several hours or even a day or two, depending on humidity and ventilation. Open windows, use fans, or a dehumidifier to speed up the drying process and improve air circulation. Proper ventilation helps to dissipate any remaining airborne odors and ensures the area is fully dry before assessing the results. For a comprehensive strategy on ensuring complete odor elimination, consult our article [How to Get Rid of Cat Pee Smell: A Complete Guide](/en/how-to-get-rid-of-cat-pee-smell-guide).

### Step 7: Re-evaluate and Repeat if Necessary

Once the area is completely dry, perform a smell test. If a faint odor persists, it indicates that some uric acid crystals remain. You may need to repeat the application of the enzymatic cleaner, ensuring deeper saturation or a longer dwell time. For very old or stubborn stains, multiple treatments might be required to fully eradicate the smell.

> 💡 **Tip:** Consider sprinkling baking soda over the dried area overnight and vacuuming it up the next day for an extra odor-absorbing boost.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always test any cleaning solution on an inconspicuous area first to ensure it doesn't cause discoloration or damage.
- Never use ammonia-based cleaners, as their smell is similar to urine and can attract your cat to pee in the same spot again.
- Address the root cause of your cat's inappropriate urination, such as litter box issues, stress, or a medical condition, to prevent future accidents.
- For severe or widespread carpet saturation, consider renting a professional-grade carpet cleaner or hiring a professional cleaning service.
- Ensure you apply enough enzymatic cleaner to fully saturate the entire area where the urine has spread, not just the surface.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why does cat pee smell so much worse than other pet urine?**

Cat urine contains high concentrations of uric acid, which forms crystals upon drying. These crystals are very difficult to break down with water alone and reactivate in humid conditions, releasing a pungent, long-lasting odor. Bacteria also contribute to the strong smell by breaking down urea.

**Can I use household cleaners like bleach or ammonia to remove cat pee smell?**

No, you should avoid using bleach or ammonia. Bleach is too harsh and can damage surfaces, while ammonia has a smell similar to cat urine, which can actually attract your cat to urinate in the same spot again. Enzymatic cleaners are specifically formulated to break down the uric acid crystals.

**How can I remove cat pee smell from washable fabrics or bedding?**

Pre-soak the items in a solution of white vinegar and water (1:1 ratio) for 30 minutes, then wash them in cold water with an enzymatic laundry additive. Air dry the items if possible, as heat from a dryer can set in residual odors. Repeat if the smell persists.

**Will the cat pee smell ever truly go away?**

Yes, with proper and thorough cleaning using an enzymatic cleaner, the cat pee smell can be completely eliminated. The key is to fully neutralize the uric acid crystals, which are responsible for the lingering odor. Multiple treatments may be necessary for old or deeply saturated stains.

**What if the cat pee has soaked into hardwood floors or subflooring?**

For hardwood, clean the surface promptly with an enzymatic cleaner and dry thoroughly. If the urine has penetrated the finish or subfloor, you might need to sand and refinish the wood, or in severe cases, replace affected subflooring. Sealing the subfloor with an odor-blocking primer after cleaning can help.

**How can I prevent my cat from peeing outside the litter box?**

Address potential causes such as medical issues (e.g., UTIs), litter box cleanliness (scoop daily, change litter often), litter type preference, location of the litter box, or stress factors. Consult with your vet or a feline behaviorist if the problem persists. Providing multiple clean litter boxes in quiet locations can also help.

**Is baking soda effective for cat pee smell removal?**

Baking soda is an excellent odor absorber and can help with residual smells after an enzymatic cleaner has been used. Sprinkle it generously over the dried, cleaned area, let it sit overnight, and then vacuum it up. However, it does not break down uric acid crystals, so it shouldn't be used as a primary cleaner for urine.
