Ugg boots, known for their cozy comfort, often get dirty from daily wear. Learning how to clean Uggs properly at home can extend their life and keep them looking fresh. With a few simple tools and techniques, you can restore your beloved sheepskin boots.

## What You Need

- Soft-bristle brush (or Ugg brush kit)
- Clean cloths or sponges
- Cold water
- Sheepskin cleaner (specifically for Uggs, optional)
- White vinegar (optional, for stains)
- Baking soda (optional, for odor)
- Paper towels or shoe trees (to maintain shape)
- Ugg protector spray (optional)

## Steps

### Step 1: Remove Loose Dirt and Debris

Use a soft-bristle brush (or an Ugg-specific brush) to gently brush the entire surface of your boots when they are dry. This helps to remove any loose dirt, dust, or mud that might be clinging to the suede, making subsequent cleaning steps more effective. Brush in one direction to maintain the nap of the suede.

### Step 2: Dampen the Boots Evenly

Lightly dampen the entire outer surface of your Ugg boots with cold water using a clean sponge or cloth. Avoid soaking them, as excessive water can damage the sheepskin. The goal is to make the suede uniformly moist, which helps prevent water spots and prepares the material for cleaning.

### Step 3: Apply Cleaner and Gently Scrub

Apply a small amount of Ugg cleaner (or a diluted solution of white vinegar and water for a DIY approach) to your damp sponge or cloth. Gently scrub the entire surface of the boots, focusing on any soiled areas or stains. Use light, circular motions, taking care not to rub too aggressively, which could damage the delicate suede.

### Step 4: Rinse and Blot Excess Water

Using a separate clean, damp cloth, gently wipe away any excess cleaner or residue from the boots. Ensure all suds are removed. Then, use a clean, dry towel to blot the boots, absorbing as much moisture as possible. This step is crucial for even drying and preventing water marks.

### Step 5: Stuff and Air Dry Properly

To help your Uggs maintain their shape while drying, stuff them loosely with paper towels or use shoe trees. Place the boots in a well-ventilated area, away from direct sunlight or any heat sources like radiators or hair dryers. Allow them to air dry completely, which can take 12-24 hours, ensuring they are thoroughly dry before the next step.

### Step 6: Restore the Suede Nap

Once the boots are completely dry, use your soft-bristle brush to gently brush the suede in a single direction again. This step helps to restore the natural nap and softness of the suede, making it look uniform and fresh. It also helps to fluff up any flattened fibers.

### Step 7: Apply a Protective Spray (Optional)

For added protection against future stains and water damage, consider applying a sheepskin protector spray designed for Uggs. Follow the product instructions carefully, typically spraying evenly from a distance and allowing the boots to dry completely. This creates a barrier that helps repel liquids and makes future cleaning easier.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always test cleaners on an inconspicuous area first.
- Never put Uggs in a washing machine or dryer.
- Use a dry brush regularly to prevent dirt buildup.
- Stuff boots with paper to maintain shape while drying.
- Address stains as soon as possible for best results.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Avoid direct heat (like radiators or hair dryers) as it can damage the suede and sheepskin.
- ⚠️ Do not use harsh chemical cleaners, as they can strip natural oils and damage the material.
- ⚠️ Over-wetting the boots can lead to water damage and discoloration.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do you clean Uggs at home?**

To clean Uggs at home, start by brushing off loose dirt, then dampen the boots evenly with cold water. Gently scrub with a sheepskin cleaner or a diluted vinegar solution, wipe clean, and blot dry. Finish by air drying and brushing the nap.

**What materials do I need to clean my Uggs?**

You'll need a soft-bristle brush, clean cloths or sponges, cold water, and optionally, a specialized sheepskin cleaner. For tough stains or odors, white vinegar and baking soda can also be useful.

**Can I put my Uggs in the washing machine?**

No, you should never put your Ugg boots in a washing machine or dryer. The harsh agitation, detergents, and heat can severely damage the delicate sheepskin and suede, leading to shrinking, cracking, and discoloration.

**How do I remove tough stains from Uggs?**

For tough stains, try applying a small amount of white vinegar diluted with water directly to the stain with a clean cloth, gently dabbing. For oil-based stains, sprinkle a bit of cornstarch or baking soda, let it sit for several hours to absorb, then brush off.

**How often should I clean my Ugg boots?**

The frequency depends on how often you wear them and the conditions. For regular wear, a light cleaning every few months is good, with deeper cleaning as needed for visible dirt or stains. Regular brushing helps maintain them between cleanings.

**How do I dry my Uggs after cleaning?**

After cleaning, stuff your Uggs loosely with paper towels to help them keep their shape. Air dry them completely in a well-ventilated area, away from direct sunlight or any artificial heat sources, which can damage the material.

**Can I use regular soap to clean Uggs?**

It's generally not recommended to use regular soap or detergents on Uggs, as they can strip the natural oils from the sheepskin and suede, potentially causing damage or leaving residue. It's best to use a cleaner specifically designed for sheepskin or a mild, diluted solution of white vinegar.
