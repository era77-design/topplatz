To mark all emails as read in Gmail, you typically need to select all visible emails in a view (like your inbox or a search result), then use the "Mark as read" option from the toolbar. This helps quickly clear your notification count and organize your inbox. This process works for both desktop browsers and the Gmail mobile app.

## What You Need

- Gmail account
- Internet access
- Web browser or Gmail app

## Steps

### Step 1: Access Your Gmail Inbox

Open your preferred web browser and navigate to mail.google.com, then log in to your Gmail account. Ensure you are in the desired folder or category, such as your Primary inbox, Social, Promotions, or 'All Mail' view. If you don't yet have an account, you can learn [How to Create a Gmail Account Quickly and Easily](/en/how-to-create-gmail-account).

> 💡 **Tip:** For mobile, open the Gmail app and sign in.

### Step 2: Select All Visible Emails on the Page

Once in your inbox or chosen folder, locate the small checkbox icon at the top left of your email list, just above the first message. Click this checkbox. This action will select all emails currently displayed on the page, which is typically 50 conversations by default.

### Step 3: Select All Emails in the Folder

After clicking the initial checkbox, a message will appear above your emails, stating something like "All 50 conversations on this page are selected." Crucially, immediately following this, you will see a link that says "Select all [number] conversations in [folder name]." Click this second link to select *all* emails within that specific folder or category, not just the ones visible on the current page.

### Step 4: Click "Mark as read"

With all desired emails selected, look for the toolbar located above your emails. In this toolbar, you'll find various action icons. Locate and click the "Mark as read" icon, which typically looks like an open envelope or an eye. This action will change the status of all selected emails.

### Step 5: Confirm the Action (If Prompted)

For very large numbers of emails, Gmail might display a confirmation dialog box asking if you are sure you want to perform this bulk action. If prompted, click "OK" or "Confirm" to proceed. The selected emails will then be marked as read, and your unread count should decrease significantly or disappear entirely.

### Step 6: Repeat for Other Categories (Optional)

If you utilize Gmail's tabbed inbox (Primary, Social, Promotions, Updates, Forums), you might need to repeat these steps for each tab to mark all emails across your entire inbox as read. Simply navigate to each category tab and follow the same selection and marking process. This ensures no unread emails are left in other sections.


## Tips & Tricks

- To quickly find only unread emails, type "is:unread" into the Gmail search bar.
- You can mark specific emails as read by selecting them individually (by clicking their checkboxes) and then clicking the "Mark as read" button.
- Use the "All Mail" view in the left-hand menu to see every email in your account, regardless of its folder or category, and mark them all as read from there.
- On the Gmail mobile app, tap the three horizontal lines (menu icon), then tap "All mail." Tap the profile picture next to an email to select it, then tap the top checkbox to select all, and finally, the "Mark as read" icon (open envelope) at the top.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Marking all emails as read will remove the unread status from important messages. Ensure you've reviewed critical emails before performing a bulk "mark as read" action, especially if you rely on the unread status for prioritization.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I undo "Mark all as read" in Gmail?**

Yes, immediately after marking emails as read, you'll usually see an "Undo" option appear briefly at the bottom left of your screen. Click it quickly to revert the action for the last performed bulk operation.

**How do I mark all emails as unread again?**

To mark emails as unread, first select the emails you wish to change (or select all again). Then, click the "Mark as unread" icon in the toolbar, which often looks like a closed envelope or a similar symbol.

**Does marking all emails as read delete them?**

No, marking emails as read only changes their status from unread to read. It does not delete them, archive them, or move them to the trash. They will remain in your inbox or whichever folder they were in.

**Can I mark emails as read on the Gmail mobile app?**

Yes, you can. In the Gmail app, navigate to the desired folder, then tap on the profile picture next to an email to select it. Once one is selected, a checkbox will appear at the top, which you can tap to select all visible emails, then use the "Mark as read" icon.

**Why does my unread count not go down after marking all as read?**

This often happens if you only marked emails in one specific category (e.g., Primary) or only the emails visible on a single page. Make sure you click the link that says "Select all [number] conversations in [folder name]" to apply the action to all emails in that view, and check other category tabs.

**Is there a keyboard shortcut to mark emails as read?**

Yes, if you have keyboard shortcuts enabled in your Gmail settings, you can select one or more emails and then press 'Shift + I' on your keyboard to mark them as read. This can be a quick way to manage your inbox once you're familiar with it.

**How do I mark all emails in a specific label as read?**

First, click on the desired label from the left-hand menu to view all emails within that label. Then, follow the same steps: click the top checkbox to select all visible emails, then click the prompt to "Select all conversations in [label name]", and finally, click the "Mark as read" button.
