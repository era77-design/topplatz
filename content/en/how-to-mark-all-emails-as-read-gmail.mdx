To mark all emails as read in Gmail, you can use the 'Select all' checkbox at the top of your inbox, then click the 'Select all conversations that match this search' link, and finally click the 'Mark as read' icon. This action helps declutter your inbox and ensures you only focus on new, unread messages.

## What You Need

- Gmail account
- Computer or mobile device with internet access

## Steps

### Step 1: Open Gmail in Your Web Browser

Start by opening your preferred web browser and navigating to mail.google.com. Log in to your Gmail account using your email address and password. If you don't yet have an account, you can quickly learn [How to Create a Gmail Account Quickly and Easily](/en/how-to-create-gmail-account).

### Step 2: Navigate to Your Inbox or Specific Folder

Once logged in, you'll see your main inbox. You can choose to mark emails as read in your primary inbox, or navigate to other categories like 'Social' or 'Promotions' by clicking their respective tabs. This method also works for custom labels or search results.

### Step 3: Select All Visible Emails

Locate the small square checkbox at the top left of your email list, just above the first message. Click this checkbox. This action will select all conversations currently visible on the first page of your inbox or selected folder.

### Step 4: Select All Conversations (Beyond the First Page)

After clicking the top checkbox, a message will appear above your emails stating, 'All X conversations on this page are selected.' Crucially, to mark ALL emails (not just those on the current page) as read, look for an additional link within this message that says, 'Select all X conversations that match this search.' Click this link.

### Step 5: Mark Selected Emails as Read

Once all conversations are selected, look for the 'Mark as read' icon in the toolbar that appears above your email list. This icon typically looks like an open envelope. Click on it to initiate the action of changing the status of all selected emails to 'read'.

### Step 6: Confirm the Action

Depending on the number of emails, Gmail might ask for confirmation, especially if you're processing a very large batch. A small pop-up or notification will appear at the bottom left, indicating that the action is being processed. Wait for this process to complete.

### Step 7: Review Your Inbox

After the process is complete, your inbox or the selected folder should now show all the previously unread emails as read. The bold formatting that typically indicates an unread email will be gone. You can then continue managing your inbox with a fresh start.


## Tips & Tricks

- Use Gmail's powerful search operators (e.g., `is:unread before:2023/01/01`) to mark specific groups of old emails as read.
- Be cautious when marking all emails as read; ensure you don't miss important unread messages by mistake.
- This method is effective for any custom label or search result, allowing for targeted inbox management.
- If you accidentally mark emails as read, a small 'Undo' notification appears at the bottom left immediately after the action. Click it quickly to revert.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Marking a large number of emails as read simultaneously cannot be easily undone for individual messages. Always double-check your selection before confirming, especially if you have crucial unread emails you might miss.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I mark all emails as read on the Gmail mobile app?**

Yes, but the process is slightly different. You typically long-press an email to select it, then tap the top checkbox (or 'Select All' option) for all visible, and then the three-dot menu for 'Mark as read'. The option to select all conversations beyond the current screen might vary or require a search function first.

**What if I only want to mark emails from a specific sender as read?**

Use the search bar at the top of Gmail to find emails from that sender (e.g., `from:sender@example.com`). Once the search results appear, follow steps 3-5 to select all matching conversations and mark them as read.

**Can I undo marking emails as read?**

Immediately after marking emails as read, a small 'Undo' notification appears at the bottom left of your screen. If you click it quickly, the action will be reverted. If you miss this window, there isn't a direct 'unmark all as unread' button, but you can search for them and mark them unread individually or in smaller batches.

**Does marking emails as read delete them?**

No, marking emails as read only changes their status from 'unread' to 'read'. They remain in your inbox or the folder/label they were in, unless you explicitly choose to delete them.

**Why would I want to mark all emails as read?**

People often mark all emails as read to declutter an overwhelmed inbox, especially if they have thousands of old unread messages they no longer need to review. It helps create a clean slate, allowing you to focus on truly new and important incoming emails.

**Does this process work for all Gmail categories (Primary, Social, Promotions)?**

Yes, you can navigate to any of your Gmail category tabs (Primary, Social, Promotions, Updates, Forums) and apply the same steps. The 'Select all conversations that match this search' link will then apply to all emails within that specific category.

**Can I mark only a selection of emails as read?**

Absolutely. Instead of clicking the top 'Select all' checkbox, you can manually click the checkbox next to each individual email you wish to mark as read. Once your desired emails are selected, then click the 'Mark as read' icon.
