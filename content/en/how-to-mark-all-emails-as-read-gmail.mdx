Marking all emails as read in Gmail helps you quickly clear your inbox of notifications and focus on new, unread messages. This process is useful for decluttering, especially after a vacation or when you want a fresh start. You can achieve this using a few simple clicks on desktop or taps on mobile, making inbox management much easier.

## What You Need

- Computer or mobile device with internet access
- Active Gmail account

## Steps

### Step 1: Open Gmail in Your Browser (Desktop)

Begin by opening your preferred web browser and navigating to gmail.com. If you aren't already logged in, enter your Gmail username and password to access your inbox. This ensures you're on the main desktop interface, which offers the most comprehensive options for bulk actions.

### Step 2: Select All Visible Emails

Once in your inbox, locate the small checkbox at the top-left of your email list, just above the 'Compose' button. Clicking this checkbox will select all emails currently visible on the page (typically 50 conversations). You'll see a notification indicating how many conversations are selected.

### Step 3: Mark All Selected Emails as Read

After selecting the visible emails, a new toolbar will appear above your inbox. Look for the 'Mark as read' icon, which resembles an open envelope. Click this icon to instantly change the status of all selected emails from unread to read. This action will apply only to the emails on the current page.

### Step 4: Mark All Emails on *All Pages* as Read (If Applicable)

If you have more than 50 unread emails, Gmail will display a message after you select the first 50, such as 'All 50 conversations on this page are selected.' Crucially, it will then offer a link: 'Select all conversations that match this search.' Click this link to extend your selection to *all* unread emails in your current view, across all pages. Then, click the 'Mark as read' icon again to apply the action to the entire set.

### Step 5: Mark Emails as Read in the Gmail Mobile App

For mobile users, open the Gmail app on your smartphone or tablet. In your inbox, tap and hold on the first email you wish to mark as read until a checkmark appears. Continue tapping on other emails to select them individually. Once you've selected all desired emails, tap the 'Mark as read' icon (the open envelope) at the top of the screen. The mobile app doesn't have a 'select all' for *all* emails like the desktop version, so you'll need to select them in batches or use the desktop method for a full sweep.

### Step 6: Verify Your Inbox Status

After performing the 'Mark as read' action, take a moment to refresh your inbox or navigate to a different label and then back to your inbox. You should now see that all previously unread emails are displayed without bolding, indicating they are now marked as read. This final check confirms the process was successful and your inbox is decluttered.


## Tips & Tricks

- To quickly mark a *single* email as read, simply click on it to open it, or hover over it in your inbox and click the 'Mark as read' icon that appears on the right.
- Use Gmail's search filters (e.g., `is:unread`) to view only unread messages, making it easier to manage them before marking all as read.
- Consider archiving or deleting emails after marking them as read to further declutter your inbox, especially for promotional or unimportant messages.
- If you accidentally mark important emails as read, look for the 'Undo' notification that briefly appears after the action, or manually mark them as unread again.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Be cautious when marking all emails as read, as you might inadvertently skip over important messages. Always review your inbox or use filters if you're concerned about missing something critical.
- ⚠️ Marking all emails as read does not delete them; they remain in your inbox or archives. If you wish to remove them, you'll need to use the 'Delete' or 'Archive' functions separately.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I undo marking all emails as read?**

Yes, immediately after marking emails as read, a small 'Undo' notification will appear at the bottom-left of your screen. Clicking this will revert the action. If you miss the undo option, you'll need to manually search for and mark specific emails as unread again.

**Does marking emails as read delete them?**

No, marking emails as read only changes their status from unread to read. The emails remain in your inbox or whichever folder they were located in. If you want to remove emails from your inbox, you'll need to use the 'Archive' or 'Delete' functions.

**How can I mark only specific emails as read?**

To mark only specific emails as read, simply click the checkbox next to each individual email you wish to select. Once you've chosen all the desired messages, then click the 'Mark as read' icon in the toolbar above your inbox.

**What if I have thousands of unread emails?**

For a very large number of emails, the desktop method of selecting all conversations that match the search (`is:unread`) is the most efficient. This ensures all emails, even those across multiple pages, are selected and can be marked as read in one bulk action.

**Can I mark emails as read for a specific label or folder?**

Yes, absolutely. First, navigate to the specific label (e.g., 'Promotions,' 'Social,' or a custom label) you want to clear. Once inside that label's view, follow the same steps as marking all emails as read in your main inbox. The action will only apply to emails within that particular label.

**Do I need a Gmail account to follow this guide?**

Yes, these instructions are specifically for managing emails within the Gmail platform. If you don't already have one, you'll need to [How to Create a Gmail Account Quickly and Easily](/en/how-to-create-gmail-account) before you can mark emails as read.

**How do I mark emails as unread again?**

To mark emails as unread, select the desired emails using the checkboxes, just as you would to mark them as read. Then, look for the 'Mark as unread' icon in the toolbar, which typically looks like a closed envelope. Click this icon to revert their status.
