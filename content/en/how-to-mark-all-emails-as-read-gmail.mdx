To mark all emails as read in Gmail, you can use the desktop interface to select all messages in a view and then apply the "Mark as read" action. This process helps you quickly clear your unread count and manage your inbox more efficiently. You can also achieve this on mobile by selecting multiple emails.

## What You Need

- A computer with internet access
- A smartphone or tablet (for mobile methods)
- An active Gmail account

## Steps

### Step 1: Access Gmail on Desktop

Open your web browser and go to mail.google.com. Log in to your Gmail account if you haven't already; if you need one, learn [How to Create a Gmail Account Quickly and Easily](/en/how-to-create-gmail-account). This is the starting point for managing your emails and accessing all the powerful features Gmail offers for inbox organization.

### Step 2: Select All Visible Emails

From your inbox, locate the checkbox at the top left, just above your email list. Click this checkbox to select all emails currently visible on the page. This initial selection is crucial for applying bulk actions to your messages.

### Step 3: Select All Emails in Your Inbox (Advanced)

After clicking the initial checkbox, a message will appear at the top, typically stating, "All X conversations on this page are selected." Crucially, you'll also see a link that says, "Select all X conversations in Primary" (or your current category). Click this link to expand your selection beyond the current page and encompass all emails in that specific inbox view.

### Step 4: Mark Selected Emails as Read

Once all desired emails are selected, look for the "Mark as read" icon in the toolbar above your emails. This icon typically looks like an open envelope. Click it to change the status of all selected messages from unread to read. Gmail will confirm the action, and your unread count will update.

### Step 5: Confirm the Action

A pop-up might appear asking you to confirm the bulk action. Click "OK" or "Confirm" to proceed. Depending on the number of emails, this process may take a moment to complete, but once done, your inbox will reflect the changes, showing a zero unread count if all were marked.

### Step 6: Mark Emails as Read on Mobile (Gmail App)

Open the Gmail app on your smartphone or tablet. Tap and hold on an email to select it, then tap on other emails to select multiple. Once selected, tap the three-dot menu icon (or similar) and choose "Mark as read" from the options. This method is ideal for managing a smaller number of unread emails on the go.


## Tips & Tricks

- Periodically review your inbox categories (Primary, Social, Promotions, Updates, Forums) to ensure you're marking all relevant emails as read, not just those in your primary tab.
- Consider using Gmail's search function (e.g., `is:unread in:inbox`) to filter for specific unread emails before marking them, giving you more granular control.
- For even quicker inbox management, explore setting up filters in Gmail to automatically mark certain types of incoming emails as read or move them to specific labels.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Be cautious when marking all emails as read, especially if you have a large volume of unread messages, as you might miss important information. Once marked, there's no 'undo all' button for this specific action, though you can individually mark emails as unread.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I mark all emails as read in a specific Gmail label or folder?**

Yes, you can. Navigate to the desired label or folder in the left sidebar, then follow the same steps as marking all emails in your inbox: select all visible, then click the "Select all conversations in X" link, and finally, click the "Mark as read" icon.

**What happens to my emails after I mark them as read?**

Marking emails as read simply changes their status from unread to read. They remain in your inbox or their respective labels/folders. This action doesn't archive, delete, or move them; it only updates their visual status.

**Is there a way to mark all emails as read on the Gmail mobile app at once?**

The Gmail mobile app doesn't have a direct "select all conversations" option like the desktop version. You can only select multiple emails by tapping and holding, then tapping others, and then marking them as read. For a full sweep, the desktop interface is more efficient.

**Can I undo marking all emails as read?**

There isn't a single "undo all mark as read" button. If you marked a large batch, you would need to manually select and mark individual emails as unread again. Exercise caution when performing bulk actions to avoid missing important messages.

**Why do I still have unread emails after marking all as read?**

This can happen if new emails arrive immediately after you perform the action, or if you only marked emails in a specific category (like Primary) and still have unread messages in other categories (Social, Promotions). Check all your inbox tabs and labels.

**Does marking emails as read delete them?**

No, marking emails as read does not delete them. It only changes their status from "unread" (usually bolded) to "read" (normal text). The emails will remain in your inbox or whatever folder they were in until you explicitly delete or archive them.
