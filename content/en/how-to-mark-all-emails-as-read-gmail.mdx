To mark all emails as read in Gmail, navigate to your desired inbox or category, select the checkbox at the top left to select all visible emails, then click the "Select all conversations that match this search" link (if present), and finally click the "Mark as read" icon (an open envelope). This helps you quickly clear your unread count.

## What You Need

- Computer or mobile device
- Internet connection
- A Gmail account

## Steps

### Step 1: Access Your Gmail Account

Open your preferred web browser and go to mail.google.com. Log in using your Gmail address and password if you aren't already. If you're new to Gmail or need to set up an account, you can learn [How to Create a New Gmail Account - Step-by-Step Guide](/en/how-to-create-new-gmail-account). Ensure you are logged into the correct account where you wish to mark emails as read.

### Step 2: Choose Your Inbox or Category

Decide which set of emails you want to mark as read. You can apply this action to your primary inbox, promotions tab, social tab, or even specific search results. Navigate to the relevant tab on the left sidebar or perform a search to filter your emails accordingly.

### Step 3: Select All Visible Emails

Locate the small square checkbox positioned at the very top of your email list, typically just below the search bar. Click this checkbox once. This action will select all the emails currently displayed on your screen, highlighting them for the next step.

### Step 4: Extend Selection to All Emails in Category (if applicable)

If you have more emails than what is shown on the current page, after clicking the top checkbox, a message will appear above your emails saying "All X conversations on this page are selected." Immediately next to this, click the link that states "Select all Y conversations that match this search" (where Y is the total number). This crucial step ensures you select every email within that category or search result, not just those visible.

### Step 5: Mark Emails as Read

Once your desired emails are selected, look for the toolbar that appears above your email list. Click the "Mark as read" icon, which typically looks like an open envelope. This action will change the status of all selected emails from unread to read, removing the bold formatting and decreasing your unread count.

### Step 6: Confirm the Action

For very large numbers of emails, Gmail might display a confirmation pop-up asking if you are sure you want to perform this bulk action. Click "OK" or "Confirm" to proceed with marking the emails as read. The unread count for that specific category should now disappear or significantly decrease, indicating a cleaner inbox.

### Step 7: Repeat for Other Categories (Optional)

If your goal is to mark all emails across your entire Gmail account as read, you will need to repeat steps 2-6 for each separate category (e.g., Primary, Social, Promotions, Updates, Forums). This systematic approach allows you to clear all unread messages from every section of your Gmail.


## Tips & Tricks

- Use Gmail's search operators (e.g., 'is:unread', 'category:promotions') to refine your selection before marking as read.
- If you accidentally mark an important email as read, quickly find it, select its checkbox, and click the 'Mark as unread' icon (a closed envelope) in the toolbar.
- Regularly archive or delete old emails to keep your inbox manageable and improve Gmail's performance.
- Consider creating filters in Gmail settings to automatically mark certain incoming emails as read based on sender or subject.
- On the Gmail mobile app, long-press an email to select it, then tap additional emails. Tap the 'Mark as read' icon (open envelope) from the top menu.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Be cautious when marking a large number of emails as read without reviewing them first, as you might miss important or time-sensitive information.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I mark only specific unread emails as read?**

Yes, instead of using the top-level checkbox, you can manually click the checkbox next to individual emails you wish to mark as read. After selecting them, click the 'Mark as read' icon in the toolbar.

**What if I accidentally mark an important email as read?**

You can easily reverse this action. Find the email, select its checkbox, and then click the 'Mark as unread' icon (which looks like a closed envelope) in the toolbar to restore its unread status.

**Does marking emails as read delete them from my inbox?**

No, marking an email as read only changes its status from unread to read. The email remains in your inbox or its respective folder until you explicitly delete or archive it.

**How do I mark all emails in my entire Gmail account as read, across all categories?**

To mark all emails across all categories (Primary, Social, Promotions, etc.) as read, you must repeat the selection and 'Mark as read' process for each category individually. There isn't a single button to clear all unread messages from your entire account at once.

**Can I mark emails as read on the Gmail mobile app?**

Yes, the process is similar on the Gmail mobile app. Long-press an email to select it, then tap any other emails you wish to include. Finally, tap the 'Mark as read' icon (an open envelope) from the top menu that appears.

**Why do I still see an unread count after marking all emails as read?**

This could happen if new emails arrive immediately after you perform the action, or if you have unread emails in other folders like Spam or Trash that might contribute to an overall unread badge count, though typically only inbox categories are counted.

**Is there a way to automatically mark certain emails as read when they arrive?**

Yes, you can create a filter in Gmail. Go to Settings > See all settings > Filters and Blocked Addresses > Create a new filter. You can specify criteria like sender or subject and then choose the action 'Mark as read' for matching incoming emails.
