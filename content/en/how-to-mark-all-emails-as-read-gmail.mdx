Marking all emails as read in Gmail helps declutter your inbox and achieve an 'Inbox Zero' state, making it easier to manage new incoming messages. This process can be done quickly using a few simple steps on both desktop and mobile versions of Gmail.

## What You Need

- Gmail account
- Internet access
- Web browser or Gmail app

## Steps

### Step 1: Access Your Gmail Account

Begin by opening your preferred web browser and navigating to gmail.com. Log in with your credentials if you aren't already signed in. Ensure you're viewing the inbox where you want to mark emails as read. If you haven't set up an account yet, you might find our guide on [How to Create a New Gmail Account - Step-by-Step Guide](/en/how-to-create-new-gmail-account) helpful.

> 💡 **Tip:** You can also use the official Gmail app on your mobile device for this process, though the steps differ slightly.

### Step 2: Select All Visible Emails

In your Gmail inbox, locate the small checkbox at the top-left corner, just above your email list. Clicking this checkbox will select all emails currently visible on the page. By default, Gmail displays 50 conversations per page, so this initial selection will only apply to those messages.

### Step 3: Select All Conversations (If Applicable)

After selecting all visible emails on the current page, a message will appear just above your emails, stating, 'All 50 conversations on this page are selected.' If you have more than 50 unread emails, a link will appear next to this message saying, 'Select all X conversations in Primary.' Click this link to extend your selection to *all* emails in that category, not just the visible ones.

### Step 4: Mark Selected Emails as Read

With your desired emails selected (either on the current page or all conversations), look for the 'Mark as read' icon in the toolbar above your emails. This icon typically looks like an open envelope. Click this icon to change the status of all selected emails from unread to read.

### Step 5: Confirm the Action

Gmail will process your request, and you'll see a confirmation message, usually at the bottom-left of your screen, indicating how many conversations were marked as read. All the selected emails will now appear in regular, non-bold text, signifying they are read. Depending on the number of emails, this might take a few moments to complete.

### Step 6: Mark as Read on Mobile (Gmail App)

Open the Gmail app on your smartphone or tablet. Tap and hold on the first email you wish to mark as read until a checkbox appears next to it. Continue tapping on other emails to select them individually. To select multiple emails quickly, after selecting the first one, tap the checkbox at the top to select all visible emails on the current screen.

### Step 7: Complete the Mobile Process

Once you've selected your desired emails in the mobile app, tap the three-dot menu icon (or the envelope icon if available) at the top of the screen. From the options, choose 'Mark as read.' The selected emails will instantly update to appear as read. Repeat the process for other categories or pages as needed, as there isn't a 'select all conversations' option across all pages like on desktop.


## Tips & Tricks

- Use Gmail's search operators (e.g., `is:unread` or `label:unread`) to filter and select specific unread emails more precisely before marking them as read.
- Regularly marking emails as read can help you maintain an 'Inbox Zero' philosophy, reducing digital clutter and improving focus.
- If you accidentally mark an email as read, simply select it again and click the 'Mark as unread' icon (closed envelope) to revert its status.



## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I mark all emails as read in a specific folder or label?**

Yes, navigate to the desired folder or label in Gmail. Then, use the 'Select all' checkbox at the top of the inbox and the subsequent 'Select all conversations in X' link to mark all emails within that specific view as read.

**What's the difference between 'Mark as read' and 'Archive'?**

'Mark as read' simply changes the status of an email from unread to read, keeping it in your inbox or current folder. 'Archive' removes the email from your inbox but keeps it accessible in 'All Mail,' decluttering your primary view without deleting the message.

**Is there a way to automatically mark emails as read in Gmail?**

While Gmail doesn't have a direct 'auto-mark as read' feature for all incoming mail, you can create filters to automatically mark certain types of emails as read upon arrival. Go to Settings > See all settings > Filters and Blocked Addresses to set this up.

**Can I undo marking emails as read?**

Yes, immediately after marking emails as read, a small 'Undo' notification usually appears at the bottom-left of your screen. Click it to revert the action. If you miss this, you can manually select the emails and choose 'Mark as unread.'

**Why are some emails still showing as unread after I marked them all as read?**

This can happen if you only marked emails on the current page as read and didn't select 'all X conversations in Primary' for subsequent pages. Also, new emails arriving immediately after your action would still be unread.

**Does marking emails as read delete them?**

No, marking emails as read only changes their status from unread to read. It does not delete them or move them from your inbox. They remain in their current location unless you explicitly choose to delete or archive them.

**Can I mark emails as read on the Gmail mobile app?**

Yes, you can mark emails as read directly from the Gmail mobile app. Simply tap and hold an email to select it, then tap additional emails, and use the 'Mark as read' option from the menu or toolbar. Note that selecting all emails across multiple pages is not as straightforward as on desktop.
