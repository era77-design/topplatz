To mark all emails as read in Gmail, navigate to your inbox, select all visible messages using the checkbox, then click the option to 'Select all conversations that match this search.' Finally, use the 'More' menu to choose 'Mark as read' and confirm your action. This process helps you quickly clear unread email counts.

## What You Need

- Computer or mobile device with internet access
- Active Gmail account

## Steps

### Step 1: Log In to Your Gmail Account

Open your web browser and go to gmail.com. Enter your email address or phone number and your password to log in. If you don't have an account, you can easily [create a new Gmail account](/en/how-to-create-new-gmail-account) to get started. Ensure you are logged into the correct account you wish to manage.

### Step 2: Navigate to Your Inbox

Once logged in, you will typically land directly in your main inbox. If not, click on 'Inbox' from the left-hand menu. This ensures you are viewing the emails you intend to mark as read, whether it's your primary, social, or promotions tab. Confirm you are in the desired email view before proceeding.

### Step 3: Select All Visible Emails

At the top of your inbox, just above your emails, you'll find a small checkbox. Click this checkbox to select all emails currently visible on the page. This typically selects up to 50 conversations at a time, depending on your display settings and screen size. A message will confirm the number of selected conversations.

### Step 4: Select All Matching Conversations

After clicking the checkbox, a message will appear above your emails, usually stating 'All 50 conversations on this page are selected.' Crucially, click on the text that says 'Select all conversations that match this search.' This action extends your selection beyond the current page to include every email in your inbox (or current view) that is not archived or deleted, effectively selecting all emails in that category.

### Step 5: Mark Emails as Read

With all desired conversations selected, locate the 'More' button (represented by three vertical dots) in the toolbar above your emails. Click on it, and a dropdown menu will appear. From this menu, select 'Mark as read.' This action will change the status of all selected emails from unread to read.

### Step 6: Confirm the Action (if prompted)

For very large inboxes, Gmail might ask you to confirm this bulk action. If a confirmation pop-up appears, click 'OK' or 'Confirm' to proceed. The process may take a few moments to complete, especially if you have thousands of emails being updated. Your inbox will then refresh, showing the updated status.

### Step 7: Verify Your Inbox Status

After the operation completes, observe your inbox. The bold formatting of unread emails should disappear, indicating they are now marked as read. You can also check the unread count next to your inbox label, which should now be zero or significantly reduced, reflecting your changes. This confirms your inbox has been successfully updated.


## Tips & Tricks

- To mark emails in a specific category (e.g., Promotions, Social) as read, navigate to that tab first, then follow the same steps.
- Use the search bar with `is:unread` to quickly filter for all unread emails across your entire Gmail account before marking them as read.
- Marking emails as read does not delete or archive them; it simply changes their status. You can still access them anytime.
- If you accidentally mark emails as read, look for the 'Undo' notification that briefly appears at the top of your screen after the action.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Be sure you want to mark ALL selected emails as read. This action is irreversible without using the 'Undo' option immediately after.
- ⚠️ Double-check that you've clicked 'Select all conversations that match this search' to avoid only marking the visible page as read.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why would I want to mark all emails as read?**

Many users mark all emails as read to achieve 'inbox zero,' reduce visual clutter, or simply clear an overwhelming backlog of unread messages. It helps in managing email stress and focusing on new, incoming communications more effectively.

**Does marking all emails as read delete them?**

No, marking emails as read does not delete them. It only changes their status from unread to read. The emails will still be in your inbox, archives, or other folders, and you can access them whenever needed through search or navigation.

**Can I undo marking emails as read?**

Yes, after you mark emails as read, a small 'Undo' notification usually appears at the bottom left of your Gmail screen for a few seconds. Clicking this will revert the action. If you miss the undo option, you would need to manually mark specific emails as unread again.

**How do I mark specific emails as read instead of all?**

To mark specific emails as read, simply click the checkbox next to each individual email you wish to select. Once selected, click the 'Mark as read' icon (an open envelope) in the toolbar above your emails, or use the 'More' menu to choose the option.

**Can I mark emails in a specific category (e.g., Promotions) as read?**

Yes, you can. First, navigate to the desired category tab (e.g., Promotions, Social, Updates). Then, follow the same steps: click the main selection checkbox, then 'Select all conversations that match this search,' and finally 'Mark as read' from the 'More' menu.

**Is this feature available on the Gmail mobile app?**

The Gmail mobile app has similar functionality, but the steps might differ slightly. You can usually long-press on an email to select it, then tap the selection circle for others, and look for a 'Mark as read' option or icon (often an open envelope) in the top bar.

**What if I only want to mark unread emails as read?**

When you use the 'Select all conversations that match this search' option and then 'Mark as read,' Gmail intelligently only changes the status of emails that are currently unread. Emails already marked as read will remain read, so there's no need for an extra filter to target only unread messages.
