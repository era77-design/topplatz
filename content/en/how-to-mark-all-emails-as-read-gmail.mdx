To mark all emails as read in Gmail, you can use the 'Select all' checkbox and then the 'Mark as read' option. This quickly clears your unread count and helps organize your inbox by removing the bold formatting from unread messages.

## What You Need

- Computer or mobile device
- Internet connection
- Gmail account

## Steps

### Step 1: Access Your Gmail Inbox

Start by opening your web browser and navigating to gmail.com. Log in to your Gmail account if you haven't already. If you're new to Gmail and need assistance setting up your account, you might find our guide on [How to Create a Gmail Account Quickly and Easily](/en/how-to-create-gmail-account) helpful.

### Step 2: Select All Visible Emails

Once in your inbox, locate the small checkbox at the top-left corner, just above your email list. Clicking this checkbox will select all emails currently visible on the page, typically around 50 conversations. This is the first step to performing a bulk action.

### Step 3: Select All Conversations in Your Inbox

After clicking the top checkbox, a message will appear above your emails, usually stating 'All 50 conversations on this page are selected.' Crucially, it will also include a link to 'Select all [number] conversations in Primary' (or the current view like 'All Mail'). Click this link to select *all* emails in that specific category, not just the visible ones. This ensures you mark everything.

### Step 4: Mark Selected Emails as Read

With all desired emails selected, look for the 'Mark as read' icon in the toolbar above your emails. This icon typically looks like an open envelope. Click this icon to change the status of all selected emails from unread to read. If you don't see it, it might be grouped under the 'More' icon (three vertical dots).

### Step 5: Confirm the Action

For large bulk actions, Gmail will often prompt you to confirm your decision. A small pop-up window or banner might appear asking 'Are you sure you want to take this action on [number] conversations?' Click 'OK' or 'Confirm' to proceed. The bold formatting of the emails will disappear, indicating they are now marked as read.

### Step 6: Repeat for Other Categories (Optional)

If you utilize Gmail's tabbed inbox feature (e.g., Primary, Social, Promotions, Updates, Forums), you might need to repeat these steps for each tab you wish to clear. Navigate to each tab, then follow steps 2-5 to mark the emails within those specific categories as read. This ensures all parts of your inbox are decluttered.

### Step 7: Mark Emails as Read on Mobile (Optional)

On the Gmail mobile app, you generally cannot mark all emails as read in one tap. To mark multiple, long-press on the icon next to the first email to select it. Then, tap the icons next to other emails you wish to select. Once chosen, tap the 'Mark as read' icon (an open envelope) at the top of the screen to change their status.


## Tips & Tricks

- Use Gmail's search filters (e.g., `is:unread`) to narrow down emails before marking as read, allowing for more targeted organization.
- Consider archiving or deleting emails you no longer need after marking them as read to further reduce clutter in your inbox.
- Create filters in Gmail settings to automatically mark certain incoming emails as read, especially for newsletters or notifications you don't need to actively open.
- Regularly clean your inbox to maintain a manageable unread count and improve overall email workflow.
- Periodically empty your spam and trash folders to ensure truly unwanted emails are permanently removed.



## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I mark all emails as read across all categories at once?**

Yes, if you navigate to the 'All Mail' view in Gmail, you can select all emails across all categories and mark them as read in one go. This includes emails that might be archived but are not in your primary inbox view.

**What if I accidentally mark important emails as read?**

Marking an email as read only changes its status; it doesn't delete it. You can always find the email using the search bar or by navigating to its folder, and then easily mark it as unread again if needed by clicking the 'Mark as unread' icon.

**Does marking emails as read delete them?**

No, marking emails as read only changes their status from unread to read. The emails remain in your inbox or their respective folders unless you explicitly choose to delete or archive them separately.

**How do I mark emails as unread in Gmail?**

To mark an email as unread, select the email(s) by checking the box next to them, then click the 'Mark as unread' icon (a closed envelope) in the toolbar above your emails. This will revert their status and make them appear bold again.

**Can I mark only emails from a specific sender as read?**

Yes, you can use Gmail's search bar to find emails from a specific sender (e.g., type `from:sender@example.com`). Once the search results appear, you can then select all of them using the checkbox and mark them as read.

**Why would I want to mark all emails as read?**

Marking all emails as read helps to clear your unread count, making your inbox feel less cluttered and easier to manage. It's often used when catching up on a large backlog, or when you want a fresh start with your inbox without deleting anything.

**Is there a keyboard shortcut to mark emails as read?**

Yes, if you have keyboard shortcuts enabled in Gmail settings, you can select emails and then press `Shift + I` to mark them as read. To enable shortcuts, go to Settings > See all settings > Keyboard shortcuts and ensure they are turned on.
