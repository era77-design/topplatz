To mark all emails as read in Gmail, open your inbox in a web browser, select the checkbox at the top left to select all visible emails, then click the 'Select all conversations that match this search' prompt if it appears. Finally, click the 'Mark as read' icon (an open envelope) to clear your unread count.

## What You Need

- Computer or mobile device with internet access
- Gmail account
- Web browser

## Steps

### Step 1: Open Gmail in Your Web Browser

Start by navigating to mail.google.com in your preferred web browser on a computer. Ensure you are logged into the Gmail account where you wish to mark emails as read. If you have multiple accounts, verify you're in the correct one before proceeding to avoid affecting the wrong inbox.

### Step 2: Navigate to Your Inbox or Specific Category

Once Gmail loads, you'll typically be in your primary inbox. If you want to mark all emails as read within a specific category (like Social or Promotions) or a custom label, click on that category or label from the left-hand menu. This ensures you're only targeting the emails you intend to clear.

### Step 3: Select All Visible Emails

Locate the small square checkbox at the very top of your email list, just above the first email. Click this checkbox. This action will instantly select all emails currently visible on the page, typically 50 emails by default. A message will appear above your email list indicating the number of selected conversations.

### Step 4: Select All Matching Emails (If Applicable)

After selecting all visible emails, a prompt will often appear just above your email list stating something like, 'All 50 conversations on this page are selected. Select all [number] conversations that match this search.' Click on this blue text. This crucial step expands your selection to include *all* emails in your current view or search results, not just the ones shown on the first page.

### Step 5: Click the 'Mark as Read' Icon

With all desired emails selected, look for the 'Mark as read' icon in the toolbar that appears above your email list. This icon typically looks like an open envelope. Click this icon to change the status of all selected emails from unread to read. Gmail will process the request, and your unread count should significantly decrease or disappear.

### Step 6: Confirm the Action

After clicking 'Mark as read,' a small notification will briefly appear at the bottom left of your screen, confirming that the conversations have been marked as read. You may notice your inbox reload or simply see the bold formatting (indicating unread status) disappear from the emails. Take a moment to verify that the unread count for your inbox or chosen category has been updated.


## Tips & Tricks

- To quickly mark specific emails as read without selecting all, hover over the email in your list and click the 'Mark as read' icon that appears on the right side of the email row.
- If you're just starting out and want to [create a Gmail account quickly and easily](/en/how-to-create-gmail-account), consider setting up filters and labels from the beginning to better manage your incoming mail and prevent future clutter.
- Use search operators like 'is:unread' to quickly filter your inbox and only see unread emails before marking them all as read. This helps ensure you don't accidentally mark read emails as unread, or vice versa.
- If you have a large number of unread emails, the process might take a few moments. Be patient and avoid refreshing the page until the confirmation message appears.
- Regularly review your inbox and mark irrelevant emails as read to keep your unread count manageable. This practice helps you focus on important communications.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Be careful when marking all emails as read, especially if you have a very large inbox. Ensure you don't accidentally mark important, unread emails that you still need to address. There is an 'undo' option that appears briefly after the action, but it has a limited window.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I undo marking all emails as read?**

Yes, immediately after marking emails as read, a small 'Undo' button will appear at the bottom left of your screen. You have a few seconds to click this to revert the action. After that window closes, undoing the action becomes more difficult.

**Does marking all emails as read delete them?**

No, marking emails as read only changes their status from unread to read. It does not delete, archive, or move the emails from your inbox. They will remain in their current location unless you take a separate action to move or delete them.

**Can I mark all emails as read on the Gmail mobile app?**

The Gmail mobile app does not have a direct 'select all conversations' option like the desktop version. You can select multiple emails by long-pressing one, then tapping others, and then using the 'Mark as read' option. However, there isn't a single button to mark *all* emails in your entire inbox as read simultaneously on mobile.

**Why would I want to mark all emails as read?**

Marking all emails as read is useful for decluttering your inbox, especially if you have a high volume of old, less important unread messages. It helps reset your unread count, allowing you to focus on new, incoming communications more effectively.

**Does this process affect emails in other folders or labels?**

This process only affects emails within the specific view (inbox, category, or label) you are currently in when you perform the 'select all' action. Emails in other folders or labels will retain their original read/unread status unless you navigate to those specific views and repeat the process.

**What if I only want to mark emails from a specific sender as read?**

To mark emails from a specific sender, use Gmail's search bar to find all emails from that sender (e.g., 'from:sender@example.com'). Then, apply the 'select all conversations that match this search' and 'Mark as read' steps as outlined in the guide. This allows for targeted inbox management.
