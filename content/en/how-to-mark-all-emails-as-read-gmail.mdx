To mark all emails as read in Gmail, simply open your inbox, select the checkbox at the top to select all visible messages, and then click the "Mark as read" icon (an open envelope). For more than one page of emails, an additional option will appear to select all conversations in your inbox.

## What You Need

- A computer or mobile device with internet access
- A Gmail account

## Steps

### Step 1: Open Gmail on Your Computer

Access your Gmail account through a web browser on your computer. Ensure you are logged into the correct account. If you're new to Gmail and setting up your first account, understanding these basic navigation steps is crucial for managing your inbox effectively. [How to Create a Gmail Account Quickly and Easily](/en/how-to-create-gmail-account)

### Step 2: Select All Visible Emails

In your Gmail inbox, locate the small checkbox positioned at the top left of your email list, just above the first message. Clicking this checkbox will instantly select all emails currently displayed on the page. This action is the first step towards managing multiple messages at once.

### Step 3: Mark Selected Emails as Read

After selecting the emails, a new toolbar will appear above your email list. Look for the "Mark as read" icon within this toolbar, which typically resembles an open envelope. Click this icon to change the status of all currently selected emails from unread to read, decluttering your immediate view.

### Step 4: Mark ALL Emails in Your Inbox (Multiple Pages)

If you have more emails than can fit on a single page, after clicking the top-left checkbox, a message will appear stating, for example, "All 50 conversations on this page are selected." Crucially, below this message, you'll see an additional option: "Select all [number] conversations in Primary." Click this link to extend your selection beyond just the visible page to encompass all emails in your current inbox view.

### Step 5: Confirm and Apply the Action

Once you've selected all the desired conversations (either on one page or across your entire inbox), click the "Mark as read" icon again. Gmail will then process your request. All chosen emails will now appear as read, helping you achieve a cleaner inbox and focus on new, incoming messages.

### Step 6: Use Search Filters for Specific Emails

If your goal is to mark only specific types of emails as read, leverage Gmail's powerful search bar. For instance, type `is:unread` to display all unread emails, or `label:promotions is:unread` to target unread promotional messages. After executing your search, follow steps 2-5 to select and mark only those specific results as read, leaving other emails untouched.

### Step 7: Marking Emails as Read on Mobile

On the Gmail mobile app, the process differs slightly. To select an email, tap the sender's profile picture or initial. To select multiple, tap the first, then tap others. Once selected, tap the three-dot menu (or an envelope icon, depending on your app version) and choose "Mark as read." Note that there isn't a direct "select all for entire inbox" feature on mobile like on desktop, making this method best for smaller batches.


## Tips & Tricks

- Regularly marking emails as read helps maintain an organized inbox and reduces visual clutter, making it easier to spot new, important messages.
- Utilize Gmail's built-in categories (Primary, Social, Promotions, Updates, Forums) to automatically sort incoming mail, which can also help you manage unread counts more effectively.
- Consider archiving emails after reading them instead of just marking them as read to keep your inbox lean. Archived emails are still searchable but removed from your main inbox view.
- Learn Gmail's keyboard shortcuts (e.g., `x` to select, `shift+i` to mark as read) for faster navigation and more efficient inbox management.
- If you accidentally mark emails as read, a small "Undo" notification will briefly appear at the bottom left of your screen. Click it immediately to revert the action.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Marking a very large number of emails as read cannot be undone once the brief "Undo" notification disappears. Be certain you wish to mark all selected emails before confirming the action.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I mark all emails as read on my phone?**

Yes, but the process is slightly different on the mobile app. You can select multiple emails by tapping their sender icons or profile pictures, and then choose "Mark as read" from the menu options that appear. There isn't a single "select all inbox" button for the entire inbox on mobile like on desktop.

**What if I only want to mark emails from a specific sender as read?**

You can use the search bar in Gmail to filter emails from a particular sender (e.g., type `from:sender@example.com`). Once the search results appear, you can then select all of them and mark them as read using the standard steps outlined for desktop.

**Does marking emails as read delete them?**

No, marking an email as read only changes its status from unread to read. The email remains in your inbox or its respective label until you explicitly delete, archive, or move it to another folder.

**How can I find my unread emails after marking everything as read?**

You can always easily find any remaining unread emails by using the search operator `is:unread` in the Gmail search bar. This will display all emails that are currently marked as unread in your account.

**Why would I want to mark all emails as read?**

Marking all emails as read is a common practice to achieve "inbox zero," reduce visual clutter, and allow you to focus on new, incoming messages without being overwhelmed by a high unread count from older correspondence.

**Is there a way to automatically mark certain incoming emails as read?**

Yes, you can create filters in Gmail to automatically mark incoming emails from specific senders or with certain keywords as read upon arrival. To set this up, go to Gmail Settings, then "See all settings," and navigate to the "Filters and Blocked Addresses" tab.

**What if I accidentally marked important emails as read and missed the undo option?**

If you missed the brief "Undo" notification, you can use the search operator `is:read` combined with other keywords (like sender, subject, or date) to find the recently marked emails. Once located, you can manually select them and mark them as unread again if needed.
