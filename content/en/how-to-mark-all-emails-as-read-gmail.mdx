To mark all emails as read in Gmail, first navigate to your inbox or use the search bar to filter for `is:unread`. Then, click the "Select all" checkbox at the top of your email list, followed by the "Select all conversations that match this search" link, and finally, the "Mark as read" icon. This action helps you quickly manage your inbox and achieve a sense of calm.

## What You Need

- Computer or mobile device with internet access
- Gmail account

## Steps

### Step 1: Open Your Gmail Account

Navigate to mail.google.com in your web browser and sign in to your Gmail account. Ensure you are logged into the correct account where you wish to mark emails as read. This is the first step to access your email management tools.

### Step 2: Search for Unread Emails

In the search bar at the top of your Gmail interface, type `is:unread` and press Enter. This action will filter your inbox to display only the emails that are currently marked as unread across all your labels and categories, making them easy to target.

### Step 3: Select All Visible Unread Emails

Locate the small square checkbox at the top-left of your email list, just above the first message. Click this checkbox to select all the unread emails currently visible on the page. This prepares the visible emails for a bulk action.

### Step 4: Expand Selection to All Matching Emails

After clicking the checkbox, a message will appear above your emails, stating 'All X conversations on this page are selected.' Immediately following this, click the link that says 'Select all Y conversations that match this search.' This crucial step ensures that *all* unread emails, not just those on the current page, are included in your selection.

### Step 5: Mark Selected Emails as Read

With all unread emails now selected, look for the 'Mark as read' icon in the toolbar above your emails. It typically looks like an open envelope. Click this icon to change the status of all selected emails from unread to read.

### Step 6: Confirm Bulk Action

For very large batches of emails, Gmail might ask you to confirm this bulk action. If prompted, click 'OK' or 'Confirm' to proceed with marking all the selected emails as read. The process may take a moment depending on the number of emails being processed.

### Step 7: Verify Your Inbox

Once the action is complete, your inbox should reflect the change, with all previously unread emails now marked as read. You can remove the `is:unread` search filter to view your full inbox and confirm the result, enjoying a clearer workspace.


## Tips & Tricks

- To mark only specific emails as read, simply select them individually using their checkboxes and then click the 'Mark as read' icon.
- You can also mark emails as read from individual messages by opening them and clicking the 'Mark as read' button in the toolbar, or by using keyboard shortcuts (Shift + I).
- If you manage multiple Gmail accounts, ensure you are logged into the correct one before performing bulk actions. [How to Create a New Gmail Account - Step-by-Step Guide](/en/how-to-create-new-gmail-account) can help if you need to set up another.
- Use Gmail's categories (Primary, Social, Promotions, etc.) to manage different types of emails. You can apply the 'mark all as read' action within a specific category by navigating to it first.
- Consider setting up filters to automatically mark certain incoming emails as read or move them to specific labels for better organization and a cleaner inbox.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Be cautious when performing bulk actions like marking all emails as read, especially if you have important unread messages you still need to address. This action cannot be easily undone for a very large number of emails.
- ⚠️ While Gmail offers an 'Undo' option for a short period after an action, it might not be effective for extremely large bulk operations or if you navigate away too quickly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I undo 'mark all as read' in Gmail?**

Yes, immediately after marking emails as read, a small 'Undo' notification usually appears at the bottom left of your screen. Click it quickly to revert the action. However, this undo option has a limited timeframe and may not work for very large bulk operations if you navigate away from the page.

**How do I mark all emails in a specific label or folder as read?**

First, navigate to the desired label or folder in the left sidebar. Then, follow the same steps outlined in this guide: click the 'Select all' checkbox, then 'Select all conversations that match this search,' and finally the 'Mark as read' icon.

**Is there a way to mark only emails from a specific sender as read?**

Yes, you can do this by using the search bar. Type `from:sender@example.com is:unread` (replacing 'sender@example.com' with the actual email address). Then, select all matching conversations and click the 'Mark as read' icon.

**What if the 'Select all conversations that match this search' link doesn't appear?**

This link only appears if there are more emails matching your search query than are currently displayed on the page. If it doesn't appear, it means all relevant emails are already selected by the initial checkbox click, and you can proceed directly to marking them as read.

**Can I mark emails as read on the Gmail mobile app?**

Yes, the process is similar on the Gmail app. You can long-press an email to select it, then tap additional emails to select multiple. To select all visible emails, some apps might have a 'Select all' option in the menu after selecting one. Then, look for the 'Mark as read' option (often a three-dot menu or an icon). For marking *all* unread emails, the desktop version is generally more straightforward.

**Does marking emails as read delete them?**

No, marking an email as read only changes its status from unread to read. It does not delete the email, move it to the trash, or archive it. The email remains in its current folder or label, simply without the 'unread' notification.

**Why would I want to mark all emails as read?**

Marking all emails as read can help you achieve 'inbox zero,' providing a clean slate and reducing visual clutter. It's useful for quickly clearing out old notifications, promotional emails you've already skimmed, or messages you've decided not to engage with, allowing you to focus on new, important communications.
