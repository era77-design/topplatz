Marking all emails as read in Gmail helps declutter your inbox and ensures you only focus on unread messages that require your attention. This process is straightforward and can be done for an entire inbox, specific categories, or filtered search results, making inbox management much more efficient.

## What You Need

- Computer or mobile device with internet access
- Gmail account

## Steps

### Step 1: Open Gmail in Your Web Browser

Begin by navigating to mail.google.com in your preferred web browser on your computer. Log in to your Gmail account if you aren't already signed in. If you're new to Gmail and need to set up an account, you can find a helpful guide on [How to Create a Gmail Account Quickly and Easily](/en/how-to-create-gmail-account).

### Step 2: Navigate to Your Desired Inbox or Category

Once logged in, you'll typically land in your 'Primary' inbox. If you wish to mark emails as read in a specific category like 'Social,' 'Promotions,' 'Updates,' or a custom label, click on that category or label from the left-hand sidebar menu. This will display only the emails within that particular section.

### Step 3: Select All Visible Emails on the Page

At the very top of your email list, slightly above the first email, you'll find a small checkbox. Click this checkbox to select all emails currently visible on your screen. Gmail typically displays 50 conversations per page by default.

### Step 4: Extend Selection to All Conversations in the Category

After clicking the initial checkbox, a message will appear above your emails, typically stating 'All 50 conversations on this page are selected.' Crucially, immediately next to this, you'll see a link that reads 'Select all [number] conversations in [Primary/Promotions/etc.].' Click this link to expand your selection to include *all* emails within that entire category or label, not just the ones currently shown.

### Step 5: Mark Selected Emails as Read

With all your desired emails selected, look for the toolbar located just above your email list. You'll find an icon that looks like an open envelope, often labeled 'Mark as read' if you hover over it. Alternatively, you might see a 'More' menu (represented by three vertical dots); click this and then select 'Mark as read' from the dropdown options.

### Step 6: Confirm the Action and Observe Changes

Gmail may display a confirmation prompt, especially if you are marking a large volume of emails as read. Confirm your choice, and you will notice the unread count for that category or label decrease or disappear entirely as the emails' status changes. This process can take a moment for very large selections.

### Step 7: Repeat for Other Categories (Optional)

If your goal is to clear all unread counts across all your Gmail categories (e.g., Primary, Social, Promotions, Updates, Forums), simply repeat steps 2 through 6 for each individual category. This ensures a comprehensive clean-up of your entire inbox.


## Tips & Tricks

- To quickly find all unread emails across your entire account, use the search bar at the top with the query `is:unread` then apply the selection and 'Mark as read' steps.
- If you accidentally mark emails as read, a small 'Undo' notification will appear briefly at the bottom left of your screen. Click it quickly to revert the action.
- Consider setting up filters to automatically mark certain low-priority incoming emails as read, saving you manual effort in the future.
- Regularly managing your inbox by marking irrelevant emails as read can significantly reduce digital clutter and improve your overall email productivity.
- The process is similar on the Gmail mobile app; look for the select-all checkbox (or long-press an email to initiate selection) and then the 'Mark as read' option in the menu.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Be cautious when selecting 'all conversations' in a category, especially if you have tens of thousands of emails, as it can take some time for Gmail to process, and you might accidentally mark important unread emails as read that you intended to review.
- ⚠️ Once emails are marked as read, they will no longer appear in the 'Unread' filter or count unless you manually mark them as unread again. Always double-check your selection before confirming.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I mark all emails as read on the Gmail mobile app?**

Yes, the Gmail mobile app offers similar functionality. Navigate to your desired inbox, tap the checkbox icon at the top to select emails, then tap the three-dot menu and choose 'Mark as read' from the options.

**What if I only want to mark emails from a specific sender as read?**

You can achieve this by using the search bar. Type `from:sender@example.com` (replacing with the actual email address) to filter emails from that sender. Then, select all the search results and proceed to mark them as read.

**How do I undo marking emails as read if I made a mistake?**

Immediately after marking emails as read, a small 'Undo' notification briefly appears at the bottom left of your screen. Click this notification quickly to revert the action. If you miss it, you'll need to manually locate and mark the emails as unread again.

**Does marking emails as read delete them from my inbox?**

No, marking emails as read only changes their status from unread to read. It does not delete them, archive them, or move them to trash. They will remain in your inbox or the folder they were in.

**Why would someone want to mark all emails as read?**

Users often mark all emails as read to declutter their inbox, especially if they have a large backlog of old or less important unread messages. This allows them to reset their unread count and focus solely on truly new and actionable emails.

**Can I mark emails as read only in specific categories like 'Promotions' or 'Social'?**

Absolutely. Simply navigate to the specific category tab (e.g., 'Promotions') or custom label you wish to clear. Then, follow the steps to select all conversations within that particular view and mark them as read.

**Is there a keyboard shortcut for marking emails as read in Gmail?**

While there isn't a single shortcut for 'mark all as read', you can enable keyboard shortcuts in Gmail settings. Once enabled, you can select emails using 'x' and then press 'shift + i' to mark selected emails as read.
