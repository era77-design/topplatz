To mark all emails as read in Gmail, you typically use the 'Select all' checkbox at the top of your inbox, followed by the 'Mark as read' button. This process can be applied to your entire inbox, specific labels, or filtered search results, helping you quickly clear your unread count and achieve inbox zero.

## What You Need

- Computer or mobile device
- Internet connection
- Gmail account

## Steps

### Step 1: Access Your Gmail Inbox

Open your preferred web browser and navigate to mail.google.com, then sign in to your Gmail account. If you're using the Gmail app on a mobile device, open the app and ensure you are logged into the correct account. This is the first step to gaining access to your emails.

### Step 2: Select All Visible Emails

On the desktop version of Gmail, locate and click the small checkbox situated at the top of your inbox, just above your list of emails. This initial action will select all emails currently displayed on the page, which typically amounts to 50 conversations. This helps you start the bulk selection process.

### Step 3: Select All Conversations in Your Inbox (Desktop Only)

After clicking the initial checkbox, a message will appear above your emails stating, 'All 50 conversations on this page are selected.' Crucially, immediately next to this message, click the link that says, 'Select all [number] conversations in Primary' (or whichever tab or label you are currently viewing). This step is vital for selecting every email in that view, not just the ones visible on the first page.

### Step 4: Mark Selected Emails as Read

Once all the desired emails are highlighted and selected, click the 'Mark as read' icon. This icon typically resembles an open envelope with a checkmark, or simply an open envelope, and is found in the toolbar directly above your emails. This action will change the status of all selected emails from unread to read.

### Step 5: Confirm the Action

A confirmation message will briefly appear, usually at the bottom left of your screen, indicating that the selected conversations have been successfully marked as read. Your unread email count should immediately reflect this change, and the emails will no longer appear bold in your inbox. This confirms your bulk action was successful.

> 💡 **Tip:** This action is generally irreversible in terms of marking as read in bulk, but you can always manually mark individual emails as unread again if you realize you need to review them further.

### Step 6: Repeat for Other Tabs or Labels (Optional)

If you utilize different inbox tabs such as Social or Promotions, or if you have custom labels with unread emails, you will need to navigate to each of these sections individually. Once in a specific tab or label, repeat steps 2-5 to ensure all emails within those distinct categories are also marked as read. This helps you clear your entire Gmail account of unread messages.


## Tips & Tricks

- **Use Search Filters for Specific Marking**: To mark only specific types of emails as read, leverage Gmail's powerful search bar. For instance, typing `is:unread from:sender@example.com` will filter for all unread emails from a particular sender, allowing you to select and mark only those as read.
- **Mobile App Process**: On the Gmail mobile app, there isn't a single 'select all' button for your entire inbox. You must long-press an email to select it, then tap additional emails to select them, and finally tap the 'Mark as read' icon (an open envelope). For clearing a large number of emails, using the desktop version is generally more efficient.
- **Manage Your Inbox Regularly**: Regularly marking emails as read can significantly help you achieve 'inbox zero' and reduce digital clutter. For new users, understanding these features from the start can be very helpful. If you're just getting started with Gmail, you might find it useful to learn [How to Create a New Gmail Account - Step-by-Step Guide](/en/how-to-create-new-gmail-account) to ensure you're familiar with all the basics of managing your account.
- **Marking Emails as Read vs. Archiving/Deleting**: Marking an email as read simply changes its status; it does not remove it from your inbox. If you aim to declutter your inbox by removing emails from view, consider using the archive or delete functions instead.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Be careful when using the 'Select all conversations' option, as it applies to *all* emails in that specific view (e.g., your Primary tab, Promotions tab, or a custom label), not just the ones currently visible on your screen. Ensure you genuinely want to mark everything as read before confirming the action.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I mark all emails as read on the Gmail mobile app?**

The Gmail mobile app does not have a single 'select all' button for your entire inbox. You need to manually select multiple emails by long-pressing the first one, then tapping others, and finally using the 'Mark as read' icon (an open envelope).

**What if I accidentally mark an important email as read?**

Don't worry, marking an email as read doesn't delete it. You can easily find the email and mark it as unread again by opening it and clicking the 'Mark as unread' icon (usually a closed envelope) or by right-clicking it in your inbox view and selecting 'Mark as unread'.

**Does marking emails as read delete them from my Gmail account?**

No, marking emails as read only changes their status from unread to read. They remain in your inbox or whichever folder they were in, still accessible. To remove emails from your inbox, you would need to archive or delete them.

**How can I mark emails as read in a specific Gmail label or folder?**

Navigate to the desired label or folder by clicking on it in the left sidebar. Once inside, follow the same steps as for your main inbox: click the 'Select all' checkbox, then click 'Select all conversations in [label name],' and finally click the 'Mark as read' icon.

**Why don't all my emails get marked as read when I first click 'Select all'?**

When you first click the 'Select all' checkbox, it only selects the emails currently visible on that specific page (typically 50 conversations). You must then click the additional link that appears, 'Select all [number] conversations in [current view],' to select *all* emails beyond the visible page.

**Can I mark only unread emails as read without affecting read emails?**

Yes, you can use the search bar to filter for unread emails first. Type `is:unread` into the search bar, then hit Enter. Once the search results appear, you can then use the 'Select all' checkbox and the 'Mark as read' button to clear only those unread emails.

**Is there a keyboard shortcut to mark emails as read in Gmail?**

Yes, if you have keyboard shortcuts enabled in your Gmail settings, you can select one or more emails and then press `Shift + I` to mark them as read. To enable shortcuts, go to Gmail Settings > See all settings > General > Keyboard shortcuts.
