To mark all emails as read in Gmail, simply select all visible emails by clicking the checkbox at the top of your inbox, then click the 'Mark as read' icon (an open envelope). If you have more emails than are visible on the current page, a banner will appear allowing you to select all conversations in your current view or label.

## What You Need

- Computer or mobile device
- Internet connection
- Gmail account

## Steps

### Step 1: Access Your Gmail Account

First, open your web browser or Gmail app and [log in to your Gmail account](/en/how-to-create-gmail-account) with your email address and password. Ensure you are on the correct account if you manage multiple, as actions taken here will apply only to the logged-in account.

### Step 2: Go to Your Inbox or Desired Label

Navigate to your primary inbox, or select a specific label (folder) from the left-hand menu where you wish to mark emails as read. This method works for any view in Gmail, including 'All Mail', 'Starred', or custom labels you have created.

### Step 3: Select All Visible Emails

Locate the small square checkbox at the very top of your email list, just above the 'Compose' button and below the search bar. Click this checkbox to select all emails currently displayed on the page. You will see a notification indicating how many conversations are selected.

### Step 4: Select All Conversations (If Applicable)

If you have more emails than are shown on the current page (e.g., more than 50), a yellow banner will appear at the top of your email list. It will say something like 'All 50 conversations on this page are selected. Select all X conversations in [Current View]'. Click the second part of this banner to select *all* emails in your current view or label, not just those on the first page.

### Step 5: Click 'Mark as Read'

After selecting the desired emails, look for the 'Mark as read' icon in the toolbar that appears above your email list. This icon typically looks like an open envelope. Click it to change the status of all selected emails from unread to read.

### Step 6: Verify the Change

Once clicked, the selected emails will no longer appear bold (if they were unread) and the unread count next to your inbox or label name will decrease or disappear. This confirms that the operation was successful and your inbox is now tidier, with all selected messages marked as read.


## Tips & Tricks

- Before marking all as read, consider using Gmail's powerful search filters (e.g., `is:unread from:sender@example.com`) to target specific groups of emails you want to manage.
- Accidentally marked important emails as read? Look for the 'Undo' notification that briefly appears in the bottom-left corner after the action, or use the keyboard shortcut `z` to reverse it.
- For a quicker process, after selecting emails, you can press `Shift + I` on your keyboard to mark them as read.
- On the Gmail mobile app, you can select multiple emails by long-pressing one, then tapping others. Then, tap the three-dot menu and choose 'Mark as read'.
- Remember that marking emails as read only changes their status; it doesn't remove them from your inbox. If you want to clear them out, consider archiving them instead.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Be cautious when marking a large number of emails as read, especially if you haven't reviewed them recently. You might inadvertently miss important communications or time-sensitive information.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I mark emails as read on the Gmail mobile app?**

Yes, open the Gmail app, long-press on one email to select it, then tap other emails to select more. Finally, tap the three-dot menu icon (or the envelope icon if available) and choose 'Mark as read'.

**What's the difference between 'Mark as read' and 'Archive'?**

'Mark as read' changes an email's status from unread to read but keeps it in your current view (e.g., Inbox). 'Archive' removes the email from your inbox and moves it to the 'All Mail' folder, effectively hiding it from your main view without deleting it.

**Can I undo marking emails as read?**

Yes, immediately after marking emails as read, a small 'Undo' notification will appear at the bottom left of your screen. Click it quickly to reverse the action. You can also manually mark specific emails as unread again by selecting them and clicking the 'Mark as unread' icon.

**How do I mark emails as read in a specific label or folder?**

Navigate to the desired label or folder from the left-hand menu in Gmail. Then, follow the same steps: select all emails using the top checkbox, and click the 'Mark as read' icon in the toolbar.

**Why are some emails still unread after I marked them all as read?**

This usually happens if you only selected emails on the current page. Ensure you clicked the banner that says 'Select all X conversations in [Current View]' after selecting the visible ones to apply the action to *all* emails in that section, across all pages.

**Does marking emails as read delete them?**

No, marking emails as read simply changes their status to indicate you've viewed them. It does not delete them. The emails remain in your inbox or the label they are assigned to, and you can still search for and access them.

**Can I mark emails as read without opening them?**

Absolutely! You can select individual emails using their checkboxes on the left side of the email list and then click the 'Mark as read' icon without ever opening them. This is a common and efficient way to quickly clear your unread count.
