To clean a cap, gently spot treat stains with a mild detergent and cool water. For a deeper clean, hand wash the entire cap, avoiding harsh chemicals or machine washing, which can distort its shape. Air dry the cap on a form to maintain its structure.

## What You Need

- Mild laundry detergent or dish soap
- Soft brush (e.g., old toothbrush) or clean cloth
- Cool water
- Clean basin or sink
- Clean towel
- Cap form, small bowl, or balloon for drying (optional)

## Steps

### Step 1: Assess Your Cap's Material and Care Label

Before cleaning, check the cap's inner label for specific care instructions, especially for materials like wool, silk, or vintage caps. Identify the brim material; older caps might have cardboard brims which should not be submerged in water. If there's no label or you're unsure, assume it's best to hand wash with cool water and mild soap.

> 💡 **Tip:** Always test your cleaning solution on an inconspicuous area first to ensure it doesn't cause discoloration.

### Step 2: Spot Clean Visible Stains

For sweat stains on the headband or specific dirty spots, apply a small amount of mild laundry detergent or dish soap directly to the stain. Gently work it in with a soft brush (like an old toothbrush) or a clean cloth, moving in circular motions. Allow the solution to penetrate for a few minutes.

> 💡 **Tip:** For tough sweat stains, a paste of baking soda and water can be effective. Apply, let sit, then gently scrub.

### Step 3: Prepare a Gentle Washing Solution

Fill a clean basin or sink with cool water. Add a small amount of mild laundry detergent (about a teaspoon) and swirl to create a soapy solution. Avoid hot water, as it can cause colors to bleed, fabrics to shrink, or brims to warp, especially if they are made of plastic.

> 💡 **Tip:** Ensure the water is cool, not warm or hot, to protect the cap's integrity.

### Step 4: Submerge and Hand Wash the Cap

Place the entire cap into the soapy water. Gently agitate the cap with your hands, focusing on areas that need more attention. Avoid harsh scrubbing or twisting, which can damage the cap's structure and fabric. Let the cap soak for 5-10 minutes for deeper cleaning, but no longer.

> 💡 **Tip:** For extremely delicate caps, simply gently swish it in the water rather than scrubbing.

### Step 5: Rinse Thoroughly with Cool Water

Remove the cap from the soapy water and rinse it under cool, running water until all suds and soap residue are gone. It's crucial to remove all detergent to prevent residue buildup once dry. Squeeze gently to remove excess water, but do not wring or twist the cap, as this can distort its shape.

> 💡 **Tip:** Ensure the water runs clear from the cap before proceeding to the drying stage.

### Step 6: Blot Dry and Reshape

Lay the cap on a clean, dry towel and gently press down to absorb as much water as possible. Carefully reshape the cap to its original form, paying close attention to the crown and brim. Take your time to get the shape right, as it will dry in this position.

> 💡 **Tip:** Avoid aggressively rubbing the cap with the towel, as this can damage the fabric fibers.

### Step 7: Air Dry on a Form

Place the cap on a clean cap form, a small bowl turned upside down, or even a blown-up balloon to help it maintain its shape as it dries. Allow it to air dry completely away from direct sunlight or heat, which can cause fading, shrinking, or damage to plastic brims. Drying can take several hours to a full day, depending on humidity.

> 💡 **Tip:** Never use a clothes dryer for caps, as the heat will almost certainly cause shrinking and damage the brim.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always use cool water to prevent shrinking, color bleeding, or brim damage.
- For tough odors, sprinkle baking soda inside the cap, let it sit overnight, then brush it off before washing.
- If your cap has a plastic brim, you can carefully clean it with a damp cloth and mild soap without fully submerging the cap if the rest of the cap isn't too dirty.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Do not machine wash most caps, especially those with cardboard brims or delicate embellishments, as it can cause irreversible damage and distortion.
- ⚠️ Avoid using harsh chemicals like bleach, as they can fade colors, damage fabric, and weaken materials.
- ⚠️ Never put a cap in a clothes dryer; the high heat will almost certainly shrink the cap and warp the brim.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I put my cap in the washing machine?**

It is generally not recommended to machine wash most caps, especially those with curved brims or cardboard inserts. The agitation and heat can cause the cap to lose its shape, shrink, or damage the brim.

**How do I clean sweat stains from a cap?**

To clean sweat stains, pre-treat them with a mild detergent or a paste made of baking soda and water. Gently scrub the stained area with a soft brush before hand washing the entire cap. Rinse thoroughly to remove all residue.

**Can I use bleach on my white cap?**

It's best to avoid bleach, even on white caps, as it can weaken the fabric fibers and potentially cause yellowing over time. Opt for a gentle stain remover or a diluted solution of hydrogen peroxide for whitening.

**How do I dry my cap without losing its shape?**

After hand washing, gently blot excess water with a towel and then reshape the cap. Air dry it on a cap form, a small bowl, or a blown-up balloon to help maintain its original structure as it dries completely.

**What if my cap has a cardboard brim?**

Caps with cardboard brims should never be submerged in water. Instead, spot clean the fabric parts of the cap with a damp cloth and mild soap, being very careful to avoid getting the cardboard wet. Allow it to air dry promptly.

**How often should I clean my cap?**

The frequency of cleaning depends on how often you wear your cap and how dirty it gets. For regular use, cleaning every few weeks to a month can keep it fresh. Spot clean as needed for immediate spills or stains.

**Can I clean a wool cap the same way?**

Wool caps require extra care. While hand washing in cool water with a wool-specific detergent is possible, avoid excessive rubbing or agitation which can cause felting. Always air dry flat or on a form, and never machine wash or dry wool.
