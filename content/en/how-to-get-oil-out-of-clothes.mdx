To effectively get oil out of clothes, start by immediately blotting the stain and then applying an absorbent powder like cornstarch or baking soda. Follow this with a direct application of degreasing dish soap, gently working it into the fabric before washing. This multi-step approach is key to breaking down and lifting the oily residue.

## What You Need

- Paper towels or clean cloth
- Cornstarch, baking soda, or talcum powder
- Liquid dish soap (degreasing formula)
- Old toothbrush or soft brush
- Laundry detergent
- Washing machine

## Steps

### Step 1: Blot Excess Oil Immediately

As soon as you notice an oil stain, grab a clean paper towel or cloth. Gently blot the stain to absorb as much excess oil as possible. Avoid rubbing, as this can spread the stain and push the oil deeper into the fabric fibers, making it harder to remove later. Work from the outside edges of the stain inward to contain it.

### Step 2: Apply an Absorbent Powder

Liberally sprinkle an absorbent powder such as cornstarch, baking soda, or talcum powder directly over the entire oil stain. These powders are excellent at drawing out oil from fabric. Let the powder sit on the stain for at least 15-30 minutes, or even a few hours for larger or older stains, to allow it to fully absorb the oil. You should see the powder clump as it soaks up the grease.

### Step 3: Gently Scrape Off the Powder

Once the absorbent powder has done its job, gently scrape it off the fabric using the back of a spoon, a dull knife, or a stiff brush. Be careful not to damage the fabric. You should notice that the powder has changed color, indicating it has absorbed some of the oil. Discard the oily powder to prevent re-depositing it onto the clothes.

### Step 4: Pre-Treat with Dish Soap

Apply a small amount of liquid dish soap directly onto the remaining oil stain. Dish soap, especially degreasing formulas, is designed to break down grease effectively. Using an old toothbrush or your fingers, gently rub the dish soap into the stained area in a circular motion. Allow the soap to sit on the stain for 5-10 minutes to penetrate the fibers.

### Step 5: Wash as Recommended

Place the item in your washing machine and wash it as you normally would, using your regular laundry detergent. For best results with oil stains, use the hottest water temperature safe for the fabric, as heat helps to further break down oil. Do not add other clothes to the load if you suspect the stain might spread or if it's a particularly stubborn stain.

### Step 6: Air Dry and Inspect

After washing, *do not* put the garment in the dryer immediately. Instead, allow it to air dry completely and then inspect the stained area closely. The heat from a dryer can permanently set any remaining oil stain, making it almost impossible to remove later. If the stain is still visible, do not proceed to drying.

### Step 7: Repeat if Necessary

If the oil stain persists after the first wash and air dry, repeat the entire process from applying the absorbent powder. Stubborn or old stains may require multiple treatments to fully disappear. You might also consider using a dedicated laundry stain remover designed for grease stains as an additional step before re-washing.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always check the garment's care label before attempting any stain removal method to ensure you use appropriate temperatures and products.
- Test any stain remover or cleaning agent on an inconspicuous area of the fabric first to ensure it doesn't cause discoloration or damage.
- For delicate fabrics like silk or wool, consider professional dry cleaning for oil stains to avoid potential damage from aggressive home treatments.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Avoid rubbing oil stains vigorously, as this can spread the oil and embed it deeper into the fabric fibers.
- ⚠️ Never put oil-stained items into a hot dryer until you are absolutely certain the stain is completely gone. Heat can set oil stains permanently, making them much more difficult, if not impossible, to remove.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do you get oil out of clothes effectively?**

To effectively get oil out of clothes, blot the excess oil, apply an absorbent like cornstarch, then pretreat with degreasing dish soap before washing in the hottest water safe for the fabric. Always air dry and inspect the garment before machine drying.

**How to get old oil stains out of clothes?**

Getting old oil stains out of clothes requires patience and repetition. You'll need to re-wet the stain, apply an absorbent powder, pretreat with dish soap, and then wash. It may take several cycles of this process to fully lift a set-in stain.

**What are the best household items to remove oil stains?**

The best household items for removing oil stains include absorbent powders like cornstarch, baking soda, or talcum powder, which draw out the oil. A degreasing liquid dish soap is also highly effective at breaking down the grease before washing.

**Can oil stains be removed from delicate fabrics?**

Oil stains can be removed from delicate fabrics, but extreme caution is advised. Always test cleaning agents on an inconspicuous area first, use gentle blotting instead of rubbing, and consider professional dry cleaning for valuable or very delicate items.

**What if an oil stain has already been dried in the dryer?**

If an oil stain has already been dried in the dryer, it will be much harder to remove, as the heat can set the stain permanently. However, you can still try saturating the stain with dish soap, allowing it to sit for several hours or overnight, and then re-washing in hot water.

**Is it safe to use commercial stain removers on oil stains?**

Yes, many commercial stain removers are formulated to tackle oil and grease stains effectively. Always read and follow the product's instructions carefully, and perform a patch test on an unseen area of the garment before applying it to the main stain.
