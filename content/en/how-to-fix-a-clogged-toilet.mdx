A clogged toilet can be a frustrating and messy problem, but many clogs can be resolved with simple household tools and techniques. Before panicking, assess the situation and try these practical steps to get your toilet flushing properly again.

## What You Need

- Rubber gloves
- Old towels or newspaper
- Toilet plunger (flange-style recommended)
- Dish soap
- Hot water (not boiling)
- Baking soda
- White vinegar
- Toilet auger (optional)

## Steps

### Step 1: Assess the Situation and Prepare

First, resist the urge to flush again, as this could cause the bowl to overflow. If the water level is high, lift the tank lid and push down the flapper to stop water from entering the bowl. Lay down old towels or newspaper around the base of the toilet to protect the floor from any splashes or spills. Put on a pair of rubber gloves for hygiene.

### Step 2: Use a Plunger Correctly

The most effective tool for a typical clog is a toilet plunger, specifically one with a flange (a narrower rubber extension below the cup). Position the plunger so the flange creates a tight seal over the drain hole. Plunge with firm, consistent up-and-down motions for about 15-20 seconds, maintaining the seal. On the final plunge, pull the plunger up quickly to break the suction and dislodge the clog.

### Step 3: Try Dish Soap and Hot Water

If plunging doesn't work or you don't have a plunger, squirt about half a cup of liquid dish soap into the toilet bowl. The soap acts as a lubricant, helping the clog slide down. Follow this by pouring a gallon of very hot (but not boiling) water into the bowl. Let it sit for 20-30 minutes; the combination should soften the clog and allow it to pass.

### Step 4: Combine Baking Soda and Vinegar

For a more powerful chemical reaction, try a mixture of baking soda and vinegar. Pour one cup of baking soda into the toilet bowl, followed by two cups of white vinegar. The fizzing reaction helps break down organic matter in the clog. Let this mixture sit for at least 30 minutes, or even overnight for stubborn clogs, before attempting to flush.

### Step 5: Employ a Toilet Auger (Snake)

If the above methods fail, a toilet auger (also known as a closet auger or toilet snake) is designed to clear deeper clogs without scratching the porcelain. Carefully insert the end of the auger into the toilet drain, cranking the handle to extend the cable. When you feel resistance, rotate the handle to break up or hook the clog. Pull the auger back out slowly, hopefully bringing the clog with it.

### Step 6: Flush and Clean Up

Once you believe the clog is cleared, try flushing the toilet. If the water drains normally, you've succeeded! If not, you may need to repeat a previous step or consider professional help. Thoroughly clean the toilet bowl, plunger, and any splashed areas with disinfectant to maintain hygiene.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always use a flange-style plunger for toilets, as it creates a better seal than a cup-style plunger.
- Never use harsh chemical drain cleaners in your toilet, as they can damage pipes and are dangerous.
- To prevent future clogs, avoid flushing anything other than human waste and toilet paper.
- Keep a small waste bin next to the toilet for tissues, wipes, dental floss, and other non-flushable items.
- If your toilet frequently clogs, there might be a larger issue with your plumbing system, such as tree roots or old pipes.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Do not use boiling water, as it can crack the porcelain of your toilet bowl.
- ⚠️ Avoid using chemical drain cleaners in your toilet, as they can be corrosive, emit harmful fumes, and may not resolve the clog effectively.
- ⚠️ If water doesn't drain at all after several attempts, or if you suspect a main line clog, it's best to call a professional plumber.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I fix a clogged toilet without a plunger?**

You can often fix a clogged toilet without a plunger by using dish soap and hot water. Pour about half a cup of dish soap into the bowl, followed by a gallon of hot (not boiling) water, and let it sit for 20-30 minutes to dissolve or lubricate the clog. Another method is to combine baking soda and vinegar, letting the fizzing action work on the obstruction.

**How do you fix a clogged toilet when there's poop in it?**

When there's poop in a clogged toilet, the best first step is usually a plunger. Ensure a tight seal and plunge firmly. If that doesn't work, try the dish soap and hot water method, as the soap can help break down organic matter and lubricate the clog. For stubborn clogs, a toilet auger can physically clear the obstruction.

**What causes a toilet to clog?**

Toilets typically clog when non-flushable items are introduced, such as excessive toilet paper, wet wipes, paper towels, dental floss, or feminine hygiene products. Sometimes, a weak flush or issues with the plumbing vent or main sewer line can also contribute to frequent clogs.

**How long should I wait after trying a clog remedy?**

For methods like dish soap and hot water or baking soda and vinegar, it's best to wait at least 20-30 minutes to allow the ingredients time to work on the clog. For very stubborn clogs, you can even let the mixture sit overnight before attempting to flush or plunge again.

**When should I call a plumber for a clogged toilet?**

You should call a plumber if you've tried multiple methods (plunging, soap/water, auger) and the toilet remains clogged, or if multiple toilets or drains in your home are backed up, which could indicate a main sewer line issue. Also call a plumber if you suspect a foreign object is lodged in the toilet that you cannot remove.
