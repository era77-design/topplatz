To fix a clogged toilet, first assess the water level to prevent overflow. Then, you can try using a plunger with proper technique, or explore alternative methods like dish soap and hot water, or a toilet auger for more stubborn clogs.

## What You Need

- Rubber gloves
- Old towels or newspaper
- Flange plunger
- Dish soap
- Hot water (not boiling)
- Toilet auger (closet auger)
- Bucket

## Steps

### Step 1: Prepare the Area and Assess the Clog

Before attempting to fix the clog, lay down old towels or newspaper around the base of the toilet to protect your floor from potential spills. If the toilet bowl is nearly full, do not flush again, as this will likely cause an overflow. Put on your rubber gloves to maintain hygiene.

> 💡 **Tip:** Never flush a second time if the water level in the bowl is high. This will almost certainly lead to an overflow onto your bathroom floor.

### Step 2: Use a Flange Plunger Correctly

Position the plunger over the toilet drain, ensuring you create a complete seal around the opening. Push down slowly to expel air, then plunge vigorously with an up-and-down motion for 15-20 seconds. Break the seal quickly on the last pull to create a suction effect. Repeat this process several times if necessary.

> 💡 **Tip:** A flange plunger, with its extended rubber flap, creates a much better seal for toilet drains compared to a cup plunger, making it more effective.

### Step 3: Try the Dish Soap and Hot Water Method

If plunging doesn't work, pour about half a cup of liquid dish soap into the toilet bowl. Let it sit for 10-15 minutes, allowing it to lubricate the clog. Follow this by pouring a gallon of hot (not boiling) water from waist height into the bowl, which adds force and helps dissolve the clog. Wait another 15-20 minutes for the soap and water to break down the obstruction.

> 💡 **Tip:** This method is particularly effective for organic clogs, such as excessive toilet paper or waste, as the soap helps them slide through the pipes.

### Step 4: Employ a Toilet Auger (Closet Auger)

For stubborn clogs that resist plunging and the soap method, a toilet auger is your next best tool. Gently insert the auger's coiled end into the drain, pushing it until you feel resistance. Crank the handle clockwise to extend the cable and break up or hook the clog. Pull the auger back slowly to extract any material caught on the coil.

> 💡 **Tip:** Toilet augers are specifically designed to navigate the toilet's curves without scratching the porcelain bowl, unlike standard drain snakes.

### Step 5: Manually Remove Visible Obstructions

If you can see an object like a small toy or air freshener causing the clog, and it's within reach, put on your rubber gloves and carefully try to retrieve it by hand. Sometimes, a simple manual removal can save you a lot of effort.

> 💡 **Tip:** Only attempt this if the object is clearly visible and easily accessible. Do not force your hand into the drain if you cannot see the obstruction.

### Step 6: Flush and Confirm the Fix

Once you believe the clog is clear, flush the toilet carefully. If the water drains normally, the clog has been successfully resolved. If the water level rises again, but doesn't overflow, you might need to repeat one of the previous steps or try a different method.

> 💡 **Tip:** If the toilet still seems sluggish, a second, careful flush after a few minutes might help clear any remaining debris.

### Step 7: Clean Up Your Workspace

After successfully clearing the clog, clean all your tools thoroughly with hot, soapy water and a disinfectant spray. Wipe down any splashed areas around the toilet and floor with a disinfectant cleaner. Dispose of any retrieved debris in a trash bag and wash your hands thoroughly.

> 💡 **Tip:** Proper cleanup prevents the spread of germs and keeps your tools ready for future use, should they ever be needed again.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always wear rubber gloves when dealing with a clogged toilet for hygiene and safety.
- Be patient; some clogs take time to dissolve or break apart, especially with the dish soap method.
- Store a dedicated flange plunger near your toilet for quick access when clogs occur.
- If multiple methods fail or if you experience frequent clogs, it's best to call a professional plumber.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Do not flush the toilet if the water level is high; this will cause an overflow.
- ⚠️ Never use boiling water, as the sudden temperature change can crack the porcelain bowl.
- ⚠️ Avoid using chemical drain cleaners; they can damage pipes, are hazardous to you, and are often ineffective against toilet clogs.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How to fix a clogged toilet without a plunger?**

You can fix a clogged toilet without a plunger by using the dish soap and hot water method, which helps lubricate and break down organic clogs. Another effective alternative is to use a toilet auger (closet auger) to physically break up or retrieve the obstruction.

**How to fix a clogged toilet with poop?**

To fix a clogged toilet with poop, start by using a flange plunger with good technique. If that doesn't work, the dish soap and hot water method is highly effective for organic clogs, as the soap helps lubricate the waste, allowing it to slide through the drain.

**What causes a toilet to clog?**

Toilets commonly clog due to flushing too much toilet paper at once, or attempting to flush non-flushable items like wet wipes, paper towels, feminine hygiene products, or small toys. Older plumbing systems or issues within the sewer line can also contribute to frequent clogs.

**How can I prevent my toilet from clogging in the future?**

To prevent future clogs, only flush human waste and toilet paper. Avoid flushing anything else, even if labeled 'flushable.' Consider using less toilet paper per flush, and ensure children understand what can and cannot be flushed down the toilet.

**When should I call a plumber for a clogged toilet?**

You should call a plumber if you've tried multiple DIY methods and the clog persists, if you experience frequent or recurring clogs, or if the clog affects multiple fixtures in your home (indicating a main sewer line issue). A plumber can diagnose and resolve deeper problems.
