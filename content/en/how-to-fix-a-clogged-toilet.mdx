A clogged toilet is a common household problem that can be resolved with a few simple tools and techniques. Most clogs can be cleared without professional help, restoring your toilet's function efficiently and safely. Follow these steps to tackle the issue head-on.

## What You Need

- Rubber gloves
- Old towels or newspaper
- Plunger (flange plunger recommended)
- Dish soap
- Hot water (not boiling)
- Baking soda
- White vinegar
- Toilet auger (snake)
- Bucket

## Steps

### Step 1: Prepare the Area and Prevent Overflow

Before you begin, put on rubber gloves to maintain hygiene. Lay down old towels or newspaper around the base of the toilet to protect your floor from splashes and spills. If the toilet bowl is completely full and close to overflowing, remove some water into a bucket to prevent a mess during plunging. Locate the shut-off valve behind the toilet and turn it clockwise to stop water from refilling the bowl if it flushes again.

### Step 2: Plunge with Proper Technique

Position your plunger firmly over the drain hole, ensuring a tight seal. Push down slowly to expel air, then pull up sharply to create suction. Repeat this push-and-pull motion 15-20 times without breaking the seal. The goal is to dislodge the clog with water pressure. If the water starts to drain, remove the plunger and flush to check if the clog is cleared. You may need to repeat this process a few times.

### Step 3: Try the Dish Soap and Hot Water Method

If plunging doesn't work, pour about half a cup of liquid dish soap into the toilet bowl. Let it sit for 10-15 minutes, allowing the soap to lubricate the clog. After the waiting period, carefully pour a gallon of hot (but not boiling) water into the bowl from waist height. The heat helps break down the clog, and the soap helps it slide through. Wait another 20-30 minutes for the combination to work, then try flushing.

### Step 4: Use Baking Soda and Vinegar for Stubborn Clogs

For more stubborn clogs, a natural chemical reaction can sometimes do the trick. Pour one cup of baking soda into the toilet bowl, followed by two cups of white vinegar. The mixture will fizz, so be prepared for some bubbling. Let this combination sit for at least 30 minutes, or even overnight for very tough clogs. The reaction helps break down organic matter and can loosen the blockage. Flush to check the results.

### Step 5: Employ a Toilet Auger (Snake)

If previous methods fail, a toilet auger, also known as a closet auger or snake, is your next best tool. This specialized tool is designed to navigate the toilet's curves without scratching the porcelain. Insert the auger's end into the drain hole, cranking the handle clockwise to extend the cable and push through the clog. Once you feel resistance, retract the auger, bringing the clog with it. Dispose of any retrieved material properly.

### Step 6: Consider a Second Flush and Clean Up

Once the clog is cleared and the toilet flushes normally, flush it a second time to ensure everything is flowing freely. Turn the water shut-off valve back to its open position. Thoroughly clean the toilet bowl, seat, and any splashed areas with a disinfectant cleaner. Wash your hands thoroughly after completing the task.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always use a flange plunger for toilets; cup plungers are better suited for sinks.
- Never use harsh chemical drain cleaners in your toilet, as they can damage pipes and harm septic systems.
- To prevent future clogs, avoid flushing anything other than human waste and toilet paper. Be mindful of how much toilet paper is used at once.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Do not use boiling water, as it can crack the porcelain of your toilet bowl.
- ⚠️ Avoid excessive force when plunging or using an auger, as this can damage the toilet' or pipes.
- ⚠️ If the water level is rising rapidly, do not flush again until you've removed some water or cleared the clog, to prevent overflow.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How to fix a clogged toilet without a plunger?**

You can fix a clogged toilet without a plunger by using a combination of dish soap and hot water, or baking soda and vinegar. These methods help break down and lubricate the clog, allowing it to pass through the pipes. If those don't work, a toilet auger (snake) is an effective tool to use.

**How to fix a clogged toilet with poop?**

To fix a clogged toilet with poop, start by using a plunger with strong, consistent thrusts to dislodge the material. If plunging isn't effective, try pouring a generous amount of dish soap into the bowl, followed by hot (not boiling) water, and let it sit for 20-30 minutes to help break down the waste and lubricate the path. If the clog persists, a toilet auger can physically clear it.

**What causes a toilet to clog?**

Toilets commonly clog due to flushing non-flushable items like wipes, paper towels, excessive toilet paper, or small objects. Issues with your toilet's flushing mechanism, low water pressure, or blockages further down the drainpipe or sewer line can also contribute to clogs.

**When should I call a professional for a clogged toilet?**

You should call a professional plumber if your toilet remains clogged after trying multiple methods, if multiple toilets or drains in your home are backing up, or if you suspect a deeper issue in your main sewer line. Persistent clogs can indicate a more serious problem requiring specialized equipment and expertise.

**Can I use chemical drain cleaners to fix a clogged toilet?**

It's generally not recommended to use harsh chemical drain cleaners in toilets. These chemicals can be corrosive, potentially damaging your toilet's porcelain finish or PVC pipes, and can be harmful if they splash back. Natural remedies and mechanical tools are safer and often more effective alternatives for toilet clogs.

**How can I prevent my toilet from clogging in the future?**

To prevent future clogs, only flush human waste and toilet paper. Avoid flushing sanitary products, 'flushable' wipes, cotton balls, or excessive amounts of toilet paper. Consider installing a low-flow toilet if your current one struggles with waste removal, and occasionally pour a bucket of hot water down the toilet to help keep the pipes clear.
