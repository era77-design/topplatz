To fix a clogged toilet, start by assessing the water level and avoiding further flushing. Often, a simple plunger can clear the blockage. For tougher clogs, household remedies or a toilet auger may be necessary.

## What You Need

- Rubber gloves
- Flange-style plunger
- Old towels or newspapers
- Bucket
- Dish soap
- Hot water (not boiling)
- Baking soda (optional)
- White vinegar (optional)
- Toilet auger/snake (for stubborn clogs)

## Steps

### Step 1: Assess the Situation and Prepare

Before doing anything, do not flush the toilet again if the water level is high, as this could lead to an overflow. If the bowl is nearly full, quickly lift the tank lid and push down the flapper valve to stop more water from entering the bowl. Lay old towels or newspaper around the base of the toilet to protect your floor from any splashes or spills, and put on your rubber gloves.

### Step 2: Try the Plunger First

Position your flange-style plunger directly over the drain hole, ensuring it creates a tight seal. Push down slowly to remove any air from the plunger, then pull up sharply to create strong suction. Repeat this plunging action vigorously 15-20 times, maintaining the seal, then quickly pull the plunger straight up. Check if the water drains normally.

> 💡 **Tip:** A good seal between the plunger and the toilet drain is critical for effective plunging. If your plunger is old or doesn't create a good seal, consider replacing it.

### Step 3: The Dish Soap and Hot Water Method

If plunging doesn't work, pour about half a cup of liquid dish soap directly into the toilet bowl. Let it sit for 10-15 minutes to allow it to lubricate the clog. Follow this by pouring a bucket of hot (but not boiling) water into the bowl from waist height to add some force. Wait another 20-30 minutes for the clog to potentially loosen and dissolve before attempting to flush.

### Step 4: Baking Soda and Vinegar (for organic clogs)

For an eco-friendly approach, especially if the clog is organic, pour one cup of baking soda into the toilet bowl, followed by two cups of white vinegar. The chemical reaction will cause fizzing, which can help break down the blockage. Let this mixture sit for at least 30 minutes, or even overnight for very stubborn clogs, before attempting to flush.

### Step 5: Use a Toilet Auger for Stubborn Clogs

If all other methods fail, a toilet auger (also known as a closet auger or snake) is designed to physically break up or retrieve clogs. Carefully insert the auger's coiled end into the drain, pushing and rotating the handle until you feel resistance from the clog. Crank the handle to push through the obstruction or hook it and pull it out. Be gentle to avoid damaging the toilet's porcelain.

### Step 6: Confirm the Fix and Clean Up

Once you believe the clog is clear, flush the toilet. If the water drains normally, your mission is accomplished! If not, you may need to repeat a previous step or consider calling a professional plumber. Dispose of any retrieved debris, clean your tools thoroughly, and disinfect the area around the toilet.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always have a high-quality flange-style plunger readily available in your bathroom for quick clog resolution.
- Prevent future clogs by only flushing human waste and toilet paper. Avoid flushing paper towels, wet wipes, cotton balls, dental floss, or feminine hygiene products.
- Regularly clean your toilet bowl and rim to prevent mineral buildup that can contribute to clogs over time.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Never use chemical drain cleaners in a toilet, as they can damage pipes, corrode porcelain, and release harmful fumes.
- ⚠️ Do not repeatedly flush a clogged toilet; this will almost certainly cause an overflow and make a bigger mess.
- ⚠️ Avoid using boiling water to clear a clog, as the extreme heat can crack the porcelain bowl or damage PVC pipes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do you fix a clogged toilet without a plunger?**

To fix a clogged toilet without a plunger, you can try pouring a mixture of dish soap and hot (not boiling) water into the bowl, allowing it to sit and lubricate the clog. Another effective method is to use a baking soda and vinegar solution, which creates a fizzing action to break down organic matter. For mechanical removal, a toilet auger is a plunger-free option.

**What causes a toilet to clog with poop?**

A toilet can clog with poop due to several reasons, including flushing an excessive amount of waste at once, insufficient water pressure from the toilet itself, or a partial obstruction already present in the drainpipe. Older or low-flow toilets may also lack the necessary force to clear larger amounts of waste effectively.

**How to unclog a toilet when nothing works?**

If plunging, dish soap, hot water, and baking soda/vinegar remedies have all failed, your best course of action is to use a toilet auger (snake). This tool can physically dislodge or retrieve stubborn obstructions deeper in the trap or drain. If even an auger doesn't work, the clog might be further down your main drain line, indicating it's time to call a professional plumber.

**Can I use boiling water to unclog a toilet?**

No, you should never use boiling water to unclog a toilet. The extreme heat can cause the porcelain bowl to crack or melt PVC pipes, leading to significant and costly damage. Always use hot tap water instead, as it's sufficient to help dissolve clogs without posing a risk to your plumbing.

**How long should I wait after trying a clog remedy?**

For methods like dish soap and hot water, wait at least 20-30 minutes to allow the solution to work effectively. If you're using baking soda and vinegar, give it at least 30 minutes, or even several hours (or overnight for very stubborn clogs), to ensure the chemical reaction has enough time to break down the blockage.

**How can I prevent my toilet from clogging in the future?**

To prevent future clogs, strictly adhere to flushing only human waste and toilet paper. Avoid flushing any non-biodegradable items such as wet wipes, paper towels, cotton balls, dental floss, or feminine hygiene products. Regularly cleaning your toilet can also prevent mineral buildup that contributes to blockages.
