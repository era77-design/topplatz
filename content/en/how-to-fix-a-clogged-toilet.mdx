Fixing a clogged toilet often requires just a few common household items and a bit of patience. Most clogs can be resolved using a plunger, but there are also effective methods involving dish soap and hot water, or even a homemade snake, before needing to call a plumber.

## What You Need

- Flange plunger
- Rubber gloves
- Old towels or newspaper
- Dish soap
- Hot water (not boiling)
- Bucket
- Wire coat hanger (optional)
- Safety glasses (optional)

## Steps

### Step 1: Assess the Situation and Prevent Overflow

First, check the water level in the toilet bowl. If it's very high, do not flush again, as this could cause an overflow. Immediately remove the tank lid and manually close the flapper valve if water is still draining into the bowl. Lay down old towels or newspaper around the base of the toilet to catch any spills.

### Step 2: Use a Plunger Effectively

Position a flange plunger firmly over the drain hole at the bottom of the toilet bowl, ensuring you create a tight seal. Plunge with strong, consistent pushes and pulls for about 10-20 seconds. The goal is to create suction and pressure that will dislodge or break apart the clog. After several plunges, quickly pull the plunger up to break the seal and check if the water drains.

### Step 3: Try the Dish Soap and Hot Water Method

If plunging doesn't work, pour about half a cup of liquid dish soap into the toilet bowl. Follow this with a gallon of hot (but not boiling) water from a bucket, pouring it from waist height to add some force. Let this mixture sit for 20-30 minutes; the soap acts as a lubricant, and the hot water helps to soften the clog, allowing it to slide down the drain.

### Step 4: Create a DIY Toilet Snake (if necessary)

For more stubborn clogs, you can fashion a makeshift toilet snake. Unbend a wire coat hanger and wrap a rag around one end to prevent scratching the porcelain. Gently push the wrapped end into the drain, twisting and pushing to break up or pull out the obstruction. Be careful not to use excessive force to avoid damaging the pipes.

### Step 5: Repeat or Consider Professional Help

If the clog persists after trying these methods, you can repeat the plunger or dish soap steps. For extremely stubborn clogs, you might consider a chemical drain cleaner, but use it strictly according to package directions and with caution. If all DIY efforts fail or if multiple drains in your home are clogged, it's best to call a professional plumber.

### Step 6: Clean Up and Test

Once the clog is cleared and the water drains freely, flush the toilet once or twice to ensure full functionality. Thoroughly clean all tools used, such as the plunger and bucket, and dispose of soiled towels or newspaper. Wash your hands thoroughly, especially if you didn't use gloves, to maintain hygiene.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always use a flange plunger for toilets; its design creates a better seal than a standard cup plunger.
- Never use boiling water, as the extreme heat can crack the porcelain bowl of your toilet.
- For tough clogs, letting the dish soap and hot water mixture sit overnight can sometimes work wonders.
- Wear rubber gloves throughout the unclogging process to maintain hygiene and protect your hands.
- If you need to turn off the water supply to the toilet, locate the small valve on the wall or floor behind the toilet and turn it clockwise.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Do not mix different chemical drain cleaners, as this can create dangerous fumes or reactions.
- ⚠️ Avoid over-flushing a clogged toilet, as this is the primary cause of overflows.
- ⚠️ If water is overflowing, immediately turn off the water supply valve behind the toilet to prevent water damage.
- ⚠️ Be cautious when using a wire coat hanger to avoid scratching the toilet bowl or damaging pipes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do you fix a clogged toilet without a plunger?**

You can often fix a clogged toilet without a plunger by pouring about half a cup of dish soap into the bowl, followed by a gallon of hot (not boiling) water. Let it sit for 20-30 minutes to allow the soap to lubricate the clog, helping it slide down the drain.

**How do you fix a clogged toilet with poop?**

To fix a clogged toilet with poop, try using a plunger first with strong, consistent pushes to dislodge it. If a plunger isn't available or effective, a mixture of dish soap and hot water can help break down the organic matter and lubricate the pipes for easier flushing.

**What can I pour down a clogged toilet to unclog it?**

You can pour a generous amount of dish soap (about half a cup) followed by a gallon of hot (not boiling) water down a clogged toilet. The soap acts as a lubricant, and the hot water helps to break down the clog, making it easier to flush away.

**How long does it take for a clogged toilet to unclog itself?**

A clogged toilet rarely unclogs itself completely, especially if the clog is solid. However, if it's a minor organic clog, letting it sit for an hour or two with dish soap and hot water can sometimes dissolve or loosen it enough to clear.

**When should I call a plumber for a clogged toilet?**

You should call a plumber if you've tried all DIY methods (plunger, dish soap, homemade snake) and the toilet remains clogged, or if multiple toilets and drains in your home are backed up, indicating a deeper main line issue. Persistent clogs can signify a more serious plumbing problem.

**Can I use a drain snake in a toilet?**

Yes, you can use a drain snake specifically designed for toilets (a closet auger) or a homemade snake from a wire coat hanger to clear clogs. Be gentle to avoid scratching the porcelain, and ensure the end is covered with a rag if using a DIY tool.
