To delete a post on Facebook, locate the specific post, click the three-dot menu icon, and select 'Move to trash' or 'Delete post'. You will then be prompted to confirm your action. This process removes the post from your timeline or the page.

## What You Need

- A Facebook account
- An internet-connected device (computer, smartphone, or tablet)

## Steps

### Step 1: Locate the Post You Want to Delete

First, navigate to your Facebook profile, a specific page, or a group where the post is located. Scroll through your timeline or feed until you find the exact post you wish to remove. If it's an older post, you might find it faster by going to your Activity Log.

> 💡 **Tip:** For personal profiles, accessing your Activity Log (Settings & Privacy > Activity Log > Your Posts) can make finding older posts much easier.

### Step 2: Access the Post's Options Menu

Once you've found the post, look for the three-dot menu icon (...). This icon is typically located in the top-right corner of the post box. Click or tap on this icon to reveal a dropdown menu with various options related to the post.

> 💡 **Tip:** The exact placement of the three-dot menu might slightly vary between desktop and mobile versions of Facebook, but it's always in the upper right of the post.

### Step 3: Choose 'Move to trash' or 'Delete Post'

From the dropdown menu, select the option that says 'Move to trash' (for personal profile posts) or 'Delete Post' (often for posts on pages or in groups). The terminology can differ based on where the post was made. 'Move to trash' sends the post to a temporary holding area for 30 days.

> 💡 **Tip:** For personal profiles, 'Move to trash' allows for recovery within 30 days, offering a safety net. For pages/groups, 'Delete Post' is often immediate and permanent.

### Step 4: Confirm Your Deletion

After selecting the deletion option, Facebook will usually display a confirmation prompt. This prompt asks if you are sure you want to delete the post and explains the implications. Read the message carefully and then click or tap 'Delete' or 'Move to trash' again to finalize the action.

> 💡 **Tip:** Always read the confirmation message carefully to understand if the deletion is temporary or permanent before proceeding.

### Step 5: Understand the Trash Folder (Personal Profiles Only)

If you moved a personal profile post to trash, it won't be immediately permanently deleted. It will reside in your 'Trash' folder within your Activity Log for 30 days. During this period, you have the option to restore the post if you change your mind. After 30 days, it will be permanently removed.

> 💡 **Tip:** You can access your Trash folder via your Activity Log to review or restore posts within the 30-day window.

### Step 6: Permanently Delete from Trash (Optional)

If you wish to permanently delete a personal profile post before the 30-day period expires, you can do so. Go to your Activity Log, then select 'Trash' from the left-hand menu. Find the post you want to permanently remove, select it, and then choose the 'Delete' option. This action is irreversible.

> 💡 **Tip:** Once a post is permanently deleted from trash, there is no way to recover it, so be absolutely certain before taking this step.


## Tips & Tricks

- Utilize the Activity Log to efficiently find and manage older posts on your personal profile.
- Be aware that posts moved to trash on personal profiles are recoverable for 30 days; after that, they are permanently deleted.
- Deleting a post removes it entirely from Facebook, unlike 'hiding' it, which only removes it from your view.
- For business pages or group posts, deletion is often instant and permanent, without a trash recovery period.
- Always double-check the post you are deleting to avoid accidentally removing the wrong content.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Deleting a post is a permanent action after the 30-day trash period (for personal profiles) or immediately (for pages/groups). There is no way to recover it once permanently removed.
- ⚠️ Deleting a post will also remove all associated comments, likes, and shares. Any shares of your post might also become inaccessible.
- ⚠️ Ensure you are logged into the correct Facebook account or page before attempting to delete a post to prevent accidental deletions on the wrong profile.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I recover a deleted Facebook post?**

Yes, if it's from your personal profile and was moved to the 'Trash' folder within the last 30 days. You can restore it from your Activity Log. Posts deleted from pages or groups are typically permanently removed immediately.

**What's the difference between 'Move to trash' and 'Delete Post'?**

'Move to trash' is typically for personal profile posts, sending them to a trash folder for 30 days before permanent deletion. 'Delete Post' is often used for pages or groups, usually resulting in immediate and permanent removal.

**Does deleting a post remove comments and shares?**

Yes, deleting the original post will remove all associated comments, likes, and shares. If someone shared your post, their shared version might also disappear or become inaccessible once the original is gone.

**Can I delete someone else's post from my timeline?**

You cannot directly delete someone else's post from their profile or a group. However, you can hide their post from your own timeline, or report it to Facebook if it violates Community Standards.

**How do I delete multiple Facebook posts at once?**

You can delete multiple posts by going to your Activity Log (for personal profiles), then 'Your Posts'. Select the posts you want to remove and choose the 'Move to trash' option, allowing for bulk deletion.

**What happens if I delete a post from a Facebook Group?**

Deleting a post from a Facebook Group usually results in its immediate and permanent removal from the group. It will no longer be visible to any group members or administrators.

**Is deleting a post the same as hiding it?**

No, deleting a post permanently removes it from Facebook, making it invisible to everyone. Hiding a post only removes it from your own timeline's view, but the post still exists and is visible to others who can access it.
