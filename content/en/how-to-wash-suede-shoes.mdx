To safely clean suede shoes, first brush off loose dirt. Then, use a specialized suede cleaner or a diluted white vinegar solution applied sparingly with a soft cloth. Always test on an inconspicuous area and allow shoes to air dry completely away from direct heat.

## What You Need

- Suede brush
- Clean microfiber cloths
- Suede eraser or pencil eraser
- White vinegar
- Water
- Shoe trees or crumpled paper towels (plain, undyed)
- Suede protector spray (optional)

## Steps

### Step 1: Remove Loose Dirt

Use a suede brush or a soft-bristled brush to gently remove any loose dirt, dust, or mud from the surface of your shoes. Brush in one direction to lift the nap and ensure thorough cleaning. For dried mud, let it completely dry before brushing, then gently break it off.

> 💡 **Tip:** For dried mud, let it completely dry before brushing, then gently break it off.

### Step 2: Tackle Stains with an Eraser

For scuffs or stubborn dry marks, gently rub a suede eraser (or a clean pencil eraser) over the affected area. Use short, firm strokes to lift the stain without pressing too hard, which could damage the suede fibers. This method is effective for localized grime.

> 💡 **Tip:** Always use a clean eraser to avoid transferring new marks to your shoes.

### Step 3: Prepare Cleaning Solution (or Suede Cleaner)

Mix equal parts white vinegar and water in a small bowl, or prepare your commercial suede cleaner according to its instructions. White vinegar is a mild acid that helps lift dirt and can also act as a deodorizer for your shoes. Ensure your chosen method is ready before applying.

> 💡 **Tip:** Always test your chosen cleaning method on a small, hidden area of the shoe first to ensure no discoloration occurs.

### Step 4: Gently Clean the Suede

Dampen a clean microfiber cloth with your cleaning solution (or apply suede cleaner directly to the cloth). Gently wipe the stained or soiled areas of the shoe in small, circular motions, being careful not to saturate the suede. Work on one small section at a time, moving quickly to prevent watermarks from setting in.

> 💡 **Tip:** Less is more when applying moisture to suede; avoid over-wetting the material.

### Step 5: Wipe Away Residue

Use a separate, clean cloth dampened *only* with plain water to gently wipe away any cleaning solution residue from the suede surface. This step is crucial to prevent streaks and ensures a clean finish on the delicate material. Repeat if necessary with a fresh damp cloth.

> 💡 **Tip:** Use distilled water for this step to avoid mineral deposits.

### Step 6: Blot and Air Dry

Blot the entire shoe with a dry, clean towel to absorb as much moisture as possible. Stuff the shoes with shoe trees or crumpled, undyed paper towels to help them maintain their shape as they dry. Allow them to air dry completely in a well-ventilated area, away from direct sunlight or heat sources, which can cause damage.

> 💡 **Tip:** Replace damp paper towels with dry ones periodically to speed up drying.

### Step 7: Restore the Nap

Once the shoes are completely dry (this can take several hours, sometimes overnight), use your suede brush to gently brush the nap back into its original direction. This will restore the soft, velvety texture and uniform appearance of the suede, making your shoes look refreshed and new.

> 💡 **Tip:** Brush lightly in alternating directions to evenly raise the nap.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always test any cleaning solution on an inconspicuous area first to prevent damage or discoloration.
- Never oversaturate suede with water or cleaning solutions; a little goes a long way.
- Stuff your shoes with plain paper towels or shoe trees while drying to help them maintain their shape.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Do not machine wash or tumble dry suede shoes, as this will severely damage the material and shape.
- ⚠️ Avoid using harsh detergents, bleach, or colored cloths, which can stain or damage suede.
- ⚠️ Never dry suede shoes with direct heat sources like hair dryers, radiators, or direct sunlight, as this can cause cracking and shrinking.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I put suede shoes in the washing machine?**

No, absolutely not. Machine washing will saturate the suede, causing it to lose its texture, shrink, and potentially damage the shoe's structure. Always clean suede by hand using specialized methods.

**How do I remove water stains from suede?**

For water stains, lightly dampen the entire affected area with a spray bottle of clean water, then blot with a clean towel. This helps to 'even out' the water mark. Allow it to air dry completely, then brush the nap.

**What if my suede shoes get really wet?**

If your suede shoes get very wet, blot them immediately with a clean towel to absorb excess moisture. Stuff them with crumpled paper towels to maintain their shape and let them air dry completely away from direct heat, which can cause cracking.

**Can I use regular soap and water on suede?**

It's generally not recommended to use regular soap on suede as it can leave residues or strip the natural oils, leading to discoloration or stiffness. Opt for specialized suede cleaners or a diluted white vinegar solution for best results.

**How often should I clean my suede shoes?**

The frequency depends on how often you wear them and the conditions. It's best to clean them as needed, addressing stains promptly to prevent them from setting. Regular light brushing helps maintain their appearance between deeper cleans.

**What's the best way to protect suede shoes from future stains?**

After cleaning and drying, apply a high-quality suede protector spray. This creates an invisible barrier against water and stains, making future cleaning easier and prolonging the life of your shoes. Reapply periodically according to product instructions.

**Can I clean oil or grease stains from suede?**

For oil or grease stains, sprinkle cornstarch or talcum powder directly onto the stain and let it sit for several hours or overnight to absorb the oil. Brush off the powder, then gently rub with a suede eraser to lift any remaining residue.
