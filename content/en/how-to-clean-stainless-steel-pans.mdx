To clean stainless steel pans, start with warm soapy water for daily grime. For stubborn food or stains, use a paste of baking soda and water or a vinegar solution to gently lift residue and restore their natural luster.

## What You Need

- Dish soap
- Warm water
- Soft sponge or cloth
- Baking soda
- White vinegar
- Non-abrasive scrubber (optional)
- Olive oil or mineral oil
- Paper towel or clean cloth

## Steps

### Step 1: Initial Wash for Everyday Grime

Wash the pan with warm water and a few drops of dish soap using a soft sponge. Gently scrub away any loose food particles and grease that are easily removed. Rinse thoroughly under running water.

### Step 2: Soak for Stuck-On Food

If food is stuck, fill the pan with warm water and a squirt of dish soap, then let it soak for 15-30 minutes. This softens the residue, making it much easier to wipe away with a sponge or soft cloth without excessive scrubbing.

### Step 3: Tackle Burnt-On Food with Baking Soda

For stubborn burnt food, sprinkle the bottom of the pan with baking soda and add a small amount of water to create a paste. Let it sit for 10-15 minutes, allowing the baking soda to work on the residue. Gently scrub with a non-abrasive scrubber or sponge until the burnt bits lift.

> 💡 **Tip:** For very tough burns, you can add a bit of white vinegar to the baking soda paste to create a fizzing action that helps lift residue.

### Step 4: Remove Discoloration and Rainbow Stains

To remove heat discoloration (rainbow stains) or water spots, pour a small amount of white vinegar into the pan and swirl it around to coat the affected areas. Let it sit for a minute or two, allowing the acidity to dissolve the discoloration. Then, rinse the pan thoroughly and wash it again with soap and water.

### Step 5: Restore Shine with Oil

Once the pan is completely clean and dry, put a tiny drop of olive oil or mineral oil on a paper towel or clean, soft cloth. Rub the interior and exterior surfaces of the pan in a circular motion. This simple step helps to polish the stainless steel and restore its natural luster and shine.

### Step 6: Rinse and Dry Thoroughly

After completing any cleaning steps, rinse the pan completely to ensure all cleaning agents are removed. Dry it immediately with a clean cloth or paper towel. Prompt drying prevents water spots and helps to maintain the pan's pristine appearance.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always let your stainless steel pans cool completely before washing them to prevent warping.
- Avoid using steel wool or harsh abrasive scrubbers, as these can scratch the surface of your pan.
- For maximum shine, always dry your pans immediately after washing to prevent water spots.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Do not use chlorine bleach on stainless steel, as it can cause pitting and corrosion.
- ⚠️ Avoid highly abrasive cleaners or scouring pads that can permanently scratch the pan's surface.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I use a dishwasher to clean stainless steel pans?**

While most stainless steel pans are dishwasher safe, handwashing is often recommended to preserve their finish and prevent potential dulling or water spots over time. Always check the manufacturer's care instructions for specific guidance on your pan.

**How do I prevent food from sticking to my stainless steel pan?**

Preheat your pan properly before adding oil, and then heat the oil until it shimmers but isn't smoking. This creates a natural non-stick barrier. Also, ensure your food is at room temperature before cooking, as cold food can cause sticking.

**What causes the rainbow-colored stains on my stainless steel pan?**

These 'rainbow' or 'heat tint' stains are caused by overheating the pan or mineral deposits from water reacting with the chromium in the stainless steel. They are harmless and can be easily removed with white vinegar.

**Is it safe to use baking soda and vinegar together on stainless steel?**

Yes, baking soda and vinegar are safe and effective for cleaning stainless steel. The fizzing reaction created when they combine helps to lift stubborn grime and burnt-on food without damaging the pan's surface.

**How often should I deep clean my stainless steel pans?**

For everyday use, a regular wash with soap and water is sufficient. Deep cleaning with baking soda or vinegar can be done as needed for burnt food, discoloration, or to restore shine, typically every few weeks or months depending on usage.

**Can I use lemon juice instead of vinegar for cleaning?**

Yes, lemon juice is also acidic and can be used as an effective alternative to white vinegar for removing discoloration and water spots from stainless steel pans. Apply it similarly, let it sit briefly, then rinse and wash.

**My pan has scratches, can I remove them?**

Minor surface scratches on stainless steel pans are often difficult to completely remove at home without specialized tools or professional polishing. It's best to prevent them by using soft sponges and avoiding abrasive cleaners or utensils during cooking and cleaning.
