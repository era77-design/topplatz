To clean your washing machine, run an empty hot water cycle with white vinegar or a specialized cleaner to remove buildup. Afterwards, wipe down the drum, dispenser, and seals, and clean the drain filter to prevent odors and improve performance.

## What You Need

- White vinegar
- Baking soda (optional)
- Microfiber cloths
- Old toothbrush or small brush
- Spray bottle (optional)
- All-purpose cleaner (for exterior)
- Commercial washing machine cleaner (optional)

## Steps

### Step 1: Prepare Your Machine

Ensure the washing machine is completely empty of clothes or any other items. Double-check pockets if you recently removed laundry, as small items can hinder the cleaning process and potentially damage the machine.

> 💡 **Tip:** Always empty the machine completely before starting any cleaning cycles.

### Step 2: Clean the Detergent Dispenser

Pull out the detergent and fabric softener dispenser drawer, if removable. Soak it in warm soapy water to loosen any hardened detergent, mold, or mildew. Use an old toothbrush or small brush to scrub away all residue, then rinse thoroughly and return it to its place.

### Step 3: Run a Cleaning Cycle (Vinegar/Baking Soda)

For a natural and effective clean, pour two cups of white vinegar directly into the detergent dispenser or drum. Run the hottest, longest cycle available on your machine, such as 'Tub Clean' or 'Sanitize'. If using baking soda, sprinkle half a cup into the drum after the vinegar cycle has completed, then run another hot rinse cycle to flush out any remaining residue.

### Step 4: Alternatively: Use a Commercial Cleaner

If you prefer a commercial product, follow the specific instructions on the packaging for your chosen washing machine cleaner. Typically, this involves adding the cleaner to the dispenser or directly into the drum and running a hot, empty cycle as directed by the manufacturer.

### Step 5: Wipe Down the Interior and Exterior

After the cleaning cycle(s) finish, use a clean microfiber cloth dampened with a vinegar-water solution (50/50) or an all-purpose cleaner. Thoroughly wipe down the inside of the drum and pay special attention to the rubber gasket around the door, especially on front-loading machines, where mold and mildew often accumulate. Don't forget to clean the machine's exterior as well.

### Step 6: Clean the Drain Filter (Front-Loaders)

For front-loading machines, locate the drain pump filter, usually behind a small panel at the bottom front. Place a shallow pan or towel underneath to catch any residual water. Carefully unscrew the cap and remove any lint, debris, or small items that may have collected. Clean the filter under running water and securely replace it.

### Step 7: Leave the Door Ajar

To prevent future mildew growth and musty odors, always leave the washing machine door and the detergent dispenser drawer slightly ajar after each use and especially after cleaning. This allows for proper air circulation, ensuring the interior dries completely.


## Tips & Tricks

- Always leave the washer door and detergent drawer slightly open after each use to allow air circulation and prevent mildew.
- Clean your washing machine monthly, or more frequently if you do a lot of laundry or notice odors.
- Use high-efficiency (HE) detergent if you have an HE machine, and always use the recommended amount to prevent excessive suds and residue buildup.

## Warnings

- ⚠️ Do not mix bleach and vinegar, as this creates dangerous chlorine gas.
- ⚠️ Always unplug the washing machine before attempting to clean the drain pump filter to prevent accidental starting or electrical shock.
- ⚠️ Consult your washing machine's user manual for specific cleaning instructions or warnings related to your model.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How often should I clean my washing machine?**

It's recommended to clean your washing machine monthly, especially if you do frequent loads or notice any musty odors. Regular cleaning prevents buildup and maintains efficiency.

**Can I use bleach to clean my washing machine?**

Yes, bleach can be used, but never mix it with vinegar or other cleaners, as this can create harmful fumes. Run a hot cycle with 1/2 cup of bleach, then follow with a plain hot rinse cycle to remove residue.

**Why does my washing machine smell bad?**

A bad smell is often caused by mold, mildew, or detergent residue accumulating in the drum, dispenser, or rubber gasket. Regular cleaning, proper ventilation, and using the correct amount of detergent can prevent this.

**Is it safe to use vinegar in my washing machine?**

Yes, white vinegar is safe and highly effective for cleaning most washing machines. It helps break down mineral deposits, soap scum, and can kill mold and mildew without harsh chemicals.

**What is the rubber gasket on my front-loader for?**

The rubber gasket, or boot, on a front-loader creates a watertight seal during cycles. It's a common spot for water, lint, and detergent residue to collect, making it prone to mold and mildew if not regularly wiped clean.

**My clothes still smell after washing. What should I do?**

If clothes still smell after washing, your washing machine likely needs a thorough deep clean. Ensure you clean the dispenser, drum, and especially the drain filter and rubber gasket, as these are common sources of odor buildup.

**Do I need a special washing machine cleaner?**

While commercial washing machine cleaners are effective, common household items like white vinegar and baking soda work just as well for most cleaning tasks. Choose what you prefer or have readily available.
